# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 084
**Title:** The National Meeting Efficiency and Biscuit Allocation Bill
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE INCIDENT ROOM, 07:00. A dashboard blinks: **"CROSS-GOVERNMENT PRODUCTIVITY — FLICKERING"**.)

Morning briefing card:

**CITIZEN SERVICE WEEK — DAY 16**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Meeting Efficiency and Biscuit Allocation Bill"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is Treasury asking whether shortbread counts as capital expenditure?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because departments logged 312,000 meeting-hours last quarter and biscuit costs have become the only measurable output.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** One directorate submitted "digestive utilisation" as a performance indicator.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So we can't fix procurement, but we can definitely track crumbs.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE AGENDA ABOUT AGENDAS

08:14. Emergency call with permanent secretaries, operations leads, and digital collaboration teams.

Slide title: **"MEETING LOAD AS A SERVICE RISK"**

**PM:** Are we saying meetings are now infrastructure?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** At current volume, yes. They're consuming delivery capacity faster than we can recruit it.

**HEAD OF OPERATIONS:** Average staff member attends 6.3 meetings daily, with 2.1 hours of duplicated updates.

**DATA ANALYST:** Teams with pre-read notes and strict decision logs complete projects 18% faster.

Four systemic issues confirmed:

1. Meetings with no decision owner
2. Recurring calls that survive long after their purpose
3. Status updates repeated across multiple forums
4. Hospitality ordering detached from attendance reality

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We've created a circular economy where information goes in circles.

## ACT TWO — THE BILL

10:31. Rapid drafting session with policy, legal, HR, and facilities.

Slide title: **"LESS TALK, MORE OUTCOME"**

**POLICY LEAD:** Introduce default 25-minute meetings, with 50 minutes only by justification.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Mandatory agenda and decision field before calendar invite can be sent.

**FACILITIES MANAGER:** Refreshment orders tied to confirmed attendees 12 hours in advance.

Three immediate controls approved:

- **Agenda Gate:** no agenda, no room, no video link
- **Decision Log Standard:** each meeting ends with owner, action, deadline
- **Biscuit Ratio Protocol:** one plate per six confirmed attendees

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** At last, reform that can be measured in both minutes and hobnobs.

## ACT THREE — PILOT DAY

12:06. Pilot launches in six departments and two central agencies.

**INCIDENT COORDINATOR:** Tracking meeting count, decision completion rate, and snack waste.

12:29. Agenda Gate enforcement starts.

**PROGRAMME MANAGER:** Recurring "touch-base" calls dropped by 27% in the first hour.

13:11. Decision Log template adopted by all pilot teams.

**DELIVERY LEAD:** Fewer follow-up meetings needed because actions are actually named.

14:34. Biscuit Ratio Protocol activates.

**FACILITIES MANAGER:** Catering costs down immediately; no signs of civil unrest.

Follow-up actions agreed:

- Weekly publication of "meeting-to-decision" ratios
- Monthly cleanup of recurring invites older than 90 days
- Quarterly training on concise briefing notes
- Public-facing note on how internal efficiency supports frontline response times

**PM:** If it doesn't produce a decision, it's a podcast, not a meeting.

## ACT FOUR — SELLING ADMINISTRATIVE SOBRIETY

16:47. Comms drafts line: **"GOVERNMENT CUTS MEETING WASTE TO SPEED DELIVERY"**

Public points cleared:

- Shorter meetings increase time for actual service work
- Clear ownership improves accountability
- Reduced duplication lowers operational drag
- Smarter facilities planning cuts avoidable spend
- Efficiency gains are reinvested into frontline priorities

By 18:12 (pilot metrics):

- Total meeting hours down 31%
- Duplicate status meetings down 44%
- Decision completion within deadline up 22%
- Catering waste down 39%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** It's practical, boring, and effective.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Perfect. Let's keep all three.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:25. Press gather by the briefing room doors.

**REPORTER:** Minister, are you really legislating biscuits?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We're legislating wasted time. Biscuits are collateral governance.

**REPORTER:** Is this symbolic rather than substantive?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** The pilot saved hours and money in one afternoon. That's substantive enough for me.

**REPORTER:** What happens next?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Expansion, measurement, adjustment. Preferably in that order.

## TAG

20:11. New default invite footer appears government-wide: **"STATE THE DECISION OR CANCEL THE MEETING."**
