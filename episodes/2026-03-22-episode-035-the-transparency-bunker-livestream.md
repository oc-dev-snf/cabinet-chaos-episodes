# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 035
**Title:** The Transparency Bunker Livestream
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

A government livestream titled **"TOTAL TRANSPARENCY BRIEFING"** begins with a holding slide, elevator jazz, and a spinning padlock icon.

MALCOLM: Why is there a padlock on a transparency stream?

GLENN: They said it means we're secure.

MALCOLM: Secure from what? Information?

The chat feed floods with: *"No audio"*, *"Is this a screensaver?"*, and one user called *PolicyGoblin94* typing *"statecraft but make it buffering."*

---

## ACT ONE

### 1) The idea

The PM wants to "rebuild trust" by showing how decisions are made in real time. The solution from the Strategy Unit: a **Transparency Bunker** fitted with cameras, microphones, and a giant red button labelled **"CLARITY MODE"**.

NICOLA: The public can watch us work.

MALCOLM: They don't want to watch you work. They barely want to watch Netflix pick a thumbnail.

A junior adviser explains there are seven camera angles:
- Desk Cam
- Corridor Cam
- Whiteboard Cam
- Whiteboard Cam (for legal)
- Plant Cam
- "Vibes" Cam
- Emergency "No Comment" Cam

### 2) The first briefing

The minister enters, waves, and reads from a teleprompter:

MINISTER: "Good afternoon. Today we will be radically open, selectively precise, and contextually accountable."

GLENN: That's not a sentence, that's a hostage note written by consultants.

The comments section instantly clips the line and turns it into a remix within nine minutes.

---

## ACT TWO

### 1) Transparency meets reality

A civil servant accidentally shares the wrong desktop to the stream:
- Draft crisis messaging document titled **"FINAL_FINAL_USE_THIS_ONE_v9_REAL"**
- A shopping list: *"oat milk, batteries, constitutional reform"*
- A sticky note: *"Do not let Malcolm near microphone"*

MALCOLM: That's not a policy leak. That's a cry for help in Arial.

### 2) The "open Q&A"

Questions are selected from the livestream chat by an AI moderation tool trained to remove abuse and irrelevant content. It removes everything except:

> "What exactly do you mean by transparency?"

Asked 412 times by 412 people.

NICOLA: Just answer it.

MINISTER: Transparency is when the public can see that government is being transparent.

MALCOLM: Brilliant. If we define words by using the same word three times, by Christmas we'll solve inflation with "money is moneying." 

### 3) Panic choreography

The PM's office demands stronger optics. They push **CLARITY MODE**. Every camera turns on at once, including Plant Cam, which now has lower latency than the main feed and becomes the nation's preferred source of government updates.

Headlines appear:
- *"Fern becomes trusted source at No. 10"*
- *"Houseplant posts steadier messaging than ministers"*
- *"Opposition promises shadow succulent"*

---

## ACT THREE

### 1) Rebrand sprint

Comms proposes renaming "Transparency Bunker" to:
1. Public Insight Hub
2. Democratic Glass Box
3. Open Government Experience Centre
4. The Situation Aquarium

MALCOLM: Number four is the only honest one.

### 2) The metrics deck

A performance report claims success:
- **Engagement:** up 900%
- **Trust:** "directionally hopeful"
- **Public understanding:** "complex"
- **Clip of minister blinking at error message:** 14.2 million views

GLENN: We're not governing. We're speedrunning embarrassment.

---

## CLIMAX

During a "live policy workshop," someone asks why the government announced three contradictory transport plans in one week.

Long silence.

Then Plant Cam audio picks up Malcolm whispering:

MALCOLM: Because the only joined-up thing in this building is the Wi-Fi password and even that's temporary.

The clip trends globally under **#BunkerTruth**.

The PM's office declares the livestream "a landmark exercise in democratic intimacy" and quietly ends it due to "scheduled maintenance."

Maintenance notice ETA: **indefinite**.

---

## TAG

Next morning, staff discover the Plant Cam still streaming.

For six uninterrupted hours it broadcasts:
- an empty chair,
- a flickering light,
- and someone in the corridor repeatedly saying, "Who's in charge of turning this off?"

Comments unanimously agree: *"Most coherent briefing all year."*
