# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 108
**Title:** The Courtesy Loop Consultation
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

A conference room door has three signs taped to it:
- **CONSULTATION IN PROGRESS**
- **CONSULTATION ABOUT CONSULTATION IN PROGRESS**
- **PLEASE KNOCK QUIETLY, THEN EMAIL WHY YOU KNOCKED**

Inside, an aide asks whether the meeting has started.

Another aide says, “We’re currently agreeing how to agree that it has started.”

Cut to opening titles.

## ACT ONE

A straightforward reform package is ready for announcement, but leadership wants to show broad listening first.

Officials launch the **Courtesy Loop Consultation**.

Official aim: gather views from all stakeholders before final wording.

Unspoken aim: ensure nobody can say they were not invited to comment on a sentence they will later criticise.

The process has three stages:
- Initial outreach: ask for feedback
- Follow-up outreach: ask if feedback is still feedback
- Assurance outreach: ask whether people felt asked enough

A fourth stage appears by noon:
- Retrospective outreach: ask whether the outreach process itself required outreach

Policy team calls this “participation density.”

## ACT TWO

A draft statement is circulated:

“We will proceed with practical reforms informed by active engagement.”

First round responses request clarity.

Second round responses request less clarity, “to preserve flexibility.”

Third round responses request a sentence acknowledging the first two rounds.

By mid-afternoon, the statement reads:

“We will proceed practically, flexibly, and with explicit recognition of previous requests for practical flexibility.”

Comms asks who approved this wording.

Everyone points politely at everyone else.

## ACT THREE

A minister requests a simple progress update: done or not done.

The team produces a tracker with status options:
- complete
- near-complete
- complete in principle
- complete pending completion

The minister circles “complete pending completion” and asks, “Is that done?”

An adviser replies, “It is done from a process perspective and pending from a timeline perspective.”

The minister asks for one owner of the final decision.

Officials propose a rotating owner model called **Shared Final Ownership Window**.

The rotation period is forty-five minutes.

## CLIMAX

At the evening briefing, the spokesperson says:

“We have completed a comprehensive consultation loop and are moving to delivery.”

A reporter asks when delivery begins.

The spokesperson replies, “Immediately after final courtesy confirmations are received.”

A second reporter asks who must confirm.

The spokesperson checks notes:

“Those who were consulted, and those who may feel they should have been consulted about the consultation.”

A third reporter asks if there is a deadline.

The spokesperson says, “Yes, the deadline is as soon as reasonably unanimous.”

## TAG

At 9:02 p.m., staff receive an email:

**Subject:** Final Courtesy Loop Closure (Interim Ongoing Final)

- Round four comments are now optional mandatory
- Silence will be treated as constructive provisional agreement
- Any explicit agreement must be recirculated for fairness
- Tomorrow’s first meeting will confirm whether tonight was the final loop

Footer note:

“Please do not call this the final version in public-facing corridors.”

**END**
