# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 062
**Title:** The Consultation Carousel
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:02. A meeting room whiteboard reads **"LISTENING EXERCISE — FINAL DRAFT v12"**.)

Emergency banner:

**PUBLIC ENGAGEMENT ACCELERATOR — LIVE**  
**Scenario:** *"The Consultation Carousel"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why are there four launch times for the same consultation?

**POLICY LEAD:** We wanted flexibility.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We wanted prime-time coverage.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** We wanted the website to survive.

**AIDE:** Citizens wanted one clear link.

Silence.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We have successfully gathered every view except the public's.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — LISTENING, LOUDLY

War room, 08:10. Project board title: **ENGAGE EARLY, ENGAGE OFTEN, ENGAGE AGAIN**

**PM:** I want this consultation to feel open, practical, and calm.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Also quick.

**TREASURY REP:** Also cheap.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Also compliant.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So: effortless perfection by lunchtime.

By 09:35, the launch plan has multiplied:

1. Three separate questionnaires ask nearly identical questions in different wording
2. A stakeholder webinar is scheduled before invitations are sent
3. Regional offices receive two toolkits labeled "Use this one" and "Actually use this one"
4. A draft press line promises "the broadest listening exercise in modern times"
5. An internal note asks whether comments in ALL CAPS count as "high-priority sentiment"

**PM:** How many channels are we using?

**DIGITAL LEAD:** All of them.

**PM:** Do they point to one place?

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Not yet, but they all point somewhere.

## ACT TWO — THE QUESTION PROBLEM

Policy room, 11:04. Sticky note on monitor: **"What are we actually asking?"**

**POLICY LEAD:** Current top question reads: "How should we optimize delivery pathways for strategic service modernization?"

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** That's not a question. That's a tax audit with punctuation.

They workshop alternatives:

**DRAFT V1:** "How can services work better for you, day to day?"

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Clear, human, dangerous.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Dangerous?

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** People might answer honestly.

**DRAFT V2:** "Which changes would most improve your experience this year?"

**PM:** Keep V2.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Add examples. If people need a glossary, we've already lost.

At 12:26, a new obstacle arrives: the impact panel requests a pre-consultation consultation.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Are we consulting on whether to consult?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** In fairness, at least it's on brand.

## ACT THREE — THE PUBLIC TEST

Community hall, 14:18. Pilot session with volunteers.

**FACILITATOR:** Please complete the form and tell us what was confusing.

Eight minutes later, findings:

- Most participants skip Q3 because it sounds like internal jargon
- The optional "context" box receives the longest, clearest feedback
- People ask for a simple timeline and one accountable contact
- Nobody objects to change; everyone objects to uncertainty

**PM (quietly):** That's useful.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Yes. Real people are notoriously data-rich.

Phone buzzes with media alert:

**"Government launches consultation with 17-page guidance annex."**

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** That's not wrong.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Then we fix what is true, not what is said.

## ACT FOUR — ONE DOOR IN, ONE REPORT OUT

Reset meeting, 16:07. New heading: **SIMPLE IN, TRANSPARENT OUT**

Agreed operating model:

- One consultation page, one form, one deadline, one contact point
- Plain-language questions tested with external volunteers before launch
- Weekly public summary: what we heard, what changes, what won't change (and why)
- Department responses signed by named officials, not anonymous "teams"
- Publish full methodology note to prevent cherry-picking accusations

By 18:40:

- Duplicate forms removed
- Webinar postponed until after invitations are sent
- Guidance annex cut from 17 pages to 4
- Press line revised from "broadest ever" to "clear, open, and accountable"

**PM:** Is this less ambitious?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. It's ambitious enough to survive contact with reality.

## CLIMAX — THE BRIEFING

Press room, 19:26.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**  
Today we simplified how this consultation works.

The public should not need specialist language to tell government what is and isn't working. From tonight, there is one route to respond, clear questions, and regular published summaries.

You'll be able to track what feedback changes policy, and where constraints mean it cannot.

**REPORTER:** Is this a response to criticism of the rollout?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It is a response to evidence from users this afternoon.

**REPORTER:** Were mistakes made this morning?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We started with complexity. We are ending the day with clarity.

## TAG

21:53. Back office. A lone printer ejects the retired 17-page annex.

A junior official places it in a folder labeled:

**"LESSONS LEARNED — DO NOT RE-LAUNCH."**

Peter-like minister walks past, sees the new consultation page open on a screen:

**"Tell us what would improve services for you this year."**

No jargon. No branching maze. Just a text box and a deadline.

**AIDE:** Think it'll hold?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If it's simple enough to explain once, it might even last until Friday.

**END**
