# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 153
**Title:** The National Implementation Lobby
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the Prime Minister announced the creation of the National Implementation Lobby.

It was not implementation itself.

It was the space immediately before implementation, where implementation could gather its thoughts.

Communications said this showed momentum.

Policy said it showed proximity to momentum.

Legal asked whether a lobby could be held responsible for what happened in the room next door.

Treasury asked whether the room next door had been budgeted for.

The Cabinet Secretary convened an emergency meeting to decide whether the lobby was a phase, a venue, or a metaphor with chairs.

Digital said it could build a portal showing citizens where their issue was waiting to be implemented.

The junior adviser asked whether waiting to be implemented was different from not being implemented.

Communications said yes, because it had signage.

Policy added that signage was a visible commitment to future substance.

The Prime Minister said the public deserved action that was orderly, responsible, and not so sudden that it looked like someone had started.

The Cabinet Secretary wrote "VISIBLE PRE-DELIVERY" on the whiteboard.

Everyone agreed it sounded close enough to a verb.

## ACT ONE

By 08:30, departments had been instructed to move all outstanding promises into the lobby.

Transport reported that several projects were now adjacent to commencement.

Health said a number of improvements were in the arrivals area.

Education said its reforms had checked in but were waiting for a badge.

The Home Office said its programme was in the lobby in principle, although operationally it remained in a secure corridor.

Digital unveiled a dashboard with four columns:

ANNOUNCED.

LOBBYING.

NEAR DELIVERY.

DELIVERY-SENSITIVE.

Communications liked delivery-sensitive because it sounded careful rather than late.

Legal asked whether near delivery could be interpreted as a promise.

Treasury asked whether promises carried heating costs.

The Cabinet Secretary proposed that all commitments should be described as "in managed proximity to delivery" until further notice.

The junior adviser asked what happened after managed proximity.

Policy said there would be a gateway review.

Communications said gateways made the lobby feel purposeful.

Treasury asked whether one gateway could serve several promises if the promises took turns.

## ACT TWO

At 10:00, the Prime Minister rehearsed a statement for regional broadcasters.

"We are getting on with delivery."

Legal said getting on with was too intimate.

Policy said delivery implied arrival.

Communications said the line needed energy but no measurable speed.

The revised version became: "We are ensuring delivery has the conditions it needs."

Treasury asked whether conditions required procurement.

Digital suggested: "The system has recognised multiple active implementation candidates."

The Cabinet Secretary said it had the calming authority of a printer menu.

The junior adviser said the public might ask when anything would actually happen.

Communications said actually was a hostile adverb.

Meanwhile, a department submitted a request to move one small policy out of the lobby and into delivery.

The room went quiet.

Policy asked whether the policy had been exposed to stakeholder temperature.

Legal asked whether delivery would create precedent.

Treasury asked whether precedent could be capped.

Communications asked whether the policy could instead be photographed near a door.

The Cabinet Secretary approved the photograph, provided the door remained closed and no one implied hinges.

## ACT THREE

At midday, the implementation lobby reached capacity.

Digital said the dashboard was showing "constructive congestion."

Communications loved it.

Policy said congestion proved demand for action.

Transport said it preferred the term managed flow.

Health said the lobby should not be described as full, because full suggested a service pressure.

Education proposed a second lobby for commitments that were ready to enter the first lobby.

Treasury asked whether the second lobby could be virtual, seasonal, and unheated.

Legal warned that two lobbies might imply a route.

The Prime Minister asked whether any policy could be safely moved through.

Policy nominated a leaflet review.

Communications said leaflets were risky because they could be read.

Digital suggested changing the leaflet review into a discovery sprint.

The junior adviser asked whether the discovery sprint would discover the leaflet.

The Cabinet Secretary said discovery was not a promise to notice anything specific.

The Prime Minister said the country needed to see a government focused on turning intention into properly governed near-action.

The Cabinet Secretary underlined near-action twice.

## CLIMAX

The afternoon press conference opened with a live tour of the National Implementation Lobby.

Behind the Prime Minister, a screen displayed:

THE NATIONAL IMPLEMENTATION LOBBY

STATUS: OPEN

PROMISES: PRESENT

DELIVERY: BEING PREPARED FOR RESPONSIBLY

The Prime Minister said: "Today we are showing that action is not an event. It is a disciplined environment."

A reporter asked whether anything had been implemented.

Communications signalled "environment."

Policy signalled "disciplined."

Legal signalled "do not say anything."

The Prime Minister said: "Several priorities are now in a strengthened position to proceed toward the relevant next stage."

Another reporter asked what the relevant next stage was.

Digital refreshed the dashboard.

It displayed:

IMPLEMENTATION CONFIDENCE: ENCOURAGING.

LOBBY STABILITY: STRONG.

DELIVERY READINESS: FORMING.

PUBLIC BENEFIT: NEARBY.

The reporter asked whether nearby meant delayed.

Treasury whispered that nearby was cheaper than here.

The Cabinet Secretary stepped forward and explained that the lobby marked a decisive shift from unmanaged non-delivery to structured pre-delivery presence.

The junior adviser said the government had opened a waiting room for its own verbs.

Communications said waiting rooms demonstrated access.

The Prime Minister concluded that the National Implementation Lobby would give the country confidence that delivery was no longer somewhere abstract, but somewhere with a floor plan.

## TAG

By evening, every department had requested a reserved seat in the lobby for its most urgent priority.

The dashboard added a new column: CHAIR-AWARE.

**END**
