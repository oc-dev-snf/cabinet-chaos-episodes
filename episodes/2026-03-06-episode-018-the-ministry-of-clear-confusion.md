# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 018  
**Title:** The Ministry of Clear Confusion  
**Genre:** Dark political satire

---

## COLD OPEN – INT. GOVERNMENT PRESS ROOM – 08:01

A new podium has been installed overnight.

Behind it: three identical logos and the slogan:

> **CLARITY THROUGH ALIGNMENT**

**PRESS SECRETARY NIA DRAKE** steps up with a stack of papers in three colours.

**DRAKE:**
Good morning. To reduce public confusion, we are replacing all conflicting statements with one consistent set of conflicting statements.

Pens pause. Cameras keep rolling.

---

## Premise
After a week of contradictory announcements from different departments, the government launches a "single source of truth" unit. Instead of fixing policy, the unit standardises spin, assigns approved versions of reality by audience segment, and accidentally publishes its own internal scorecard showing which lies poll best.

---

## ACT 1 – THE UNIT

Cabinet Office, briefing room.

A digital board reads:

> **MINISTRY OF CLEAR CONFUSION (MCC)**

Director Owen Kells presents the framework.

- Version A: "firm reassurance"
- Version B: "humble correction"
- Version C: "bold reframing"

**KELLS:**
Different truths for different needs. The public doesn’t want facts. They want emotional ergonomics.

Deputy Minister Alia North raises a hand.

**ALIA:**
What if journalists compare the versions?

**KELLS:**
Then we announce Version D: "context evolved."

---

## ACT 2 – THE MATRIX

In the war room, staff sort audiences by mood rather than geography.

Columns on screen:

- Angry commuters
- Tired parents
- Optimistic retirees
- People who only read headlines
- People who only watch clips at 2x speed

Each group gets a tailored line for the same failed transport reform.

At 11:43, an intern uploads the wrong file to the public site:

> **MCC PERFORMANCE DASHBOARD.xlsx**

Tabs include:

- **Believability Index**
- **Outrage Half-Life**
- **Apology Conversion Rate**

A red cell flashes:

> *"If trust falls below 30%, schedule patriotic infrastructure montage."*

---

## ACT 3 – THE BRIEFING

Back in the press room, Drake takes questions.

**REPORTER:**
Did you knowingly issue four incompatible statements?

**DRAKE:**
Not incompatible. Sequentially valid.

**REPORTER 2:**
Your own spreadsheet calls citizens "narrative receivers."

**DRAKE:**
That term is under review.

Opposition spokesperson Mira Vane appears on split-screen from a corridor.

**VANE:**
They’ve outsourced honesty to an algorithm and kept the invoice hidden.

Government backbencher Tom Eddis, trying to defend the rollout, goes live on radio.

**EDDIS:**
Look, all modern governments optimise communication. We’re just... transparent about not being transparent.

He realises what he said two seconds too late.

---

## ACT 4 – THE CORRECTION CYCLE

By evening, the government releases a corrective statement:

> **"The dashboard was a draft simulation and not intended for external confidence."**

Ten minutes later, a second correction:

> **"The previous correction was itself part of a simulation."**

Twenty minutes later, a third:

> **"Please refer to the pinned post for the latest authoritative uncertainty."**

The pinned post links to a 404 page titled:

> **TRUST HUB (TEMPORARILY UNAVAILABLE)**

---

## TAG – 22:36

In a near-empty office, Kells opens tomorrow’s prep folder.

Slide one reads:

> **PHASE TWO: AUTHENTICITY AUTOMATION**

Subtitle:

> **"Pilot: AI-generated sincerity for ministerial apologies."**

Outside, protest chants drift in through double glazing.

Inside, someone adjusts the dashboard threshold from **30% trust** to **24% trust** and labels it:

> **REALISTIC MODE**
