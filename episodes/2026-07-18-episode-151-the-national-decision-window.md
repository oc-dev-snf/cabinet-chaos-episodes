# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 151
**Title:** The National Decision Window
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the Prime Minister announced that the government would open a National Decision Window.

No decision had yet been placed inside it.

Communications said the window demonstrated openness.

Policy said it demonstrated potential openness, pending a view.

Legal asked whether a window could be interpreted as an invitation.

Treasury asked whether windows counted as infrastructure.

The Cabinet Secretary convened an emergency meeting to decide whether the Decision Window was a process, a metaphor, or a room booking.

Digital said it could be all three with the right login.

The junior adviser asked what happened if the public looked through the window and saw nothing.

Communications said nothing was better than confusion, provided nothing was branded correctly.

Policy said there were several options ready to be considered.

Legal said considered should not imply touched.

Treasury asked whether untouched options depreciated.

The Prime Minister said the country needed to know that decisions were visible, even before they became decisions.

The Cabinet Secretary wrote "VISIBLE PRE-DECISION CONFIDENCE" on the whiteboard.

Everyone nodded, because the alternative was choosing something.

## ACT ONE

By 08:30, departments had been asked to submit items suitable for the first Decision Window.

Transport offered a consultation on the direction of future directions.

Health offered a pathway to align pathway language.

Education offered a pilot to improve the way pilots are announced.

The Home Office offered a robust framework whose details were available only to robustness.

Digital offered a portal showing whether the portal was ready to show things.

Treasury offered a spending envelope with no spending and a very firm envelope.

Communications said the first item needed to feel decisive without becoming specific.

Policy suggested "a commitment to review the options for action."

Legal removed commitment.

Treasury removed action.

Digital added a status badge.

The resulting phrase read: "A review of options."

Communications said it lacked lift.

The Cabinet Secretary upgraded it to "A National Review of Available Options."

Legal said available created exposure.

Treasury said national created cost.

The junior adviser suggested "Some options exist."

The room regarded this as dangerously close to evidence.

## ACT TWO

At 10:00, the Decision Window design team arrived with three prototypes.

The first showed a large button marked DECIDE.

Legal rejected it immediately.

The second showed a progress bar moving from reflection to delivery.

Policy asked what came between reflection and delivery.

Digital said the bar did not have room for all of government.

The third showed a calm blue rectangle with the words "Decision Activity Underway."

Communications said it was perfect because it sounded alive.

Treasury asked whether activity was chargeable.

The Prime Minister rehearsed the launch line: "Today, we are opening the window on the decisions that matter."

Legal said opening the window might imply ventilation.

Policy said decisions that matter might imply some decisions did not.

Communications suggested: "Today, we are increasing visibility around decision activity."

The Cabinet Secretary said it had the texture of seriousness.

The junior adviser asked whether the public wanted texture or a decision.

Communications said the public wanted to feel the machinery working.

Policy said the machinery preferred not to be observed while thinking.

## ACT THREE

At midday, a briefing note accidentally included a column titled "ACTUAL DECISION."

The room treated it as a leak from the future.

Communications proposed renaming it "Public-Facing Moment."

Policy preferred "Potential Settlement Point."

Legal preferred "Non-Binding Directional Event."

Treasury preferred deleting the column.

The Prime Minister asked whether there was anything ready to announce.

Policy said several things were near readiness.

Legal said near should be replaced with adjacent.

Digital said adjacent could be mapped.

Treasury said mapping adjacent readiness required a business case.

The Cabinet Secretary proposed a compromise: the government would announce the opening of the Decision Window and invite departments to populate it in due course.

Communications said due course sounded orderly.

Policy said populate sounded constructive.

Legal said invite sounded voluntary enough.

The junior adviser asked whether the government was effectively launching a shelf.

The Cabinet Secretary said it was not a shelf.

Digital said the interface was technically a shelf.

Communications said the word shelf would never leave the room.

## CLIMAX

The press conference began at 15:00.

Behind the Prime Minister, the screen displayed:

THE NATIONAL DECISION WINDOW

STATUS: OPEN

DECISIONS: BEING POSITIONED

The Prime Minister said: "For too long, people have not been able to see where decisions are in the system."

A reporter asked where this decision was.

Communications signalled "visible."

Policy signalled "process."

Legal signalled "avoid location."

The Prime Minister said: "It is visible within the process."

Another reporter asked whether anything had actually been decided.

Digital refreshed the dashboard.

It displayed:

WINDOW CONDITION: ACTIVE.

OPTION FIELD: POPULATING.

DECISION CONFIDENCE: EMERGING.

DELIVERY VIEW: PENDING VIEW.

The reporter asked whether emerging confidence was different from uncertainty.

Treasury whispered that uncertainty was cheaper.

The Cabinet Secretary said the dashboard showed an encouraging transition from unstructured possibility to managed expectation.

The junior adviser said the government had opened a window, put a curtain in front of it, and called the curtain transparency.

Communications said that was a strong visual if framed positively.

The Prime Minister concluded that the National Decision Window would allow the country to watch government move with care, pace, and appropriate caution towards the point at which movement could responsibly be described.

## TAG

By evening, departments had been instructed to identify all pending decisions and assess whether they were window-ready.

Legal circulated guidance on the safe use of glass-related metaphors.

Digital added a curtain opacity slider.
