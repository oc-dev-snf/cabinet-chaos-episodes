**Title:** The National Microphone Etiquette Protocol
**Genre:** Original political satire

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE SITUATION ROOM, 07:02. A strategic call is underway. Someone is typing with the intensity of a woodpecker.)

Morning briefing card:

**PUBLIC SERVICE DELIVERY WEEK — DAY 12**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Microphone Etiquette Protocol"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why does transport policy sound like it's being drafted inside a dishwasher?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Multiple teams joined from shared offices. No one claimed the noise.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** We currently have three speaking requests, two echo loops, and one unidentified crisp packet.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Good. Nothing says institutional credibility like mystery crunching.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE SOUND OF ADMINISTRATION

08:19. Cross-government coordination call. Slide title: **"AUDIO FRICTION AND DECISION DRIFT"**.

**PM:** We lost nine minutes this morning to "can you hear me now" diplomacy.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Two directors delivered identical updates because both thought they were on mute and kept restarting.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** A clip is circulating of a senior official giving a solemn update while a smoke alarm chirps every 43 seconds.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We are governing by notification tone.

Review identifies four recurrent failures:

1. Unclear speaking order in large calls
2. Open mics during sensitive briefings
3. No shared rules for remote, office, and hybrid attendees
4. Meeting chairs improvising moderation under pressure

**PM:** I want less acoustic improvisation and more actual decisions.

## ACT TWO — PROTOCOL BY NECESSITY

10:37. Rapid response group assembled: operations, digital, HR, security, and ministerial private offices.

Slide title: **"IF EVERYONE SPEAKS, NO ONE IS HEARD"**

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Default all-hands calls to moderated queue mode above 20 participants.

**HR LEAD:** Provide a chair script that includes explicit handoff lines and a 15-second pause discipline.

**SECURITY LEAD:** Sensitive meetings require headset-only attendance unless in approved secure rooms.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Introduce a two-minute pre-call check: mic, camera, notifications, and background noise.

Three controls approved immediately:

- **Queue-first speaking protocol** for high-attendance calls
- **Mandatory chair script** for policy and incident meetings
- **Pre-call audio check** logged by meeting coordinator

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** At last, a policy that can be enforced by not talking over each other.

## ACT THREE — LIVE PILOT

12:14. Pilot exercise with 160 participants across 19 teams.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Injecting two events: accidental unmute wave at minute five, and chair handoff at minute eleven.

12:19. Unmute wave occurs.

**CHAIR:** Non-speakers remuted. Queue preserved. Continue with item three.

12:25. Chair handoff triggered.

**DEPUTY CHAIR:** Taking over. Decision log remains live. No agenda reset.

12:33. Meeting closes on time with all decision items recorded.

Immediate follow-up approved:

- Daily 08:20 high-volume briefing under new protocol
- Weekly chairing drills for private office teams
- One-page etiquette guide issued to all departments
- 30-day scorecard: interruption time, overrun minutes, decision completion

**PM:** This is the rare reform that improves both efficiency and blood pressure.

## ACT FOUR — ANNOUNCING BORING SUCCESS

16:52. Press line signed off: **"GOVERNMENT STANDARDISES HYBRID MEETING ETIQUETTE"**.

Public line focuses on:

- Clear speaking and moderation rules
- Better continuity during host or chair transitions
- Reduced accidental disruption
- Faster completion of agenda decisions
- Measurable service discipline across departments

By 18:07 (pilot metrics):

- Non-agenda interruption time down 48%
- Meetings ending within scheduled window up 31%
- Repeat updates due to confusion down 57%
- Decision items closed during first pass up 24%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We are celebrating calm, which is difficult to brand but good for everyone.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If the loudest sound tomorrow is agreement, frame it.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:29. Reporters gather outside the atrium.

**REPORTER:** Minister, are microphone rules really worth national attention?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Small dysfunction multiplied by thousands of meetings becomes real delay.

**REPORTER:** Some call this over-management.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Basic standards are how you free people to do substantive work.

**REPORTER:** Is this a one-off fix?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. It's a maintenance habit with measurable outcomes.
