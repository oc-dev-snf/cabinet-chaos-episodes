# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 047
**Title:** The National Pothole Innovation Challenge
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(TRANSPORT BRIEFING ROOM. A giant map of roads is covered in red dots. A banner reads: “ZERO POTHOLES, MAXIMUM CONFIDENCE.”)

**CALLUM:** Is that the whole country in red?

**TRANSPORT ADVISER:** Mostly roads. And one very upset car park in Swindon.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Morning headlines say drivers are forming support groups around wheel alignment.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We need a decisive announcement by noon.

**INNOVATION LEAD:** Good news. We can launch a National Pothole Innovation Challenge.

**CALLUM:** We already know how to fill potholes.

**INNOVATION LEAD:** Exactly. But now we can do it competitively.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

Emergency options are presented on a slide deck titled: “Road Surface Futures.”

Option A: fund local councils directly for rapid repairs.
Option B: create a central platform for “crowdsourced crater intelligence.”
Option C: host a televised hackathon.

**CALLUM:** A is practical. Why are B and C longer?

**POLICY LEAD:** They have stronger narrative architecture.

**CALLUM:** Do they have asphalt?

**POLICY LEAD:** Not explicitly.

A junior analyst quietly passes up a one-page note:

**“If we release emergency maintenance funds today and simplify procurement for 90 days, repairs can begin this week.”**

The room nods politely, then returns to discussing app branding.

## ACT TWO

A prototype demo begins.

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** Citizens upload pothole photos, then rate potholes by emotional impact.

**CALLUM:** Emotional impact?

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** “Mildly betrayed” to “existentially jarred.”

**OPS DIRECTOR:** The platform can auto-generate a “crater journey score” for each constituency.

**CALLUM:** Could it auto-generate crews with shovels?

**OPS DIRECTOR:** Phase two.

Meanwhile, local councils call in with the same request: permission to use existing contractors without a six-week tender exercise.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** If we waive that temporarily, fixes start immediately.

**GOVERNANCE LEAD:** That is efficient, but we’ll need a robust accountability wrapper.

**CALLUM:** How robust?

**GOVERNANCE LEAD:** Four forms and a weekly steering call.

**CALLUM:** So... less robust.

## ACT THREE

Press office drafts three slogans:
- “Paving the Future”
- “Innovation Under Your Tyres”
- “No Hole Left Behind”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We can launch with hard hats and drones.

**CALLUM:** Or with repaired roads.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Harder to film.

Callum takes the marker and writes a new plan on the whiteboard:

1. Release emergency road maintenance funding today.
2. 90-day fast-track procurement for patch and resurface work.
3. Publish weekly repair totals by area.
4. Keep the reporting to one page.

**TRANSPORT ADVISER:** That’s... surprisingly implementable.

**CALLUM:** Aim low enough and we may actually hit competence.

## CLIMAX

Noon press statement:

**“Government will fund immediate local road repairs, cut temporary procurement delays, and publish weekly progress so communities can track delivery.”**

A reporter asks whether this replaces the Innovation Challenge.

**CALLUM:** We ran a challenge internally. The winning idea was “fix the potholes.”

**REPORTER:** Any role for new technology?

**CALLUM:** Yes. Phones can still take photos. Crews can still use maps. We’re not anti-progress; we’re pro-flat-road.

## TAG

(EVENING. Situation room dashboard updates.)

**AIDE:** First-day figures are in. Crews dispatched to 312 priority sites.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Social media sentiment is up. Apparently “my suspension survived” is trending.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Can we still do the drone footage?

**CALLUM:** Yes. Film the repaired roads. Wild concept: before-and-after.

An email pops up:

**Subject:** “Invitation: Global Summit on Disruptive Surface Strategy.”

Callum reads it, sighs, and forwards it to “Someday/Maybe.”

**END**
