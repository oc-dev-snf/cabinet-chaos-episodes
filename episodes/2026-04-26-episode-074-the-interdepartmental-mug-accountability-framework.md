# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 074
**Title:** The Interdepartmental Mug Accountability Framework
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE KITCHEN, 07:00. A hand-written sign reads: **"MUGS ARE PUBLIC ASSETS"**. Dishwasher open, containing one spoon and a classified folder.)

Morning briefing card:

**GOVERNMENT OPERATING DISCIPLINE WEEK — DAY 6**  
**Scenario:** *"The Interdepartmental Mug Accountability Framework"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is everyone holding paper cups like we are at a flood relief center?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Ceramic stock has collapsed. No one can prove who took what to which floor.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Facilities logged forty-seven "temporary mug loans" and one "strategic saucer redeployment."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We have decentralised crockery without federalising responsibility.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — CAFFEINE WITHOUT GOVERNANCE

08:11. Incident room. Whiteboard reads: **NO MUGS, NO MEETINGS.**

**PM:** We can survive policy disagreement. We cannot survive Monday with no tea.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Every building uses a different kitchen norm. Some label mugs by team, some by rank, some by passive-aggressive Post-it.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Press office has already drafted: "Government launches emergency cup procurement taskforce."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We are one headline away from becoming a hydration consultancy.

Rapid assessment identifies five structural faults:

1. No shared inventory baseline for reusable mugs
2. No return pathway across floors or departments
3. Kitchen ownership split between estates, facilities, and nobody
4. Meeting rooms treated as mug sinkholes
5. No minimum service standard for wash-cycle turnaround

**PM:** Build a fix that is boring, fast, and impossible to misread.

## ACT TWO — FRAMEWORK, NOT FOLKLORE

10:36. Joint session with estates, facilities, procurement, and departmental operations.

Slide title: **"REUSABLE CUPS REQUIRE REUSABLE RULES"**.

**FACILITIES LEAD:** Proposal: one shared mug register by building zone, not by team hierarchy.

**ESTATES DIRECTOR:** Add return points on every floor with two daily collection sweeps.

**PROCUREMENT LEAD:** Standardise replacement orders with a cap tied to verified losses, not panic estimates.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Keep it practical. Track movement patterns, not personalities.

Framework agreed with four mandatory controls:

- **Inventory:** daily mug count by zone with visible dashboard
- **Flow:** floor-level return stations with fixed pickup times
- **Ownership:** named kitchen operations lead per building
- **Service level:** 90-minute maximum from return bin to clean shelf

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** At last, policy that fits on a lanyard card and survives contact with reality.

## ACT THREE — THE LIVE TRIAL

13:09. Pilot across three central offices during peak meeting turnover.

**FACILITATOR:** Simulate high-demand usage with 30% cross-floor movement and random room switches.

**SITE A:** Return bins reduced corridor mug drift within one hour.

**SITE B:** Wash-cycle bottleneck cleared after rescheduling dishwasher loads off quarter-hour peaks.

**SITE C:** Initial confusion over ownership resolved when building lead posted visible duty rota.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Predictable result: where ownership is explicit, crockery remains in civilisation.

Mid-pilot corrective pack approved:

- 06:45 baseline count before first briefings
- Dedicated "meeting sweep" at 11:30 and 16:00
- Weekly variance review on losses above threshold
- One-page kitchen protocol issued to all teams

**PM:** Administrative competence is just logistics plus memory.

## ACT FOUR — THE ANNOUNCEMENT

17:04. Press office posts: **"COMMON REUSABLE CUP STANDARD ADOPTED ACROSS CENTRAL OFFICES"**.

Statement includes:

- Shared inventory and return model
- Building-level ownership and rota accountability
- Wash-cycle service standard
- Evidence-based replacement procurement
- Four-week review timetable

By 18:03 (pilot metrics):

- Reusable mug availability up 71%
- Paper cup usage down 58%
- Meeting start delays linked to beverage setup down 36%
- Average mug return time cut from 5h 20m to 1h 12m

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We solved a small problem properly, so no crisis developed. This may confuse people.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Quiet systems are the loudest proof of work.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:31. Media pen near reception coffee cart.

**REPORTER:** Minister, should government really be announcing mug policy?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Institutions run on details. Details include clean cups and functional mornings.

**REPORTER:** Critics call this symbolic management.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Symbolic management uses slogans. This uses collection routes.

**REPORTER:** What if departments ignore the framework?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Then they explain variance figures at review, holding a paper cup.

## TAG

**"RETURN FAST. REFILL FASTER."**
