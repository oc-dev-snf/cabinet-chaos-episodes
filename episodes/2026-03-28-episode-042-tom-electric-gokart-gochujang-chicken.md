# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 042
**Title:** Tom, Torque & Gochujang
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(OPEN PLAN OFFICE. A whiteboard reads: “DELIVERABLES, NOT DRAMA.” Someone has added underneath: “ALSO LUNCH.”)

CALLUM stands over a laptop. TOM bursts in carrying a helmet and a takeaway bag.

**TOM:** Right. Two announcements. One: I have upgraded transport strategy.

**CALLUM:** Is this another scooter incident report in human form?

**TOM:** Electric go-kart. Street legal-ish in spirit.

**CALLUM:** “In spirit” is usually where insurance dies.

**TOM:** Two: I brought gochujang chicken.

**CALLUM:** Spell that.

**TOM:** G-o-c-h-u-j-a-n-g.

**CALLUM:** Good. Last time procurement wrote “gocha-gang” and we almost signed a catering contract with a drum-and-bass collective.

(Beat. Everyone stares at the helmet.)

**CALLUM:** Why is it scorched?

**TOM:** Aerodynamics. And confidence.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

Emergency standup. No one is standing because morale has posture injuries.

**CALLUM:** Today’s priorities: ship cleanly, communicate clearly, and nobody test-drive anything in the car park during office hours.

**TOM:** Counterproposal: we launch morale initiative “Torque Thursday.”

**CALLUM:** It is Saturday.

**TOM:** Pilot program then.

MARS from Comms joins, already tired.

**MARS:** I have drafted three statements in advance:
1) “No one was hurt.”
2) “No policy was technically breached.”
3) “We are reviewing the definition of technically.”

**CALLUM:** Why three?

**MARS:** Experience.

A junior analyst peeks in.

**ANALYST:** Is the smell… chilli?

**TOM:** Gochujang chicken. High heat, high trust.

**CALLUM:** This is exactly how chaos gets branded as culture.

## ACT TWO

Lunch becomes a strategic summit. Greaseproof paper serves as briefing material.

**TOM:** Look, the go-kart is not a toy. It’s an agile mobility platform.

**CALLUM:** You zip-tied a cup holder to a battery.

**TOM:** Customer-centric hydration.

**MARS:** Legal asked if “customer” means “Tom.”

**TOM:** Legal has no vision.

They taste the chicken.

**CALLUM:** Annoyingly good.

**MARS:** What’s in the sauce?

**TOM:** Gochujang, garlic, soy, a little honey, and consequences.

Phone buzz. Building manager message: “Who parked a tiny racing vehicle in visitor bay?”

**CALLUM:** Tom.

**TOM:** It is charging. That is not parking. That is future-readiness.

## ACT THREE

Crisis room. Whiteboard now reads: “RISK REGISTER.” Under it: “+ napkins.”

**CALLUM:** We need one coherent plan before this becomes a story with photos.

**MARS:** Too late. There are already photos. One has 2,000 likes and the caption says “Startup Wimbledon kart finals.”

**TOM:** Organic reach.

**CALLUM:** We are not pivoting into motorsport because you discovered a plug socket.

**TOM:** I’m not pivoting. I’m integrating.

**CALLUM:** Into what?

**TOM:** Delivery ops. Imagine this: hot gochujang chicken dispatched at speed.

**MARS:** Please stop saying “at speed” while legal is in the channel.

The analyst returns with printed comments from social:
- “Is this satire?”
- “Can I invest?”
- “Recipe pls.”

**CALLUM:** Great. We’ve accidentally built a brand triangle: transport, food, and plausible deniability.

## CLIMAX

Town-hall in the office kitchen.

**CALLUM:** Final decision. We keep the innovation energy, remove the litigation energy.

**TOM:** Clarify.

**CALLUM:** The go-kart stays offsite. The chicken stays onsite. Weekly lunch demo, monthly ops demo, zero vehicular demos near HR.

**MARS:** I can sell that. “Bold flavour, controlled rollout.”

**TOM:** Can I at least rename the initiative?

**CALLUM:** Fine.

**TOM:** Project Redline Drumstick.

**CALLUM:** No.

**MARS:** Absolutely no.

**ANALYST (quietly):** I kind of love it.

(Everyone hears it. Silence. Tiny nods.)

**CALLUM:** …Workshopping title pending.

## TAG

Evening. Callum opens the office fridge. A labelled container:
“GOCHUJANG CHICKEN — NOT FOR PILOT TESTING. – Tom”

He smiles, takes one piece, and checks the group chat.

New message from Tom:
“Good news: go-kart now has indicators.”

Second message:
“Bad news: they’re bike lights taped to a mug.”

Callum closes fridge, stares into middle distance.

**CALLUM:** We are one decent idea away from disaster at all times.

(He takes another piece of chicken anyway.)

**END**
