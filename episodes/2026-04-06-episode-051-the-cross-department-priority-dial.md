# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 051
**Title:** The Cross-Department Priority Dial
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OPERATIONS ROOM. Three dashboards show the same programme marked simultaneously as RED, AMBER, and “gently urgent.”)

**CALLUM:** Why is one project on three urgency levels at once?

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Different departments use different priority scales.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** One team has five levels. One has seven. One has “priority, but emotionally.”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Reporters are asking why “critical” items keep waiting behind “strategic soon-ish.”

**CALLUM:** Excellent. We’ve invented quantum backlog.

**POLICY DIRECTOR:** We could launch a Cross-Department Priority Dial. One shared scale.

**CALLUM:** If this ends with laminated posters, I’m resigning to a quiet spreadsheet.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

A policy sprint maps current systems:

- 19 priority labels in active use across central teams.
- 34% of escalations are re-escalations of already escalated work.
- Average time to agree “how urgent” something is: 11 days.

**CALLUM:** We’re spending two weeks naming the fire.

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** Proposal: four levels only — NOW, NEXT, PLAN, PARK.

**CALLUM:** Clear. Brutal. Human.

**DELIVERY LEAD:** Each level gets a service promise. NOW means same-day owner. NEXT means owner in 48 hours.

**CALLUM:** Good. Urgency without ownership is just theatre.

Treasury joins with mild alarm.

**BUDGET OFFICIAL:** If everything shifts to NOW, won’t this collapse?

**CALLUM:** Then nothing is NOW. Cap it: no team may mark more than 15% of work as NOW without director sign-off.

## ACT TWO

Pilot rolls out in transport, health, and local government interfaces.

Day three:

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** “NOW” tickets fell from 42% to 13% after caps.

**CALLUM:** Reality returns.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** But teams are gaming “NEXT” by adding “urgent context” in notes.

**CALLUM:** Add a required field: consequence if delayed.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Dropdown options?

**CALLUM:** No. Full sentence. If they can’t explain consequence, it’s probably PARK.

A governance adviser raises process risk.

**GOVERNANCE ADVISER:** Different departments still need policy discretion.

**CALLUM:** Keep discretion. Standardize language. Freedom in policy, consistency in triage.

**POLICY DIRECTOR:** Shared monthly calibration meeting?

**CALLUM:** Thirty minutes. No slides longer than the problem.

## ACT THREE

Comms proposes launch lines:

- “One government, one urgency language.”
- “Faster triage, clearer accountability.”
- “Less escalation noise, more delivery signal.”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Should we brand this as a national transformation?

**CALLUM:** It’s a common dictionary with deadlines.

Implementation agreed:

1. Priority Dial levels fixed: NOW, NEXT, PLAN, PARK.
2. Mandatory owner + due date on NOW/NEXT items.
3. “Consequence if delayed” sentence required for escalation.
4. Team-level NOW cap at 15%, override logged publicly.
5. Weekly scorecard: volume by level, age of NOW items, escalation reversals.

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** This might reduce panic.

**CALLUM:** Good. Panic is not a delivery method.

## CLIMAX

Press briefing, evening.

**REPORTER:** Is this just renaming existing priorities?

**CALLUM:** Yes, and that’s the point. When everyone uses different words, no one can compare performance.

**REPORTER:** Are departments losing autonomy?

**CALLUM:** No. They keep policy control. We’re aligning triage so urgent means urgent everywhere.

**REPORTER:** What happens if teams ignore the caps?

**CALLUM:** Overrides are visible on the scorecard with reasons. If you want to break the rule, explain it in public.

## TAG

(NIGHT. OPERATIONS ROOM. The “NOW” queue is shorter, and older items have named owners.)

**AIDE:** First pilot week: unresolved escalations down 31%.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Also, fewer messages marked “critical!!!” with three exclamation marks.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Should we still print mugs that say “Dial Before You Escalate”?

**CALLUM:** Fine. But only if they’re reusable and the procurement note is shorter than this conversation.

A new request pops up.

**Subject:** “Immediate request to classify tomorrow’s brainstorming as NOW.”

Callum clicks PARK, assigns an owner, and leaves before the priority debate can reproduce.

**END**
