# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 014
**Title:** The Corridor Pilot of Common Sense
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

A fluorescent-lit corridor outside a committee room. Three whiteboards, two dead markers, one tray of untouched fruit.

A laminated sign reads: **"PILOT: COMMON SENSE DELIVERY UNIT"**.

**MINISTER:** Why is common sense in pilot phase?

**SPAD:** Because full rollout failed impact assessment.

**PERMANENT SECRETARY:** Also, there is no budget line for “sense” unless we call it transformation.

A policy officer appears with a stack of briefing cards held together by panic.

**POLICY OFFICER:** We’ve got twenty minutes before the press asks why yesterday’s announcement contradicts last week’s announcement.

**MINISTER:** Does it?

**POLICY OFFICER:** In every available tense.

---

## ACT ONE

Inside the room, the team reviews the new operating model.

Slide one: **"LESS NOISE, MORE CLARITY"**.

Slide two: seven layers of approval, twelve arrows, and a dotted line labelled **"TRUST (aspirational)"**.

**COMMS LEAD:** The idea is simple. We answer straight questions with straight answers.

**DIRECTOR OF DELIVERY:** We’ve drafted a prototype response.

> “We are proactively reviewing implementation pathways in light of evolving stakeholder expectations.”

**MINISTER:** That is not straight. That is a staircase with no landing.

**SPAD:** We can simplify.

**COMMS LEAD:** We did. That was version twelve.

A junior aide wheels in coffee and a portable printer that sounds like it’s filing a formal complaint.

**AIDE:** Journalists are in the lobby. One asked if this is a relaunch of the relaunch.

**MINISTER:** Tell them it’s continuity.

**PERMANENT SECRETARY:** With what?

**MINISTER:** With confidence.

**PERMANENT SECRETARY:** We’re currently out of confidence. Could offer consistency.

---

## ACT TWO

The pilot begins with a live test: one reporter, one question, one answer, no jargon.

**REPORTER (practice feed):** Why did the policy change overnight?

**MINISTER:** Because new evidence came in.

Everyone freezes. Silence. Genuine surprise.

**SPAD:** That was... understandable.

**COMMS LEAD:** Disturbingly understandable.

**DIRECTOR OF DELIVERY:** We need guardrails before this gets reckless.

Legal enters carrying a folder marked **"WORDS WE CAN LIVE WITH"**.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** You may say “new evidence.” You may not say “we guessed wrong.”

**MINISTER:** What if we did guess wrong?

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Then we deeply considered several possibilities with urgency.

The team drafts emergency language tiers:

- Tier 1: plain English
- Tier 2: careful English
- Tier 3: committee-safe mist

**PERMANENT SECRETARY:** We keep jumping from Tier 1 to fog machine.

Phones buzz. A leaked internal draft is now circulating online, including the phrase:

**"Narrative hydration checkpoint."**

**SPAD:** We are getting mocked for hydrating the narrative.

**COMMS LEAD:** Who wrote that?

No one makes eye contact.

---

## ACT THREE

Crisis room. New rule board goes up:

1. say what happened
2. say what changes next
3. say when people will notice
4. stop inventing poetic nouns

**MINISTER:** Good. We do that.

**DIRECTOR OF DELIVERY:** One caveat. The steering group wants a pilot evaluation framework before we confirm the pilot.

**MINISTER:** Of common sense?

**DIRECTOR OF DELIVERY:** Governance requires evidence of sense before endorsing sense.

**PERMANENT SECRETARY:** We’ve institutionalised hesitation.

The first real press statement is drafted:

> “Yesterday’s guidance was unclear. We corrected it this morning. Updated details are now published.”

The room goes quiet again.

**COMMS LEAD:** It sounds human.

**SPAD:** Are we allowed to sound human?

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Under controlled conditions.

---

## CLIMAX

Live at the lobby microphones.

**JOURNALIST:** Minister, has your department lost control of messaging?

**MINISTER:** We made guidance too complicated. We’ve simplified it and published the update.

**JOURNALIST:** Why should anyone trust this version?

**MINISTER:** Because this one says exactly what changed and when.

**JOURNALIST:** Is that your new strategy?

**MINISTER:** Yes. Fewer slogans. More answers.

In the corridor, staff stare at the monitor like they’ve witnessed a rare weather event.

Breaking caption appears:

**“Department Issues Clear Statement; Analysts Confirm It Contains Verbs.”**

---

## TAG

Late evening. The committee room is empty except for one flickering screen saver and the rule board.

Someone has added a handwritten fifth rule:

**5. if a sentence needs a translator, delete it**

**SPAD (packing up):** Should we escalate this to leadership?

**PERMANENT SECRETARY:** No. Frame it as a temporary corridor trial.

**SPAD:** Temporary?

**PERMANENT SECRETARY:** If they think it’s temporary, they might let it continue.

Lights out.

**END**
