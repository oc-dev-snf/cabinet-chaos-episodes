# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 050
**Title:** The National Inbox Zero Accord
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OPERATIONS ROOM. A wall display shows 14,372 unread internal emails, blinking like a minor emergency.)

**CALLUM:** Why does my inbox look like a weather warning?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Cross-department coordination has intensified.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Also, everyone keeps replying-all to say “Noted.”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Journalists are asking why decision notes arrive after policy announcements.

**CALLUM:** Because half the civil service is trapped in email archaeology.

**POLICY DIRECTOR:** We could launch a National Inbox Zero Accord.

**CALLUM:** Is that policy, or a cry for help?

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

A rapid taskforce presents its findings:

- 41% of internal emails are informational and require no action.
- 28% are reminders about previous reminders.
- 12% are meeting requests to discuss email volume.

**CALLUM:** So email is now self-replicating.

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** We propose four labels: ACTION, DECISION, INFO, and FYI-IF-YOU-INSIST.

**CALLUM:** Keep the first three.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** We can add a mandatory “What I need from you” line at the top.

**CALLUM:** Finally, a sentence with a purpose.

Finance raises a concern.

**BUDGET OFFICIAL:** If people send fewer emails, they might book more meetings.

**CALLUM:** Then cap meetings at 25 minutes unless there is actual fire.

The room quietly agrees this is radical.

## ACT TWO

Pilot begins across three departments.

Day two report:

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Reply-all traffic down 37%.

**CALLUM:** Good.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Calendar invites up 19%.

**CALLUM:** Predictable.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** We introduced a “meeting purpose” field. Blank purpose means automatic decline.

**CALLUM:** Civilization advances.

A governance official enters with caution.

**GOVERNANCE ADVISER:** We must preserve audit trails. Fewer emails cannot mean fewer records.

**CALLUM:** Fine. Decisions go in the case log within one hour. Email is not an archive; it’s a courier.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** We can auto-prompt case-log entry when someone marks a message DECISION.

**CALLUM:** Ship it.

## ACT THREE

Comms drafts language for public briefing:

- “Sharper internal coordination”
- “Faster policy communication”
- “Less inbox drag on delivery”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Should we call it a transformation programme?

**CALLUM:** No. It is an adult sentence and a checkbox.

Implementation plan signed:

1. Required subject prefix: ACTION, DECISION, or INFO.
2. Mandatory first line: requested action + deadline.
3. Default internal meeting length reduced to 25 minutes.
4. Decision emails trigger case-log prompts automatically.
5. Weekly dashboard: unread volumes, response times, and avoidable meeting counts.

**POLICY DIRECTOR:** This feels… practical.

**CALLUM:** Don’t worry, we can still add a logo later.

## CLIMAX

Press scrum, late afternoon.

**REPORTER:** Is “Inbox Zero Accord” a gimmick?

**CALLUM:** It started as one. Then officials asked a dangerous question: “What if we actually fix the process?”

**REPORTER:** Are you banning long emails?

**CALLUM:** No. We’re banning emails that hide the ask like a treasure hunt.

**REPORTER:** And if departments ignore it?

**CALLUM:** Their unread counts become public dashboard metrics. Sunshine is a management tool.

## TAG

(EVENING. OPERATIONS ROOM. Inbox counter now reads 8,903. Still large, but no longer apocalyptic.)

**AIDE:** First-week metrics: median response time down 29% in pilots.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Also, people have stopped replying-all with “Thanks.”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Should we still produce the launch video with dramatic music?

**CALLUM:** Yes, but keep it brief and captioned. If the video is longer than the policy note, we have learned nothing.

A fresh email arrives.

**Subject:** “Discussion: framework for reducing unnecessary framework discussions.”

Callum marks it INFO, closes laptop, and walks out before anyone can click Reply All.

**END**
