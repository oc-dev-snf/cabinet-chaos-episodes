# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 023  
**Title:** The Department of Temporary Permanence  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

**INT. CABINET SITUATION ROOM – 06:58**

A wall screen flashes:

> **URGENT: TEMPORARY ARRANGEMENT NOW IN ITS 14TH MONTH**

An aide places three folders on the table:

- TEMPORARY PLAN
- INTERIM PLAN
- LONG-TERM TEMPORARY PLAN

A minister arrives, still carrying breakfast and a look of regret.

**MINISTER:**
Which one are we implementing?

**AIDE:**
All three, in sequence, simultaneously.

The Prime Minister enters.

**PRIME MINISTER:**
Right. Let’s bring stability to this fluid situation.

---

## ACT ONE

The government launches a new office: **The Department of Temporary Permanence**.

Its mission statement:

> “Delivering short-term arrangements with long-term confidence.”

At the press podium, the spokesperson explains:

1. “Temporary does not mean uncertain.”
2. “Permanent does not mean inflexible.”
3. “We are combining the strengths of both words.”

A reporter asks whether this is just a rebrand of last year’s “Interim Stability Unit.”

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Absolutely not. Different logo.

Across departments, guidance is issued:

- Replace “deadline” with “review horizon.”
- Replace “delay” with “extended transition.”
- Replace “U-turn” with “policy boomerang.”

By noon, every policy tracker has a new status column:

> **SETTLED (FOR NOW)**

---

## ACT TWO

Treasury asks when the temporary energy support will end.

Cabinet Office reply:

> “At the point it is no longer temporarily required on a permanent basis.”

Health asks if emergency staffing funds can be planned beyond this quarter.

Reply:

> “Yes, but in a non-binding way.”

Transport asks whether weekend works are confirmed.

Reply:

> “Confirmed pending possible reconfirmation.”

A policy analyst builds a dashboard called **The Continuity Compass**.

Needle positions include:

- Stable-ish
- Dynamic-stable
- Temporarily locked
- Permanently pending

Every time someone says “just for now,” the needle spins.

Opposition MPs call the scheme “government by sticky note.”

Government MPs call it “pragmatic continuity.”

Voters call it “can someone just tell us what’s happening.”

---

## ACT THREE

Emergency strategy meeting.

Agenda item:

> **How long can a temporary policy remain temporary before it becomes furniture?**

Proposals include:

- A sunset clause that renews itself if nobody objects.
- Annual “temporary audits” to certify ongoing temporariness.
- A national amnesty for expired interim measures.

Legal counsel suggests clearer definitions.

The room pauses, worried.

**SENIOR ADVISER:**
Clear definitions reduce room for manoeuvre.

**LEGAL COUNSEL:**
That is, historically, their purpose.

Silence. Pens hover. Nobody makes eye contact.

---

## CLIMAX

Evening briefing. A journalist asks plainly:

**JOURNALIST:**
When will households see a final, fixed plan on bills and support?

The Prime Minister answers without slogan, without flourish.

**PRIME MINISTER:**
People need predictability. We can’t promise every variable, but we can set firmer milestones and publish them. Tomorrow we’ll release dates, triggers, and what changes if conditions shift. If we need to revise, we’ll explain why in plain language.

The room stills.

A few heads nod.

For one minute, politics sounds like administration again.

In the back row, the communications team quietly delete a draft phrase: “agile permanence.”
