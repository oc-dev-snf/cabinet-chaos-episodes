# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 070
**Title:** The Cabinet Cupboard Capacity Review
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:00. A storage cupboard door is held shut with a ceremonial ribbon and three policy binders.)

Morning briefing card:

**GOVERNMENT OPERATING DISCIPLINE WEEK — DAY 2**  
**Scenario:** *"The Cabinet Cupboard Capacity Review"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is the national resilience cupboard making a noise like a distressed accordion?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** It's over capacity, Minister. We have seven emergency plans, twelve draft emergency plans, and one emergency plan to simplify emergency plans.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Also two ring lights from last year's transparency initiative.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So if there's a crisis, do we open the cupboard or just brief from outside it?

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE INVENTORY HEARING

08:22. Temporary command room. Whiteboard reads: **IF EVERYTHING IS ESSENTIAL, NOTHING IS FINDABLE.**

**PM:** We've had three incidents this quarter where teams couldn't locate the latest guidance in under ten minutes.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** One team printed five conflicting versions and called it "scenario planning."

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Journalists are asking whether government has a system or just very determined shelving.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We do have a system. It's called "where did we put it this time?"

Audit findings are presented:

1. Duplicate guidance stored under different names
2. "Final" documents superseded by newer "final-final" versions
3. Critical checklists hidden in appendix folders
4. Ownership unclear for archived but still active processes
5. Emergency contacts last updated "post-election"

**PM:** New principle: if it can't be found quickly, it doesn't exist operationally.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** And if it has "v7" in the title, we admit something went wrong before breakfast.

## ACT TWO — THE CUPBOARD STANDARD

10:41. Cross-department workshop. Slide title: **ONE CUPBOARD RULESET (DRAFT FOR ADOPTION)**.

**DATA ADVISER:** We propose a single filing standard across departments: Purpose, Owner, Last Tested, Next Test.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Include legal basis for each protocol. No ghost mandates.

**OPERATIONS DIRECTOR:** Also include a retire date, otherwise nothing ever dies.

Standard agreed with four required labels:

- **LIVE:** currently approved and actively used
- **BACKUP:** approved but contingency-only
- **DRAFT:** in review, not operational
- **ARCHIVE:** retained for record, not to be used

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Beautiful. Bureaucracy with verbs.

By 11:33 they add a red-box question for every plan: **"Who acts in the first 15 minutes?"**

**PM:** If that answer is "it depends," it isn't a plan yet.

## ACT THREE — THE STRESS TEST

13:19. Simulation exercise: nationwide service disruption drill.

**FACILITATOR:** You have two minutes to locate response protocol and named duty lead.

**TRANSPORT REP:** Found in 47 seconds.

**HEALTH REP:** Found in 62 seconds; contact list checked this morning.

**EDUCATION REP:** Found in 71 seconds; old version auto-redirected.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Look at that — progress you can measure without interpretive dance.

Mid-exercise issue: one department still stores key procedures in a "misc" folder.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Who named a folder "misc" in a crisis system?

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Someone optimistic about memory.

They enforce a fix:

- No "misc" labels in operational directories
- Automatic expiry warnings at 90/60/30 days
- Monthly cupboard owner sign-off
- Quarterly public summary of readiness documentation hygiene

**PM:** We are now publishing cupboard governance. This is where we are as a country.

## ACT FOUR — THE RELEASE

17:06. Press office briefing: **CABINET CAPACITY & CLARITY UPDATE**.

Release package includes:

- Unified directory map for all central response plans
- Public explanation of LIVE/BACKUP/DRAFT/ARCHIVE labels
- Timestamped ownership register
- Test-cycle calendar for core plans
- Plain-language note on what changed this week

By 18:02:

- Internal retrieval time down 54%
- Duplicate document reports down 41%
- Staff confidence score up from 5.8 to 7.1
- External queries answered with one consistent reference set

**HEAD OF COMMS:** This is the first time "where is the plan" had one answer.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Write that on the wall. Then laminate it irresponsibly.

## CLIMAX — THE PRESS PENALTY BOX

18:44. Lobby scrum.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this genuine reform or just tidying before scrutiny?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If tidying lets people act faster in real events, that's reform in sensible shoes.

**REPORTER:** Why not announce a bigger structural overhaul?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because "big" is not a delivery unit. "Find the right document in under a minute" is.

**REPORTER:** Are you saying government has been running on clutter?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I'm saying we've replaced ornamental complexity with usable order. Nationally, that's a growth sector.

## TAG

20:27. Corridor outside the command room. The cupboard door now closes quietly.

A fresh sticker appears:

**"IF IT'S IMPORTANT, IT GETS A LABEL."**

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (off-screen):** Next week we do the shared drive. Notify my next of kin.

**END**
