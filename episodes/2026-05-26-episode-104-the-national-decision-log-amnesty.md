# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 104
**Title:** The National Decision Log Amnesty
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

A private office whiteboard reads:

**TOP PRIORITIES**
1. Confirm yesterday’s decision
2. Confirm what the decision was about
3. Confirm who confirmed it

A policy adviser whispers, “If we write it down, it becomes discoverable.”

A director whispers back, “If we don’t write it down, it becomes folklore.”

Cut to opening titles.

## ACT ONE

After a week of contradictory announcements, the minister orders every department to produce a complete log of major decisions made in the last quarter.

Nobody can agree on where those decisions live.

Some are in meeting minutes, some in chat threads, some in voice notes, and one appears to be written on the back of a catering invoice.

Cabinet Office declares an emergency fix: the **National Decision Log Amnesty** — a one-week safe window to report undocumented decisions without blame.

A new unit is launched by midday: **LANTERN** — the Log Assurance and Narrative Traceability Emergency Response Network.

## ACT TWO

LANTERN issues a simple form with five fields:
- What was decided
- When it was decided
- Who was in the room
- Why it was decided
- Where evidence might exist

Within hours, submissions flood in.

Patterns emerge:
- 28%: “Decision made in principle, subject to practical reality”
- 19%: “Decision agreed verbally pending formal language”
- 11%: “Decision remembered differently by all attendees”

Comms calls the response “healthy transparency.”

Operations calls it “structured archaeology.”

## ACT THREE

To speed progress, LANTERN runs a live reconciliation session called the **Decision Clinic**.

Three teams bring three versions of the same transport policy decision.

Version A says rollout begins next month.

Version B says rollout begins after a pilot.

Version C says the pilot was the rollout.

A facilitator proposes a compromise statement: “Implementation sequencing remains intentionally adaptive.”

All three teams approve it immediately.

## CLIMAX

At the evening briefing, the minister announces early success:

“Over 4,000 decisions have now been recovered, categorized, and given a stable reference number.”

A reporter asks how many were previously written down.

An official replies, “That depends how generously one defines ‘written’ and ‘down.’”

A second reporter asks what happens after the amnesty ends.

The minister says, “From next week, all decisions must be logged within 24 hours, or they become provisional weather.”

## TAG

At 7:03 p.m., civil servants receive a new template:

**DECISION LOG — QUICK ENTRY (MANDATORY)**

Field 1: Decision summary
Field 2: Confidence level
Field 3: Link to evidence
Field 4: If no evidence, explain why
Field 5: If explanation unavailable, nominate someone optimistic

A footer reads:

“Version 1.0 — superseded by Version 1.1 (attached separately).”

**END**
