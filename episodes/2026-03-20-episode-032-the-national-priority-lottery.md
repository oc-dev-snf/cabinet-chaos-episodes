# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 032  
**Title:** The National Priority Lottery  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## Premise
A central unit launches a "single national priorities list" to force departments to agree what matters most. Within hours, every team relabels its own work as critical, and the list collapses under 147 "top priorities" ranked jointly first.

## COLD OPEN – INT. CABINET OFFICE KITCHENETTE – 7:01 AM

A whiteboard reads:

**"TODAY: PRIORITIES RESET"**

Under it, someone has already written:

**"ALL PRIORITIES ARE PRIORITY."**

**JULIA:**
Who wrote that?

**DAN:**
No signature. Strong cross-departmental energy.

**NINA:**
Or strong fear of choosing.

A policy adviser pins up a flowchart titled:

**"HOW TO DECIDE WHAT MATTERS"**

It is twelve pages long.

---

## ACT ONE – INT. DELIVERY UNIT WAR ROOM – 8:12 AM

A dashboard labeled **NATIONAL PRIORITY REGISTER (BETA)** loads slowly, then reveals:

- 147 items marked "URGENT"
- 93 marked "IMMOVABLE"
- 44 marked "EXISTENTIAL"

**ANALYST:**
The system only supports ten top priorities.

**DAN:**
And we’ve submitted a constitutional novella.

**JULIA:**
Who marked office stationery "existential"?

**ANALYST:**
Facilities. Their note says, "Without pens, governance degrades."

**NINA:**
Fairly hard to dispute.

### SCENE 2 – INT. MINISTERIAL OUTER OFFICE – 8:49 AM

A minister reviews a red folder stamped **"PRIORITY JUSTIFICATION"**.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Why is this section called "Moral urgency narrative"?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
Guidance says each bid needs emotional and operational framing.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Can we be operational without being emotional?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
Not under the new template.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
So we now have performative sincerity as process.

---

## ACT TWO – INT. CROSS-GOVERNMENT TRIAGE CALL – 9:34 AM

Thirty tiny video squares. Everyone speaking in careful urgency.

**CHAIR:**
Right. We need consensus on the top ten.

**HEALTH REP:**
Our seven are non-negotiable.

**TRANSPORT REP:**
Our six are also non-negotiable.

**TREASURY REP:**
Can we reduce urgency inflation before lunch?

**NINA:**
Can we define "priority" first?

Silence. Several people mute to cough strategically.

**DAN:**
Current working definition appears to be "thing I do." 

### SCENE 2 – INT. STRATEGY POD – 10:18 AM

Julia redraws the board with three columns:

1. **Must happen now**
2. **Should happen soon**
3. **Would be nice if reality cooperates**

Teams begin moving cards. Then someone introduces a fourth column:

4. **Must happen now (politically)**

Within minutes, cards migrate back upward.

**JULIA:**
We’ve invented a staircase that only goes to the top floor.

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Media line draft: "Government sharpened focus on delivery priorities."

**DAN:**
Have we?

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Not yet, but we can say "ongoing sharpening."

---

## ACT THREE – INT. LIVE BRIEFING ROOM – 11:11 AM

The spokesperson stands before a slide titled:

**"TOP TEN PRIORITIES"**

The slide has seventeen bullet points in tiny font.

**REPORTER 1:**
Why are there seventeen in the top ten?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Some are jointly ranked.

**REPORTER 2:**
How many are jointly ranked first?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Nine.

**REPORTER 2:**
That seems mathematically assertive.

**SPOKESPERSON:**
We are taking an inclusive approach to urgency.

Backstage, Dan closes his eyes like a man hearing distant thunder indoors.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRIME MINISTER’S OFFICE – 11:47 AM

A one-page note lands on the desk:

> **PRIORITY REGISTER – DAY ONE**  
> Clarity: mixed  
> Participation: universal  
> Decision quality: pending  
> Number of "critical" items: escalating

**PM:**
Why is everything critical?

**ADVISER:**
Because departments are rational. If resources follow priority labels, labels become strategy.

**PM:**
So we built a system that rewards panic.

**NINA:**
More accurately, we formalised it.

---

## CLIMAX – INT. CABINET ROOM – 12:29 PM

Emergency meeting. Whiteboard squeaks under repeated edits.

**COMMITTEE CHAIR:**
Proposal A: Keep one national list.

**TREASURY REP:**
Unworkable unless we cap entries and enforce trade-offs.

**HEALTH REP:**
Trade-offs imply owning losses.

**TRANSPORT REP:**
And owning losses implies headlines.

**JULIA:**
Proposal B: Three-tier list with hard limits.

**DAN:**
And every new "must-do" requires removing one existing "must-do." 

A long, haunted pause.

**PM:**
Approved. Fixed slots. No automatic upgrades. If it rises, something else drops.

Room reaction: the collective face of people discovering arithmetic.

---

## TAG – INT. CABINET OFFICE CORRIDOR – 6:04 PM

A fresh poster goes up:

**"PRIORITY IS A CHOICE, NOT A MOOD."**

Below it, someone has taped a smaller note:

**"To add a priority, remove one."**

A third sticky appears five minutes later:

**"Can we classify this note as urgent?"**

Julia walks past, reads all three, and adds one final line in black marker:

**"Only if you can say what waits instead."**

She caps the marker and keeps walking.

**END**
