**Genre:** Original political satire

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE BRIEFING ROOM, 06:57. A whiteboard reads: **"SECURE ACCESS IS A SHARED VALUE"**. Underneath: **"WHO LOCKED THE PROJECTOR?"**.)

Morning briefing card:

**CIVIC OPERATIONS CONTINUITY WEEK — DAY 9**  
**Scenario:** *"The Cross-Government Password Harmonisation Exercise"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why can nobody open the 7 a.m. briefing deck?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because the password policy changed at midnight in three departments and none of them changed the same way.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** One system now requires 18 characters, one forbids symbols, and one rejects any word found in a dictionary, including "cabinet."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We've created security through mutual incomprehension.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — ACCESS DENIED, GOVERNANCE APPROVED

08:09. Emergency access call. Screen title: **"AUTHENTICATION FRICTION LANDSCAPE"**.

**PM:** I have six secure portals and four of them believe I am an intruder.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Overnight, staff spent 43 cumulative hours on lockouts before breakfast.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Journalists are asking whether "zero trust" now means trusting nobody, including ministers.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We cannot run state business via password reset emails and emotional resilience.

Rapid diagnosis identifies four causes:

1. Departmental password rules changed independently
2. No common cadence for credential rotation
3. Inconsistent multifactor fallback for urgent decision windows
4. Service desks staffed for normal load, not synchronized policy shifts

**PM:** I want secure access that is secure for work, not secure against work.

## ACT TWO — THE HARMONISATION EXERCISE

10:41. Cross-government working group assembled: cyber, digital, HR operations, service desks, and departmental leads.

Slide title: **"CONSISTENCY IS A SECURITY CONTROL"**

**CYBER LEAD:** We keep strong standards but align them: minimum length, passphrase model, and approved symbol set.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Rotation windows move to staged waves, not simultaneous midnight detonations.

**HR OPERATIONS LEAD:** New-starter and role-change flows will include pre-provisioned MFA enrollment before first login.

**SERVICE DESK LEAD:** Critical-hours lockout lane will be staffed and measured separately from routine tickets.

Three controls agreed for immediate rollout:

- **Unified passphrase baseline** across core systems
- **Staggered rotation schedule** with blackout periods around key briefings
- **Emergency authentication protocol** with auditable temporary access path

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** At last, a cybersecurity strategy that permits government to log in.

## ACT THREE — LIVE TEST, REAL PEOPLE, FEWER PANICS

12:06. Simulation begins: 120 users across five departments attempt routine and urgent access tasks.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Test includes a forced lockout in one major portal.

12:12. Lockout spikes as expected.

**SERVICE DESK LEAD:** Critical lane activated. Median wait under six minutes.

12:19. MFA mismatch appears on legacy mobile tokens.

**CYBER LEAD:** Temporary exception path applied under protocol, with automatic expiry and review flag.

12:31. All urgent access tasks completed; incident log generated automatically.

Post-test actions approved:

- Weekly cross-department credential readiness check
- Monthly service desk surge drill tied to policy changes
- Common plain-language user guidance published to all staff
- 30-day review of lockout rates and false-positive security alerts

**PM:** Security should feel disciplined, not theatrical.

## ACT FOUR — COMMUNICATIONS WITHOUT COMPLACENCY

16:48. Press office statement: **"GOVERNMENT ADOPTS COMMON SECURE ACCESS OPERATING STANDARD"**.

Statement highlights:

- Aligned passphrase and MFA baseline
- Staggered change windows to protect operational continuity
- Dedicated urgent-hours access support
- Full audit trail for any emergency exception
- Independent review in 30 days

By 18:03 (pilot figures):

- Staff lockouts during critical windows down 46%
- Median urgent access recovery down from 39m to 11m
- Repeat reset requests down 41%
- Service desk overflow incidents down 52%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We have announced competence in identity management with only mild public yawning.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If the public is bored, that means it worked.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:29. Corridor scrum outside briefing suite.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this not just internal IT housekeeping?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Internal IT housekeeping is what keeps external government functioning.

**REPORTER:** Critics say this proves systems were badly managed before.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Critics are correct that we found a weakness and fixed it. That's the point of management.

**REPORTER:** Are emergency exceptions a security risk?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Uncontrolled exceptions are a risk. Controlled, logged, expiring exceptions are governance.
