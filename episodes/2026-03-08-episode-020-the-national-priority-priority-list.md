A digital board flickers at the front of the room:

> **TOP TEN NATIONAL PRIORITIES**

A policy aide clicks “refresh.”

The list reorders itself.

1. Cost of living  
2. NHS waiting lists  
3. Productivity  
4. Border system capacity  
5. Housing delivery  
6. Skills pipeline  
7. Local transport reliability  
8. Childcare access  
9. Regional growth  
10. Public trust

He clicks “refresh” again.

Now it reads:

1. Public trust  
2. Cost of living  
3. NHS waiting lists  
4. Everything else (under review)

**MINISTER ADA REED** enters with two coffees and a grin that suggests avoidable confidence.

**REED:**
Excellent. Dynamic prioritisation in action.

**CHIEF OF STAFF NOAH QUILL:**
It’s tied to live media coverage.

**REED:**
Even better. Responsive government.

---

## Premise
To prove focus, the government launches a “National Priority Priority List” that updates every hour based on polling, media sentiment, and ministerial interventions. Departments race to align with whichever issue is currently ranked first. By midday, everyone is fully committed to three contradictory agendas, and by evening, the only consistently delivered output is the list itself.

---

## ACT 1 – THE ANNOUNCEMENT

Press prep room, 09:12.

A comms slide reads:

> **ONE GOVERNMENT. ONE LIST. ONE DIRECTION.**

Below it, smaller text reads:

> *Direction subject to hourly recalibration.*

Reed rehearses key lines.

- “This administration is laser-focused.”
- “Every pound goes to top priorities.”
- “If priorities change, that proves we’re listening.”

Quill hands out a one-page implementation note:

- Each department must publish “Priority Alignment Statements” by 11:00
- Spending bids must reference at least two top-three priorities
- Any project outside top ten must be relabelled or paused

Treasury liaison Priya Dhan peers at the clock.

**DHAN:**
The top ten has changed twice during this meeting.

**REED:**
That’s policy agility.

---

## ACT 2 – THE ALIGNMENT PANIC

Cross-department call, 11:37.

A wall of officials appears on-screen, all holding different versions of the same guidance note.

Transport has retagged a rail upgrade as “cost of living resilience.”

Education has renamed a teacher retention package “public trust in outcomes.”

Housing proposes “temporary permanent acceleration zones.”

Cabinet Office analyst Finn Lowe shares a spreadsheet titled:

> **PRIORITY-TO-PROJECT JUSTIFICATION MATRIX v9_final_FINAL2**

Columns include:

- Current priority rank
- Historic priority rank (last 4 hours)
- Confidence score that priority remains priority
- Political shelf-life (minutes)

A red banner appears at the top of everyone’s screen:

> **LIVE UPDATE: SKILLS PIPELINE MOVES TO #1**

Silence.

Then nineteen people start typing at once.

---

## ACT 3 – THE MEDIA CYCLE

Broadcast studio, 15:26.

Reed is interviewed by **JAYA MENON**, calm and relentless.

**MENON:**
At 10 a.m. you said housing was your top priority. At noon it was border processing. At 2 p.m. it was childcare. Which one is it now?

**REED:**
All of them, in an integrated sequence.

**MENON:**
What sequence?

**REED:**
A responsive one.

Opposition spokesperson **Gareth Pike** appears on split-screen.

**PIKE:**
They’ve built a wheel of fortune and called it strategy.

Government backbencher **Leah Broome** calls into a radio show later.

**BROOME:**
Look, people don’t care about rankings. They care that things work.

**HOST:**
Are things working?

Long pause.

**BROOME:**
The list works flawlessly.

---

## ACT 4 – DELIVERY DAY

Cabinet update, 18:54.

Departments submit their “end-of-day delivery returns.”

Combined outcomes:

- 47 policies renamed
- 19 projects reclassified
- 11 press lines updated
- 0 service changes reached the public

Quill presents a success summary:

> **OUTPUT EXCEEDED EXPECTATION: 100% PRIORITY COMPLIANCE ACHIEVED**

Dhan points to the footnote:

> *Compliance measured as timely relabelling activity.*

Reed stares at the board as it refreshes one last time.

New #1 priority:

> **Government Focus and Stability**

She nods.

**REED:**
Finally, something we can all rally behind.

The board immediately demotes it to #4.
