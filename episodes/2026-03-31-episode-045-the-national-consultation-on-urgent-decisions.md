# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 045
**Title:** The National Consultation on Urgent Decisions
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET SITUATION ROOM. A red folder marked URGENT sits untouched beside a tray of pastries.)

**CALLUM:** Why is the emergency response paper still sealed?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** We’re waiting for stakeholder input.

**CALLUM:** It’s an emergency.

**POLICY LEAD:** Exactly. We need inclusive urgency.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** We’ve launched a rapid consultation.

**CALLUM:** How rapid?

**OPS DIRECTOR:** Four phases. Discovery, listening, reflection, and implementation-prep.

**CALLUM:** That’s a project plan, not a sprint.

(A junior adviser appears with two clipboards.)

**AIDE:** Minute-to-minute timeline, minister. Also a second timeline for approving the first timeline.

**CALLUM:** We now have governance for time itself.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

An “Emergency Decision Forum” convenes. Thirty people on video call, all on mute except one person eating crisps.

**CALLUM:** We need one clear decision by noon.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Before noon, we should test language sensitivity.

**POLICY LEAD:** We drafted options A through F.

**CALLUM:** Which is best?

**POLICY LEAD:** Depends whether we optimise speed, confidence, legal resilience, or perception of preparedness.

**CALLUM:** Which one optimises solving the actual problem?

(Long pause. Several cameras turn off.)

On the screen appears a matrix:
- **Fast and risky**
- **Slow and safe**
- **Visible and vague**
- **Invisible and technically perfect**

**OPS DIRECTOR:** We’re leaning toward “visible and vague.” It scales nicely.

**CALLUM:** Vague does scale. That’s why it’s vague.

## ACT TWO

Departments submit final views, each beginning with “fully supportive” and ending with “subject to minor existential concerns.”

A compromise paper emerges: “Immediate Interim Transitional Holding Position (Draft).”

**CALLUM:** This title has three synonyms for not deciding.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** It shows momentum.

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** We can launch a public tracker.

**CALLUM:** Tracking what?

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** Readiness to decide.

Press office requests a statement.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** I’ve drafted: “Government is moving decisively through a structured deliberative process at pace.”

**CALLUM:** That sentence has taken us farther from reality.

Meanwhile, legal team asks whether consultation responses from “Anonymous Spreadsheet 12” count as formal submissions.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** It has 800 rows and one angry pie chart.

**CALLUM:** Fine. Treat it as emotionally binding, not legally binding.

## ACT THREE

Noon approaches. No decision made. Alarm reminder pings every ten minutes with the label: “DECIDE SOMETHING.”

**CALLUM:** Right. New protocol.

He writes on the whiteboard:
1. Name the problem in one sentence.
2. Pick the minimum action that helps today.
3. Publish what we know, what we don’t, and next update time.

**POLICY LEAD:** That removes three working groups.

**CALLUM:** They can be reassigned to useful panic.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** What about consultation?

**CALLUM:** Keep it, but parallel. Action first, refinement second.

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** And the tracker?

**CALLUM:** Track outcomes, not readiness to outcome.

Room goes quiet. Then nods. Reluctant, but real.

A short directive is issued. Clear owner, clear deadline, clear public update slot.

## CLIMAX

Press briefing. Fewer adjectives than usual.

**CALLUM (to cameras):** We made an initial decision this morning, based on current evidence. We’ll update it as new information arrives.

**REPORTER:** So you acted before consulting everyone?

**CALLUM:** We acted to stabilize the situation, and we’re consulting to improve the next step. Both matter.

**REPORTER 2:** Is this a change in government style?

**CALLUM:** It’s a change in government sequencing.

**REPORTER 3:** Are you saying the old process was too slow?

**CALLUM:** I’m saying urgency deserves verbs.

(Several correspondents stop typing, then start again faster.)

## TAG

Late evening. The urgent folder is finally open, empty except for a sticky note:

**“Decision made at 11:42. Nice work. Don’t overthink the next one.”**

Callum smiles for half a second.

Then a new calendar invite lands:

**“Post-Decision Reflection Kickoff (Pre-Read Optional, 47 pages).”**

**CALLUM:** Of course.

He clicks “Maybe.”

**END**
