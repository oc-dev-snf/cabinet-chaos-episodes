# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 147
**Title:** The National Context Shelf
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the government discovered it had misplaced the context.

The Prime Minister wanted it found before questions.

Policy had background.

Communications had tone.

Legal had caveats.

Treasury had a receipt for a consultation room nobody remembered booking.

The Cabinet Secretary said the issue was not that decisions lacked context. The issue was that context existed in too many places and was therefore vulnerable to being understood.

The junior adviser asked whether context was just information near other information.

The room paused.

Communications said that was exactly the sort of unhelpful clarity that created a media cycle.

By 07:14, Policy proposed the National Context Shelf: a secure cross-government repository where every announcement could be placed beside enough surrounding explanation to make it feel inevitable.

The Prime Minister liked it.

It sounded thoughtful, structured, and unlikely to be delivered before lunch.

## ACT ONE

The first disagreement was over what counted as context.

Policy said history.

Communications said framing.

Legal said anything that reduced reliance on verbs.

Treasury said context should be cost-neutral unless it was replacing a more expensive excuse.

Digital displayed the draft categories:

BACKGROUND.

WIDER PICTURE.

RELEVANT CONSIDERATIONS.

EMERGING LANDSCAPE.

The Cabinet Secretary said "emerging landscape" was strong because it sounded like geography without requiring travel.

The Prime Minister asked whether the public would see the shelf.

Communications said the public would see the benefits of the shelf.

The junior adviser asked whether that meant no.

Legal said no was an answer, and answers were best avoided during development.

Communications drafted the launch line:

WE ARE PUTTING EVERY DECISION IN ITS PROPER CONTEXT.

Policy worried "proper" implied previous context had been improper.

Legal changed it to "appropriate."

Treasury asked whether appropriate context came in cheaper than proper context.

Nobody had the courage to answer.

## ACT TWO

By mid-morning, departments were ordered to submit contextual material for current priorities.

Transport sent a map with no labels.

Health sent a timeline that began with "lessons learned" and ended with "further lessons anticipated."

Education sent a photograph of a classroom whiteboard reading CONTEXT TO FOLLOW.

The Home Office sent a black folder marked OPERATIONAL CONTEXT.

Legal asked whether operational context could be summarized.

The Home Office said yes, but not operationally.

Treasury submitted a single paragraph explaining that the wider fiscal context remained wider than the fiscal envelope.

Communications called it bleak but usable.

Digital built a dashboard where each policy appeared on a shelf beside its context.

The first item was labelled:

DECISION: TO ANNOUNCE A REVIEW.

CONTEXT: THERE HAS BEEN INTEREST.

The Prime Minister asked who was interested.

Policy said stakeholders.

The Prime Minister asked which stakeholders.

Policy said interested ones.

The Cabinet Secretary praised the circularity as efficient.

The junior adviser asked whether the shelf was becoming a place to hide uncertainty.

Communications said no, it was a place to display confidence at a respectful distance from uncertainty.

## ACT THREE

At 12:05, the Prime Minister requested a rehearsal.

Communications arranged a lectern beside a narrow bookcase labelled NATIONAL CONTEXT SHELF.

On the top shelf sat three folders, a mug, and a laminated card reading WIDER PICTURE.

Facilities asked whether the shelf was load-bearing.

Treasury asked whether the mug was reusable.

Legal asked whether "wider picture" implied a narrower picture existed and had been withheld.

Communications turned the card around.

The Prime Minister began: "Today, we are giving the country the context it deserves."

Policy nodded.

Communications smiled.

Legal underlined "deserves."

Treasury wrote "possible entitlement?"

The Prime Minister tried again: "Today, we are giving the country the context it requires."

Legal said "requires" was worse.

The Prime Minister tried: "Today, we are giving the country context."

Communications said it sounded like a threat.

Digital refreshed the dashboard.

The shelf now showed a warning:

CONTEXT EXCEEDS ANNOUNCEMENT.

Policy said that was healthy.

Communications said it was dangerous.

The Cabinet Secretary said it depended whether the context had been approved.

The junior adviser asked whether the decision should be changed to fit the context.

The room looked at them with the quiet concern normally reserved for someone opening a window in a submarine.

## CLIMAX

The launch began at 15:00.

The Prime Minister announced a new approach to transparent decision-making with context at its heart.

Communications had briefed reporters that the shelf would show how choices were rooted in evidence, engagement, and national priorities.

Policy had briefed that the shelf was a framework.

Legal had briefed that the shelf was not a promise, platform, archive, record, guarantee, or furnishing.

Treasury had briefed nothing, which it described as fiscally responsible.

A reporter asked what decision was being put into context today.

The Prime Minister gestured to the shelf.

Communications signalled "families."

Policy signalled "wider picture."

Legal signalled "subject to context."

The Prime Minister said: "Families understand that the wider picture is best seen in context."

The reporter asked what the wider picture showed.

Digital's dashboard attempted to help and projected the live shelf behind the lectern.

It displayed:

ANNOUNCEMENT: CONTEXT SHELF.

CONTEXT: NEED FOR CONTEXT SHELF ARISING FROM LACK OF CONTEXT SHELF.

STATUS: SELF-SUPPORTING.

For a moment, nobody moved.

The Cabinet Secretary said self-supporting sounded structurally sound.

Treasury asked whether it meant they could cancel maintenance.

Communications called it elegant.

The junior adviser said it was a cupboard arguing for its own existence.

The Prime Minister called it a decisive step toward a more contextual future.

## TAG

By evening, departments had been asked to contextualise all previous context by close of play.

Digital added a second shelf for shelf-related context.

Legal labelled it provisional.
