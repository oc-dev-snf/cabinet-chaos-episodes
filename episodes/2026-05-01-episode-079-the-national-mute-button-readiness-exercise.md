# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 079
**Title:** The National Mute Button Readiness Exercise
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE BRIEFING SUITE, 06:59. A wall of screens. Twelve remote attendees. Six are muted. Two are not, but should be.)

Morning briefing card:

**INTERDEPARTMENTAL CONTINUITY WEEK — DAY 11**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Mute Button Readiness Exercise"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why can I hear a blender, a dog, and what sounds like a leaf blower in fiscal policy?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** We launched a resilience drill for hybrid meetings.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** The new conferencing policy made mute status "user-configurable by context." Nobody agrees what the context is.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We have digitized confusion.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — SIGNAL, NOISE, AND GOVERNANCE

08:11. Coordination room. Slide title: **"AUDIO DISCIPLINE PERFORMANCE GAP"**.

**PM:** This morning's security update was delayed because three teams were trying to identify whose kettle was whistling.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** We now have a "sorry, you go" loop that consumed eight minutes and one policy decision.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Clips are circulating of officials solemnly nodding while a ringtone plays uninterrupted.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We are a major government, not a community podcast with bandwidth issues.

Root-cause summary identifies four breakdowns:

1. No standard mute defaults at meeting start
2. Chairing rules varied by department
3. No fallback host when lead moderator dropped
4. Device checks were optional and usually skipped

**PM:** I want a process where the loudest thing in the room is the policy disagreement.

## ACT TWO — THE READINESS EXERCISE

10:42. Rapid task cell formed: digital service, operations, security, HR, and communications.

Slide title: **"CLARITY IS A CONTROL"**

**DIGITAL LEAD:** All meetings begin muted by default, with managed unmute for speakers.

**SECURITY LEAD:** Sensitive briefings move to named-host controls with backup host assignment.

**HR LEAD:** Chair script becomes mandatory: roll call, speaking order, mute check, decision recap.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Five-minute pre-brief tech check for all sessions above ten attendees.

Three immediate controls approved:

- **Default mute protocol** with visible speaker queue
- **Backup host handoff** when primary host disconnects
- **Pre-brief device checklist** (mic, headset, notifications, network)

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Progress. We have replaced vibes with procedure.

## ACT THREE — LIVE STRESS TEST

12:06. Simulated cross-government call with 140 participants across 17 teams.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Injecting two failures: host dropout at minute six, accidental unmute cluster at minute nine.

12:12. Host drops.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Backup host active in 14 seconds. Agenda continues.

12:15. Unmute cluster detected.

**CHAIR:** Non-speakers returned to mute. Queue restored.

12:24. Decision points completed on schedule.

Post-test actions approved:

- Daily 08:30 continuity call using the new protocol
- Weekly moderation drills for private offices
- Unified quick-guide card on every conference room desk
- 30-day audit: interruption rate, meeting overrun, decision completion

**PM:** Reliability is boring, and boring is exactly what we need.

## ACT FOUR — COMMS WITHOUT THE CONFETTI

16:38. Press line cleared: **"GOVERNMENT ADOPTS HYBRID MEETING RELIABILITY STANDARD"**.

Statement emphasizes:

- Consistent start-of-call controls
- Clear speaking and moderation rules
- Backup continuity when hosts fail
- Basic device hygiene before major meetings
- Measured outcomes published internally

By 18:03 (pilot indicators):

- Non-agenda interruption time down 52%
- Meetings ending on time up 33%
- Chair handoff delays down 71%
- Decision items closed in-session up 26%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We are announcing functioning conference calls and resisting the urge to call it transformation.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If nobody tweets about it, we'll know it worked.

## CLIMAX — THE CORRIDOR QUESTIONS

18:22. Reporters outside the media room.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this not all a bit trivial?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Trivial failures repeated at scale become strategic delay.

**REPORTER:** Critics say you're over-standardizing.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Standards are what let people focus on substance instead of wrestling settings.

**REPORTER:** Was today's chaos avoidable?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Today's chaos was rehearsed, observed, and reduced. That's the point of an exercise.
