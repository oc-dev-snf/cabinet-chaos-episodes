# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 061
**Title:** The Corridor Metrics Pilgrimage
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 06:55. A giant dashboard glows on the wall. Every metric is green except one: **PUBLIC PATIENCE — AMBER, BLINKING**.)

Emergency banner:

**PERFORMANCE VISIBILITY SPRINT — ACTIVE**  
**Scenario:** *"The Corridor Metrics Pilgrimage"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why does the dashboard say we are "112% on schedule"?

**DATA LEAD:** We changed the denominator to reflect ambition.

**AIDE:** The PM wants a corridor walk-through in ten minutes. Every stop must show measurable progress.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We have thirty-seven slides and no completed policy.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Perfect. We'll present the graph and hope nobody asks what it measures.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — KPI THEATRE

War room, 07:40. Whiteboard heading: **IF IT MOVES, METRIC IT**

**PM:** I want clear evidence the plan is working.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** We launched the Visibility Sprint.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Is that different from doing the work?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It's adjacent to doing the work, with better fonts.

By 09:20, the sprint produces side effects:

1. Teams pause delivery to update status trackers every twelve minutes
2. A new metric appears: **"Meetings About Throughput"**
3. Regional offices receive three conflicting templates titled FINAL
4. A ministerial briefing includes a pie chart labeled "confidence" with no source
5. Journalists post photos of officials posing beside screensavers

**PM:** Why has output dropped?

**DATA LEAD:** Because we are now measuring output in real time.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We have built a stopwatch that requires six operators.

## ACT TWO — THE DEFINITION CRISIS

Metrics room, 10:33. Stickies everywhere. One reads: **"What counts as done?"**

**DATA LEAD:** We need one definition of "completed action."

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Legal says it's complete when drafted.

**TREASURY REP:** Finance says complete when funded.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Press says complete when announced.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Reality says complete when people notice a difference.

They test wording options for the noon update:

**DRAFT V1:** "Delivery momentum remains strongly positive."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Means nothing.

**DRAFT V2:** "We confused activity with progress this morning."

**CHIEF WHIP:** Accurate. Mildly career-limiting.

**DRAFT V3:** "We are simplifying performance reporting to focus on outcomes delivered, not meetings held."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** V3. Keep it boring. Boring is trustworthy.

## ACT THREE — THE CORRIDOR WALK

Cabinet corridor, 13:05. PM tour begins. Departments line up with laminated boards.

**PM:** Show me one number that matters.

**HEALTH OFFICIAL:** "Checklist completion rate" is up 40%.

**PM:** Are services faster?

**HEALTH OFFICIAL:** We don't have that metric yet.

Next stop.

**TRANSPORT OFFICIAL:** "Cross-team synchronization events" doubled.

**PM:** Are trains on time?

**TRANSPORT OFFICIAL:** We can get back to you with a synchronization subgroup.

Next stop. Peter-like minister steps in.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Prime Minister, we can keep touring dashboards, or we can lock three outcomes and publish weekly proof against each.

**PM:** Why wasn't that the plan this morning?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because we briefly mistook visibility for delivery.

AIDE hands over a panicked note:

"Social trend: #SpreadsheetGovernment"

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Of course it is.

## ACT FOUR — THREE NUMBERS, NO ESCAPE ROUTES

Reset meeting, 15:22. New board title: **OUTCOMES OR IT DOESN'T COUNT**

Agreed framework:

- Three public outcome metrics per department, published weekly
- One shared definition of "complete" tied to real-world effect
- Stop reporting internal process metrics to external audiences
- Dashboard freeze: no new KPIs without ministerial sign-off
- Friday audit: remove any metric nobody can explain in one sentence

By 18:00:

- Departments cut average reporting pack from 42 pages to 9
- Press office issues a single plain-language note
- Internal teams resume paused work queues

**PM:** Can we call this Phase Two Reform?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We can call it counting the right things at last.

## CLIMAX — THE BRIEFING

Press room, 19:11.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**  
Today we reviewed how performance data is used across government.

This morning, too much attention went to internal activity measures and not enough to public outcomes. That is now corrected.

From this week, each department will publish a short set of outcome metrics with clear definitions and regular updates. If a number cannot be explained plainly, it will not be used to claim progress.

**REPORTER:** Is this an admission the dashboard strategy failed?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It is an admission that dashboards are tools, not achievements.

**REPORTER:** Who approved the earlier framework?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Collective urgency approved it. Collective common sense replaced it.

## TAG

22:18. Empty corridor. A cleaner wheels past a neglected display screen still showing:

**"MEETINGS ABOUT THROUGHPUT: 287"**

Peter-like minister takes a marker and writes underneath:

**"OUTCOMES DELIVERED: ASK TOMORROW."**

The cleaner nods.

**CLEANER:** Best metric I've seen all day.

Phone buzzes with evening headline:

**"Government Cuts KPI Clutter, Promises Outcome-First Reporting."**

Peter-like minister pockets the marker.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** A rare administrative miracle: fewer numbers, more meaning.

**END**
