# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 150
**Title:** The National Sensible-Sounding Plan
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the Prime Minister announced that the government would shortly publish a Sensible-Sounding Plan.

The plan did not yet exist, but the title had tested extremely well.

Communications said "sensible" reassured people who wanted change without the unpleasantness of alteration.

Policy said "sounding" created useful distance from measurable intent.

Legal said "plan" should be treated as a communications device unless and until it acquired verbs.

Treasury asked whether sensible-sounding was cheaper than sensible.

The Cabinet Secretary convened an emergency meeting to determine what kind of plan could safely sound sensible by lunchtime.

Digital asked whether it needed a dashboard.

Communications said everything needed a dashboard, but this one should not look like it was measuring anything.

The junior adviser asked whether the public might eventually expect the plan to do something.

The room paused, as if someone had opened a window in a submarine.

The Prime Minister said the public wanted confidence.

Policy said confidence could be delivered through sequencing.

Legal said sequencing should not imply a sequence.

Treasury asked whether confidence could be piloted in three regions using existing chairs.

## ACT ONE

By 08:45, the Sensible-Sounding Plan had a launch grid, a holding line, and no contents.

Communications proposed three pillars: stability, fairness, and practical delivery.

Policy asked what they meant.

Communications said that was the advantage of pillars.

Legal said stability might imply the current situation was unstable.

Treasury said fairness often became expensive when translated into forms.

Digital suggested replacing practical delivery with visible delivery, because visible delivery could be represented by a progress bar.

The Cabinet Secretary wrote "VISIBLE PRACTICALITY" on the whiteboard.

Nobody objected, which made it official enough to worry everyone.

The Prime Minister rehearsed: "This government has listened, learned, and landed a sensible plan."

Communications liked the rhythm.

Policy disliked "landed" because there was no landing site.

Legal disliked "learned" because it implied previous uncertainty.

Treasury disliked "listened" because consultation responses might need storing.

The junior adviser suggested: "We have noticed things and arranged words around them."

Communications said it was too honest for breakfast.

## ACT TWO

At 10:30, departments were asked to contribute one sensible-sounding measure each.

Transport offered a review of journey confidence.

Health offered a framework for patient-facing clarity about future frameworks.

Education offered a national conversation about improving conversations.

The Home Office offered a robust pathway with no disclosed location.

Digital offered a portal where citizens could track whether tracking was available in their area.

Treasury offered a commitment to examine the affordability of commitment.

Policy combined the submissions into a document called "A Practical Route to Better Outcomes."

Legal removed "route" because routes led somewhere.

Communications removed "better" because better invited comparison.

Treasury removed "outcomes" because outcomes had a habit of wanting funding.

The document was retitled "A Practical Approach."

Legal asked whether approach implied proximity.

The Cabinet Secretary suggested "A Sensible-Sounding Approach."

Communications said it was on brand.

The junior adviser asked whether the plan had now become an approach to sounding like a plan.

The Prime Minister said that was precisely the kind of agility voters expected from modern government.

## ACT THREE

At 12:00, the launch team discovered that the briefing deck contained a slide headed "WHAT WILL CHANGE?"

The room treated it as a hostile document.

Communications proposed replacing it with "WHAT PEOPLE WILL HEAR."

Policy said hearing was not delivery.

Communications said it was the first stage of perceived delivery.

Digital added a listening metric.

Legal asked whether people could reasonably rely on hearing.

Treasury asked whether listening metrics required specialist headphones.

The Prime Minister tried a new line: "This plan will make government feel closer to people's everyday lives."

Legal said closer created a spatial promise.

Policy said everyday lives were operationally broad.

Communications suggested "This plan reflects the texture of daily concern."

The Cabinet Secretary said texture sounded thoughtful.

Treasury asked if texture had a unit cost.

The junior adviser asked whether anyone outside the building would understand it.

Communications said understanding was only one route to confidence.

Legal asked for route to be struck from the minutes.

## CLIMAX

The press conference began at 15:00.

Behind the Prime Minister, the lectern sign read:

A SENSIBLE-SOUNDING PLAN FOR THE COUNTRY.

Communications had argued that the hyphen made it look designed.

Policy had argued that the hyphen connected the two least dangerous words.

Legal had approved the hyphen as non-binding.

Treasury had asked whether the hyphen was reusable.

A reporter asked what the plan would actually change.

The Prime Minister said: "It will change the way government approaches the things people care about."

The reporter asked which things.

Communications signalled "everyday."

Policy signalled "priorities."

Legal signalled "not exhaustive."

The Prime Minister said: "Everyday priorities, without exhausting the list."

Another reporter asked when the plan would be published.

Digital put the dashboard on screen.

It displayed:

PLAN STATUS: SENSIBLE-SOUNDING.

CONTENT STATUS: IN APPROACH.

PUBLICATION WINDOW: CONFIDENCE-DEPENDENT.

CHANGE LEVEL: UNDER DESCRIPTION.

The room went quiet.

The Cabinet Secretary said the dashboard showed healthy movement through the pre-delivery phase.

Treasury asked whether under description counted as capital formation.

The junior adviser said the government had built a signpost, held a press conference next to it, and refused to say whether there was a road.

Communications called it a disciplined exercise in directional reassurance.

The Prime Minister said the country would welcome a plan that met the moment without rushing the moment into specifics.

## TAG

By evening, departments had been instructed to identify all existing policies that could be made to sound more sensible by Monday.

Legal circulated guidance on the safe use of adjectives.

Digital added a hyphen readiness tracker.
