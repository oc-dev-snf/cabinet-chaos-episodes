# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 043
**Title:** The National Inbox Zero Emergency
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET BRIEFING ROOM. A giant screen reads: “URGENT: UNREAD EMAILS (17,842)”.)

**CALLUM:** Why does the national dashboard look like my aunt’s promotions folder?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Overnight, every department marked every other department “just circling back.”

**POLICY LEAD:** We now have twelve parallel threads titled “Quick one.” None are quick. One is about bins. One is about submarines. Same subject line.

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** I’ve prepared a modern response.

**CALLUM:** Please tell me it’s not another app.

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** It’s called *Reply-All Lite*.

**CALLUM:** That sounds like *Reply-All*, but with less accountability.

(An aide enters with a printed email chain, held like contaminated evidence.)

**AIDE:** This one started Tuesday. It’s now 143 messages and no decision.

**CALLUM:** On what?

**AIDE:** Whether to schedule a meeting to define decision criteria.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

Emergency taskforce assembled: Policy, Comms, Ops, and one person from IT who has already given up spiritually.

**CALLUM:** Right. New rule: no email longer than six lines.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Bold. Half the government writes greetings like Victorian novels.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** Also we have a tactical issue: people are using “per my last email” as a declaration of war.

**POLICY LEAD:** We need triage categories.

They build a whiteboard:
- **Green:** Informational, ignorable, possibly sent to wrong list.
- **Amber:** Action needed, unclear owner, four attachments named “Final_v7_realfinal”.
- **Red:** “Can we jump on a quick call?” sent at 16:58 Friday.

**IT ADVISER:** I can set up filters.

**CALLUM:** Yes. Prioritize anything containing “urgent,” “media query,” or “minister copied in by accident.”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** That’s everything.

## ACT TWO

A pilot begins in one department. For six glorious minutes, inbox volume drops.

Then someone discovers “high importance” flags.

Then someone else discovers you can flag calendar invites.

Then a senior adviser sends an all-staff email titled: “Please don’t overuse urgent.” marked urgent.

**CALLUM:** We are fighting fire with lighter fluid and meeting notes.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** Suggest we appoint one accountable decision-maker per thread.

**POLICY LEAD:** Radical.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Unsubscribe me from this revolution.

Meanwhile, the press office receives a leaked screenshot of the national task board, including a column labeled “Things We Meant To Answer Last Week.”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** I can handle this. We frame it as transparency.

**CALLUM:** Is it transparency if it happened by mistake?

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** In modern governance, that’s most transparency.

## ACT THREE

Cabinet reconvenes. The numbers are better, but fragile.

Unread emails: down to 9,204.

Meeting invites: up 380%.

Decision memos: still hiding in draft folders across the kingdom.

**CALLUM:** We need structural reform, not motivational posters.

**IT ADVISER:** Then adopt the protocol we tested years ago.

**CALLUM:** The one everyone ignored?

**IT ADVISER:** The very same. Clear owners, hard deadlines, and if no decision in 48 hours, auto-escalation.

(Room goes quiet like someone proposed taxes on vibes.)

**POLICY LEAD:** It could work.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** It will upset people.

**CALLUM:** Excellent. That’s how we know it’s real policy.

## CLIMAX

Press briefing. Clean podium. Slightly haunted faces.

**CALLUM (to cameras):** Today we launched a practical reform package to reduce administrative noise and speed up decision-making.

Reporter shouts:

**REPORTER:** Is it true ministers were CC’d on a lunch order chain for three days?

**CALLUM:** No comment on menu logistics.

Another reporter:

**REPORTER 2:** Are you declaring war on email?

**CALLUM:** No. We are pursuing peaceful coexistence with consequences.

Back inside, the team watches inboxes update in near real time.

Unread count: 8,999.

Everyone applauds like they just landed a spacecraft.

## TAG

Late evening. Office mostly empty.

Callum opens laptop to one new message:

**Subject:** Quick one

**From:** Callum (Scheduled send, yesterday)

**Body:** “Can we set a time to discuss reducing internal email load?”

He stares at it, sighs, and clicks *Archive*.

Two seconds later, another email arrives.

**Subject:** Re: Quick one

**From:** Everyone

Callum closes laptop slowly.

**CALLUM:** We’re not a government. We’re an auto-reply with a flag.

**END**
