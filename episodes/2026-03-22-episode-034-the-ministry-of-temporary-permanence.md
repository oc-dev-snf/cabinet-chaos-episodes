# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 034  
**Title:** The Ministry of Temporary Permanence  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## Premise
After criticism that major policies change every six weeks, the government launches a "Stability Package" requiring every urgent decision to be classed as "temporary" for an initial twelve-month bedding-in period. Nothing is permanent, except the temporary arrangements.

## COLD OPEN – INT. CABINET OFFICE BRIEFING ROOM – 7:02 AM

A digital slide flickers:

**"NATIONAL STABILITY PACKAGE: PHASE ONE (INTERIM FINAL)"**

Underneath, in smaller font:

**"Version 14 — Pending Last Minor Rewrite"**

**POLICY DIRECTOR:**
Are we calling this final?

**SPECIAL ADVISER:**
Final-temporary.

**POLICY DIRECTOR:**
Meaning?

**SPECIAL ADVISER:**
It is fully agreed for now, subject to complete reconsideration.

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
Media line says this ends constant policy churn.

**POLICY DIRECTOR:**
By introducing structured churn.

---

## ACT ONE – INT. STRATEGY UNIT – 8:11 AM

A wall chart reads:

**"DECISION LIFESPAN FRAMEWORK"**

Columns:
1. **Immediate Temporary**
2. **Extended Temporary**
3. **Temporary Until Long-Term Review of Temporary Status**

**OPERATIONS LEAD:**
Each policy starts temporary to prove durability.

**DIRECTOR GENERAL:**
How long does temporary last?

**OPERATIONS LEAD:**
Twelve months, automatically renewable in the interests of stability.

**DIRECTOR GENERAL:**
So... permanent.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:**
No. Permanence would imply confidence.

### SCENE 2 – INT. MINISTERIAL OFFICE – 8:49 AM

A minister rehearses with two briefing cards.

Card one: **"GOVERNMENT IS STEADFAST."**  
Card two: **"ALL ARRANGEMENTS SUBJECT TO ACTIVE REVIEW."**

**MINISTER:**
Do these lines conflict?

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Only if read together.

**MINISTER:**
What if someone asks whether this policy will still exist next year?

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Say, "It will remain in force while we assess long-term options for its future continuity."

**MINISTER:**
That sounds like a voicemail from uncertainty.

---

## ACT TWO – INT. CROSS-DEPARTMENT IMPLEMENTATION CALL – 9:34 AM

Twenty-eight officials on screen. Six different templates open. Nobody has the same version.

**CHAIR:**
Agenda item one: classification protocol for urgent temporary permanency measures.

**TREASURY REP:**
Funding can be approved for temporary measures, but not recurring temporary measures.

**CABINET REP:**
These are non-recurring temporary measures renewed on a recurring basis.

**TREASURY REP:**
That is a recurring measure with extra vowels.

**SERVICE DELIVERY REP:**
Frontline teams need to know whether to build this into long-term systems.

**CHAIR:**
Official answer: yes, but in a way that signals reversibility.

Long silence.

**SERVICE DELIVERY REP:**
So we hard-code uncertainty.

### SCENE 2 – INT. COMMS WAR ROOM – 10:18 AM

A giant monitor displays draft headlines to pre-bunk:

- **"U-TURN GOVERNMENT U-TURNS ON U-TURNS"**
- **"MINISTERS ANNOUNCE LASTING TEMPORARY PLAN"**
- **"POLICY NOW ON PROBATION"**

**HEAD OF COMMS:**
We need one phrase that sounds firm and flexible.

**SENIOR ADVISER:**
"Durably agile."

**POLICY DIRECTOR:**
That sounds like a yoga mat.

**HEAD OF COMMS:**
Fine. "Stable by design, adaptive by necessity."

**SENIOR ADVISER:**
Still sounds like we’re apologising in advance.

---

## ACT THREE – INT. PRESS BRIEFING ROOM – 11:12 AM

Backdrop reads:

**"CERTAINTY THROUGH MANAGED TRANSITION"**

**REPORTER 1:**
Is this policy permanent?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
It is a temporary policy with long-term intent.

**REPORTER 2:**
Will it change?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Only where necessary to preserve consistency.

**REPORTER 3:**
How often is necessary?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
On a predictable ad hoc basis.

Pens stop. Typing accelerates.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PM’S OFFICE – 11:46 AM

A one-page dashboard lands on the desk:

> **STABILITY PACKAGE – DAY ONE STATUS**  
> Policies relabelled as temporary: 42  
> Policies operationally treated as permanent: 39  
> Policies understood by delivery teams: 7  
> Public confidence metric: "monitoring"

**PM:**
Why does this read like we invented uncertainty with branding?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:**
Because we tried to stop chaos without choosing a direction.

**PM:**
Can we pick one?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:**
That would be a permanent move.

**PM:**
Then make it temporary for a week and permanent by Friday.

---

## CLIMAX – INT. CABINET ROOM – 12:28 PM

Emergency alignment meeting. Whiteboard now says:

**"RULE OF THREE"**

1. **Declare intent clearly**  
2. **Set review date publicly**  
3. **Limit revisions to defined triggers**

**CABINET SECRETARY:**
No more rolling ambiguity. Every temporary measure gets one owner, one review date, one decision point.

**TREASURY REP:**
If review date passes with no action?

**CABINET SECRETARY:**
Default is continuation for six months, with published rationale.

**SERVICE DELIVERY REP:**
That at least lets people build systems once.

**PM:**
Good. We can be flexible, but on purpose.

---

## TAG – INT. CABINET OFFICE CORRIDOR – 6:04 PM

A fresh poster appears:

**"TEMPORARY DOES NOT MEAN VAGUE."**

Someone has added a sticky note:

**"Owner: Named. Review: Dated. Changes: Explain them."**

A second sticky note appears beneath:

**"This message is temporary until further notice."**

A third sticky, in block capitals:

**"FURTHER NOTICE: TOMORROW 09:00."**

For once, everyone writes it in their calendar.

**END**
