# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 048
**Title:** The National Queue Modernisation Mission
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET DELIVERY ROOM. A screen reads: “PUBLIC SERVICE WAIT TIMES: AMBER, RED, VERY RED.” A whiteboard says: “VISIBLE PROGRESS BY LUNCH.”)

**CALLUM:** Why is every service dashboard flashing like a Christmas tree?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Because people are waiting too long for appointments, permits, and callbacks.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Morning shows are running split screens of queues and disappointed faces.

**EFFICIENCY MINISTER:** We can announce the National Queue Modernisation Mission.

**CALLUM:** Is that where we shorten queues?

**EFFICIENCY MINISTER:** Eventually. First we classify them.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

A taskforce presents three pathways:

Option A: extend opening hours for six weeks and redeploy staff to backlog teams.
Option B: launch a Queue Experience App with live mood indicators.
Option C: appoint a Queue Czar and hold a summit.

**CALLUM:** A sounds useful. Why are B and C in 42-slide decks?

**POLICY DIRECTOR:** They have stronger stakeholder choreography.

**CALLUM:** Do they process one extra case today?

**POLICY DIRECTOR:** Not in phase one.

A quiet operations analyst hands over a note:

**“If departments run evening triage, simplify low-risk approvals, and call people when slots open, backlog drops this month.”**

The room calls it “interesting,” then debates app color themes.

## ACT TWO

Pilot ideas arrive quickly.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** The app can assign each queue a “trust temperature.”

**CALLUM:** Can it assign each queue an extra clerk?

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Not without procurement.

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** We can, however, simplify repeat forms for renewals and auto-approve straightforward cases.

**CALLUM:** When?

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** If legal signs off this afternoon, tomorrow.

Regional offices report the same bottleneck: too much time spent re-checking simple submissions.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Give teams a short list of what truly needs escalation.

**GOVERNANCE ADVISER:** We need safeguards.

**CALLUM:** Fine. How many?

**GOVERNANCE ADVISER:** One audit trail, one weekly exception report.

**CALLUM:** Excellent. That’s safeguards, not modern sculpture.

## ACT THREE

Press office proposes campaign lines:
- “Queueing for the Future”
- “Smarter Waiting Starts Today”
- “From Line to Liftoff”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We can launch with branded rope barriers.

**CALLUM:** Or we can reduce the need for rope barriers.

Callum writes the operational plan:

1. Six-week extended service hours in highest-backlog offices.
2. Fast-track low-risk renewals with clear criteria.
3. National callback protocol for cancelled slots.
4. Weekly published wait-time metrics by department.
5. One-page escalation rules for frontline teams.

**EFFICIENCY MINISTER:** That’s practical.

**CALLUM:** Don’t spread it around.

## CLIMAX

Noon statement:

**“Government is implementing immediate measures to cut waiting times: extended hours, faster low-risk processing, and transparent weekly reporting.”**

A reporter asks whether the Queue Modernisation Mission is mostly branding.

**CALLUM:** It was. Then we accidentally made it useful.

**REPORTER:** Is technology still part of the plan?

**CALLUM:** Yes. We’ll use software for scheduling, reminders, and triage. Mostly we’ll use people to help other people faster.

## TAG

(EVENING. Operations dashboard refreshes.)

**AIDE:** First-day results: average callback delay down 18% in pilot offices.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Public feedback is better. People enjoy being called back before retirement.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Are we still doing campaign posters?

**CALLUM:** Yes. Put the wait-time chart on them.

A calendar invite appears:

**Subject:** “International Forum on Transformative Queue Narratives.”

Callum stares at it, forwards it to “Later,” and turns back to the dashboard.

**END**
