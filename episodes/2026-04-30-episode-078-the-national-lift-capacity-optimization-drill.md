# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 078
**Title:** The National Lift Capacity Optimization Drill
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE ATRIUM, 06:58. Two lifts. One marked **"STAFF"**, one marked **"PRIORITY STAFF"**. Both are stuck on Floor 7.)

Morning briefing card:

**PUBLIC SERVICE DELIVERY READINESS WEEK — DAY 10**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Lift Capacity Optimization Drill"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is the entire senior team on the stairs before dawn?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Facilities launched a pilot to improve vertical movement efficiency.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** They introduced a booking app for lifts. It crashed under demand from the first twelve people trying to book the 07:00 slot.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Perfect. Nothing says operational readiness like cardio under duress.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — UPWARD MOBILITY, DOWNWARD MOMENTUM

08:14. Incident room. Slide title: **"VERTICAL TRANSPORT THROUGHPUT VARIANCE"**.

**PM:** We have a building of decision-makers stranded by floors.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Teams are arriving to meetings in three waves: early, breathless, and philosophically late.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Journalists have noticed a queue stretching past reception and are calling it "the staircase economy."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We cannot run national coordination on step-count targets.

Initial findings identify four failures:

1. Lift booking windows were too narrow for peak demand
2. Priority rules were unclear and inconsistently applied
3. No fallback process existed when the booking app failed
4. Facilities telemetry wasn't connected to operations planning

**PM:** I want a system where people reach meetings before the meetings become historical documents.

## ACT TWO — THE OPTIMIZATION DRILL

10:36. Cross-functional cell convenes: facilities, digital service, security, operations, and people leads.

Slide title: **"MOVEMENT IS INFRASTRUCTURE"**

**FACILITIES LEAD:** We return lifts to mixed mode during peaks: one for accessibility and urgent movement, one for general traffic.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** App moves from mandatory booking to optional forecasting. No one should need software permission to change floors.

**SECURITY LEAD:** Priority criteria become plain and published: accessibility, emergency response, time-critical duties.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** We stagger top-floor briefings by ten minutes and cap simultaneous starts.

Three immediate controls approved:

- **Peak-hour mixed lift mode** with visible floor marshals
- **Manual fallback protocol** triggered automatically if app latency exceeds threshold
- **Shared telemetry dashboard** for live queue length and wait times

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** At last, modernization that includes a Plan B and gravity.

## ACT THREE — LIVE BUILDING TEST

12:02. Controlled stress test begins with 180 participants across nine floors.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Test includes a simulated app outage at minute eight.

12:10. Outage triggers.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Manual mode active. Forecasting paused, movement continues.

12:17. Queue pressure rises on lower floors.

**FACILITIES LEAD:** Marshals reroute short-hop travelers to stairs, reserve lift cycles for long-haul and accessibility users.

12:28. Meeting attendance stabilizes across departments.

Post-test actions approved:

- Daily pre-08:00 queue calibration
- Weekly mixed-mode drill with facilities and security
- Standard signage pack for all major government buildings
- 21-day review of average wait, missed starts, and accessibility performance

**PM:** The goal is not heroic improvisation. The goal is ordinary reliability.

## ACT FOUR — COMMUNICATIONS, MINUS VICTORY LAP

16:41. Press office line issued: **"GOVERNMENT BUILDINGS ADOPT PRACTICAL FLOW STANDARD"**.

Statement highlights:

- Simpler lift operations during peak periods
- Transparent priority rules for fairness and access
- Automatic fallback when digital tools fail
- Performance metrics published internally each week
- Independent operational review after 21 days

By 18:05 (pilot metrics):

- Median peak wait time down 38%
- On-time attendance at first meetings up 29%
- Accessibility-priority delays down 44%
- App-related disruptions reduced to near zero during fallback windows

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We have announced functioning lifts without promising national transcendence.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If nobody notices, we may have achieved excellence.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:26. Reporters by the reception desk.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this really a matter for government attention?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Operational friction compounds. Minutes lost in buildings become hours lost in decisions.

**REPORTER:** Critics say this is micromanagement.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Good management is often microscopic right before it becomes visible performance.

**REPORTER:** Was the pilot a failure?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** The pilot exposed failure conditions early, then we fixed them. That's what pilots are for.

## TAG

20:12. New sign in atrium: **"STAIRS FOR CHOICE, LIFTS FOR FLOW, ACCESS FOR ALL"**.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (off-screen):** Tomorrow's risk register includes escalators, morale, and the sandwich queue.

**END**
