# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 060
**Title:** The Briefing Labyrinth
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 06:58. A quiet corridor. Then three phones ring at once. A printer ejects a page titled **"LINE TO TAKE — FINAL_v12_REAL_FINAL"**.)

Emergency banner:

**NATIONAL MESSAGE ALIGNMENT WINDOW — ACTIVE**  
**Scenario:** *"The Briefing Labyrinth"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why does this say "use plain language" in seven different fonts?

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Because six teams edited it overnight.

**AIDE:** There are three versions of the same sentence and one of them is in track changes with a poem.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** The helpline wants to know which script is live.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Tell them the truth: we are currently governed by competing adjectives.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — TOO MANY TRUTHS

War room, 07:30. Whiteboard heading: **ONE FACT, ONE SENTENCE, ONE OWNER**

**PM:** I need one explanation, not a literature festival.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We built a rapid-response message process.

**CHIEF WHIP:** And rapidly produced confusion.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We have created a maze where every exit is labeled "clarity" and none of them lead outside.

By 09:05, effects stack up:

1. Local offices receive Script A, then correction to Script B, then "ignore both" note
2. Interview spokespeople quote different timelines on live radio
3. Internal channel erupts over whether "issue" sounds weaker than "problem"
4. Journalists post side-by-side screenshots of contradictory lines
5. Trend appears: **#PickAScript**

**PM:** Freeze comms output now.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We can pause releases, but everyone already downloaded copies.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We have achieved decentralized inconsistency.

## ACT TWO — WORDS VS. MEANING

Message room, 10:18. Draft wall projected.

**DRAFT V1:** "A dynamic narrative recalibration cycle is currently underway."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** That sounds like we swallowed a consultancy.

**DRAFT V2:** "We issued mixed messages and wasted everyone’s morning."

**CHIEF WHIP:** Honest. Possibly too honest for daytime.

**DRAFT V3:** "We circulated conflicting guidance this morning. We have withdrawn duplicate scripts, assigned one accountable source, and will issue timed updates."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** V3. Keep it human. Keep it short.

Then legal sends suggested edits:

Replace "conflicting" with "variance-rich."  
Replace "withdrawn" with "temporarily deprioritized."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No one outside this building has ever said "variance-rich" and meant good news.

## ACT THREE — THE COMMITTEE PREP

Committee rehearsal, 13:02.

**CHAIR (speakerphone):** Minister, did your office lose control of public messaging discipline?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We lost consistency for several hours, yes. We have now centralized sign-off and timestamped each release.

**OPPOSITION MP:** Was the public misled?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** The core facts did not change. The wording did, too often and too quickly. That is still a failure.

**OPPOSITION MP:** Why did this happen?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because speed outran governance. Everyone could publish; no one clearly owned the final line.

AIDE slides over another note:

"Prefer phrase: temporary narrative divergence event."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I prefer reality.

## ACT FOUR — SMALL RULES, BIG RELIEF

Fix plan approved at 15:40:

- Single source channel for live scripts
- One duty editor per shift with explicit authority
- Mandatory timestamp + version ID on every public line
- Automatic archive of superseded statements
- Daily ten-minute rehearsal: "say it plainly" drill

By 18:10:

- Regional teams report matching scripts
- Broadcasters receive one unified correction note
- Press tone shifts from mockery to "messy but corrected"

**PM:** Can we call this communications modernization?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We can call it learning to finish a sentence before publishing it.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Evening bulletins need a closer.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Try: one message, one owner, no labyrinth.

## CLIMAX — THE BRIEFING

Press room, 19:03.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**  
This morning, multiple overlapping scripts were circulated across departments. That created avoidable confusion.

The underlying facts remained stable, but our wording did not. We have now withdrawn duplicate lines, established a single sign-off path, and attached timestamps to all updates.

Our standard is simple: the public should not need a flowchart to understand government communication.

**REPORTER:** Who is accountable?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I am accountable for the system. Named editors are accountable for each release. We will publish that structure tonight.

**REPORTER:** Is this spin control after a bad day?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It is control, yes — of process, not of facts.

## TAG

22:27. Quiet office kitchenette. Kettle hums.

An intern places a pilot tool on the counter:

**"AUTO-SYNONYM ENGINE — MAKES STATEMENTS SOUND MORE STRATEGIC"**

Demo output:

"Delay" becomes "temporal flexibility."  
"Error" becomes "alignment opportunity."

Peter-like minister writes on the box:

**"BAN FOR CIVILIAN SAFETY."**

Sticks it in a cupboard labeled **GADGETS THAT MADE THINGS WORSE**.

Phone buzzes with late headline:

**"Government Cuts Script Chaos With Single-Source Rule."**

He sips tea, nods.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Historic step forward: fewer metaphors, more nouns.

**END**
