# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 039
**Title:** The National Strategy Refresh Refresh
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

In the Cabinet briefing room, a giant slide reads:

**"NATIONAL STRATEGY REFRESH (REFRESH) — DELIVERING RENEWED RENEWAL"**

MINISTER: Today we launch a decisive update to our strategic update.

MALCOLM: You've announced a sequel to an announcement.

GLENN flips through the deck.

GLENN: Why is page one labeled "Version Final v7 FINAL2"?

MALCOLM: Because certainty is a journey, Glenn, and this lot missed their stop.

---

## ACT ONE

### 1) The urgency to look busy

After criticism that government priorities are "too broad to measure and too vague to miss," the center orders every department to submit a strategy refresh by end of week.

The brief says each refresh must include:
- a new mission statement
- no reduction in existing commitments
- at least three "visible wins" per quarter
- zero new spending

NICOLA: So: do more things, with fewer things, while promising everything.

MALCOLM: Exactly. It's policy karaoke. Same tune, new confidence.

### 2) The language arms race

Departments begin rewriting goals in increasingly heroic prose.

Examples include:
- "accelerate momentum velocity"
- "embed delivery culture into outcomes architecture"
- "unlock frontline synergies at pace"

GLENN: None of these mean anything.

MALCOLM: They mean everyone slept four hours and consulted a thesaurus with trust issues.

---

## ACT TWO

### 1) The consultation that consults itself

To appear collaborative, officials launch a one-day cross-government workshop called **"Listening at Speed."**

Attendees are divided into tables named:
- Ambition
- Delivery
- Credibility
- Other

By lunchtime, each table has produced the same recommendation: pick fewer priorities and actually finish them.

A senior adviser marks this as "insufficiently transformational."

NICOLA: We asked for input, got clear input, then rejected it for being clear.

MALCOLM: Democracy's cousin: participation theatre.

### 2) The tracker spiral

A new tracker appears: **"REFRESH READINESS RAG+"**.

It scores departments on:
- number of strategic verbs
- visual clarity of roadmaps
- confidence tone in executive summaries

Red departments panic and submit longer PDFs.
Amber departments submit shorter PDFs with brighter colors.
Green departments quietly reuse last year's plan and rename the file.

GLENN: Are we measuring progress or formatting?

MALCOLM: In Westminster, formatting is progress with a lanyard.

### 3) The ministerial rehearsal

Before a media round, the minister practices lines:

- "This is not a reset; it's a refinement."
- "Not a U-turn, a precision bend."
- "Not delay, deliberate sequencing."

NICOLA: Could we just say "we overcomplicated it and fixed it"?

MALCOLM: Absolutely not. Honesty must be piloted in a controlled environment.

---

## ACT THREE

### 1) The accidental breakthrough

At 8:40 p.m., a tired private office team combines all submissions into one document.

They remove duplicates, buzzwords, and decorative diagrams.

What remains fits on two pages:
- five national priorities
- one lead minister each
- monthly milestones
- a short list of what will stop

GLENN: This is... good.

NICOLA: It's good because no one had energy left to make it complicated.

MALCOLM: Exhaustion: the civil service innovation lab.

### 2) The public launch

At the next day's press conference, the minister unveils the condensed plan as a bold new operating model.

Reporters ask why previous versions were 120 pages.

MINISTER: We have moved from depth to focus.

MALCOLM (under his breath): Translation: we found the delete key.

---

## CLIMAX

Three weeks later, something unusual happens.

Departments stop rewriting mission statements every Friday.

Directors ask "what finished this month?" instead of "who owns the narrative?"

Cabinet meetings get shorter because decisions are attached to dates, names, and actual deliverables.

The system is still messy, still political, still noisy — but slightly less addicted to reinvention.

For once, a "refresh" means maintaining momentum, not restarting from slide one.

MALCOLM: Miracles do happen. We've replaced strategic improv with a to-do list.

## TAG

to:

The office pauses in respectful silence.

MALCOLM nods solemnly.

"Put that in the constitutional archive."
