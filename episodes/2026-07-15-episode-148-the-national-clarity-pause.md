# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 148
**Title:** The National Clarity Pause
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the government announced it would pause for clarity.

Nobody knew what had been paused.

Communications said that was premature.

Policy said the pause applied to a decision that had not yet been announced, though several people had reacted to it in principle.

Legal said a pause was safest when attached to something non-specific.

Treasury asked whether a pause had a cost centre.

The Prime Minister entered carrying a folder marked DECISION: HOLD.

The Cabinet Secretary explained that the National Clarity Pause would demonstrate responsiveness without the inconvenience of responding.

The junior adviser asked whether clarity usually required stopping everything first.

Communications said clarity required a controlled absence of noise.

At that exact moment, three departments emailed different lines to the press.

One said the policy was paused.

One said the process was paused.

One said the pause itself was proceeding at pace.

The Prime Minister asked which version was true.

The Cabinet Secretary said truth was exactly what the pause was designed to establish.

## ACT ONE

By 08:30, a cross-government pause grid had been assembled.

It had columns for paused, not paused, paused pending review, and paused for presentational purposes.

Digital added a filter marked "clarity confidence."

It immediately returned no results.

Communications wanted the announcement to sound calm.

Policy wanted it to sound evidence-led.

Legal wanted it to avoid suggesting evidence had previously been walking around unattended.

Treasury wanted it to be free.

The Prime Minister tried the line: "We are taking a moment to listen."

Communications liked it.

Policy liked it.

Legal asked who was being listened to.

The Prime Minister said the country.

Legal asked whether the country had been notified.

Communications changed the line to: "We are taking a moment to reflect."

Treasury asked whether reflection could be done in existing mirrors.

The Cabinet Secretary said the important thing was to project grip.

The junior adviser asked whether announcing a pause suggested the grip had slipped.

Communications said only if people understood announcements.

## ACT TWO

At 10:00, departments began submitting items for the pause grid.

Transport paused a consultation on whether future consultations should have fewer questions.

Health paused a pilot designed to accelerate the pausing of pilots.

Education paused a guidance note because the guidance was still finding itself.

The Home Office submitted a paragraph stating that operational activity continued except where it did not.

Legal called it robust.

Policy called it textured.

Communications called it Wednesday.

Treasury submitted a blank cell and said it represented a saving.

The Prime Minister asked whether the public would notice the pause.

Communications said the public would notice the seriousness.

The Cabinet Secretary said noticing the pause itself would be suboptimal.

Digital demonstrated the dashboard.

Each policy could be dragged into a holding area labelled CLARITY.

The first policy vanished.

Digital said that was a visual metaphor.

Legal said it was discoverable.

Treasury asked whether vanished policies still needed funding.

The junior adviser asked whether the whole government was now a waiting room with a crest.

Nobody wrote that down, which meant it was usable later.

## ACT THREE

At 12:15, the Prime Minister rehearsed the statement.

Behind the lectern stood a banner reading:

CLARITY THROUGH PAUSE.

Communications said the phrase had tested well with people who had not been told what it meant.

Policy said the pause allowed space for stakeholder engagement.

Legal said stakeholders should not be promised space unless there was an allocation method.

Treasury asked whether stakeholders could bring their own space.

The Prime Minister began: "Today, we are pausing to provide clarity."

The Cabinet Secretary winced.

Communications said it sounded as if clarity was late.

The Prime Minister tried again: "Today, we are creating the conditions for clarity."

Legal said conditions could imply liability if clarity failed to occur.

The Prime Minister tried: "Today, we are ensuring clarity has room to emerge."

Policy loved it.

Communications loved it.

Treasury asked whether emerging clarity was taxable.

Digital refreshed the dashboard.

The main status changed from PAUSED FOR CLARITY to CLARITY AWAITING PAUSE.

The Cabinet Secretary said this was just sequencing.

The junior adviser said it looked like the pause was waiting for permission from itself.

Communications asked them to stop producing usable metaphors.

## CLIMAX

The press conference began at 15:00.

The Prime Minister announced a calm, responsible, evidence-led clarity pause.

Communications had briefed that the pause showed discipline.

Policy had briefed that the pause showed learning.

Legal had briefed that the pause was not an admission, delay, reversal, retreat, or corridor.

Treasury had briefed that no new money had been harmed in the making of the pause.

A reporter asked what had changed since yesterday.

The Prime Minister said the government had listened carefully.

The reporter asked to whom.

The Prime Minister looked at Communications.

Communications signalled "families."

Policy signalled "stakeholders."

Legal signalled "not specifically."

The Prime Minister said: "Families and stakeholders have been clear that they want clarity."

The reporter asked what clarity they had asked for.

Digital attempted to help by projecting the live pause grid behind the lectern.

It displayed:

ITEM: CLARITY PAUSE.

STATUS: PAUSED.

REASON: CLARITY.

NEXT STEP: CLARIFY PAUSE.

The room went quiet.

The Cabinet Secretary said the loop showed internal consistency.

Treasury asked whether internal consistency could replace external delivery.

Communications called it a disciplined holding position.

The junior adviser said it was a government pressing the snooze button on its own explanation.

The Prime Minister called it a mature reset.

## TAG

By evening, departments had been instructed to pause all references to the pause until the pause could be clarified.

Digital added a button marked RESUME.

Legal disabled it pending advice.
