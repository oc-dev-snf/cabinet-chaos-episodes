# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 058
**Title:** The National Hold Music Review
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 06:58. Three phones ring continuously. Nobody answers because everyone is on hold with someone else in the same building.)

A government-wide alert pings:

**SERVICE CONTACT EXPERIENCE UPGRADE — NOW LIVE**
**Policy name:** *"The National Hold Music Review"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why are citizens hearing pan flutes when they call Tax, oboes for Transport, and whale sounds for Licensing?

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Personalisation.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** They think they’ve called a wellness retreat.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** We meant to test one hotline.

**AIDE:** It propagated to every public number with a waiting queue.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We have accidentally composed the soundtrack to administrative despair.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE SOUND OF POLICY

War room. Whiteboard heading: **WHY IS THIS HAPPENING TO US?**

**PM:** Explain the policy in plain language.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** We replaced standard hold tones with “trust-building audio environments.”

**CHIEF WHIP:** You replaced beeps with vibes.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Correct.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We have governed by playlist.

By 08:20, reports flood in:

1. A pensions line loops one 12-second harp phrase forever
2. Passport callers hear upbeat jazz during outage notices
3. A council helpline inserted bird noises every time someone says “urgent”
4. Morning radio dubs it “Muzak Austerity”
5. Trending tag: **#PleaseJustAnswerThePhone**

**PM:** Turn it off.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Trying. The control panel says “audio serenity lock enabled.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Of course. We can launch chaos instantly but need two-factor peace.

## ACT TWO — WORDS FAIL, AGAIN

Comms drafts public lines.

**DRAFT V1:** “An enhanced sonic queue architecture has been deployed.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** That reads like a speaker brochure.

**DRAFT V2:** “We changed hold music and overdid it.”

**CHIEF WHIP:** Horrifyingly clear. Keep that energy.

**DRAFT V3:** “A contact-centre audio update was applied too broadly. We are restoring standard settings and prioritising callback speed.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Print V3 before someone adds the word “journey.”

Then a procurement slide leaks:

**“MUSIC MOODS: CALM COMPLIANCE / PATIENT CITIZEN / OPTIMISTIC RESIGNATION”**

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** It’s everywhere.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I admire the honesty of “optimistic resignation.”

## ACT THREE — THE COMMITTEE PREP

Select Committee prep call.

**CHAIR (speakerphone):** Minister, did your department commission mood-engineered hold systems without user testing?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We commissioned a limited pilot. What happened after that was a technical overreach and a governance underreach.

**OPPOSITION MP:** Is this innovation or distraction?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** At this hour, it is mostly percussion.

**OPPOSITION MP:** People waited forty minutes listening to woodwinds.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Which is thirty-nine minutes too many, regardless of instrument.

Legal proposes language tweaks:

Replace “error” with “experience inconsistency event.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. If the soup is salty, we don’t call it sodium-forward dining.

## ACT FOUR — FIX IT BEFORE DINNER

Recovery plan launched:

- Revert all departments to one neutral hold tone
- Enable immediate callback option everywhere possible
- Publish live average wait times by service
- Suspend non-essential call scripts and voice overlays
- Start independent review of procurement and rollout controls

By 15:10:

- Average wait perception complaints down 61%
- Callback uptake reaches 44%
- Social sentiment moves from “furious” to “mocking but calmer”

**PM:** Can we frame this as a learning exercise?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We can frame it as Tuesday. People still needed answers.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Evening bulletin wants one quote.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Give them this: service first, soundtrack never.

## CLIMAX — THE BRIEFING

Press briefing, 18:11.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**
This morning, a hold-audio update was deployed beyond its intended scope. It created frustration for people trying to reach essential services.

That is on us. We have restored standard settings, expanded callback options, and published live wait data so the public can see performance in real time.

We are also introducing a hard rule: no contact-centre experience changes go live nationally without independent operational sign-off.

**REPORTER:** Who approved the rollout?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** The chain of approval is being published within 48 hours.

**REPORTER:** Is this government more interested in optics than delivery?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Delivery is the only optic that matters when someone needs help.

## TAG

23:03. Office mostly empty.

An intern wheels in a box labelled:

**“HOLD MUSIC PHASE 2 — EMOTIONAL SYNC PACK”**

Peter-like minister stares, opens it, finds twenty USB sticks and a note:

**“Includes celebratory sax for delayed calls.”**

He closes the box, writes in thick marker:

**“NO. ANSWER FASTER INSTEAD.”**

Hands it to facilities.

Phone buzzes with headline:

**“Government Promises Less Jazz, More Service.”**

He exhales.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Finally, a manifesto.

**END**
