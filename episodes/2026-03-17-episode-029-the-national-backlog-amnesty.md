# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 029  
**Title:** The National Backlog Amnesty  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## Premise
After years of unresolved action items, the government launches a one-day “Backlog Amnesty” promising a clean slate for delayed work across every department. What starts as an administrative reset quickly mutates into a competitive sport of relabeling unfinished tasks as “strategic incubation,” while officials race to erase liabilities before anyone asks what was actually delivered.

## COLD OPEN – INT. CABINET OFFICE OPEN-PLAN FLOOR – 7:01 AM

A giant countdown clock reads: **"BACKLOG AMNESTY WINDOW: 11:59:12 REMAINING"**.

Rows of staff stare at dashboards full of red overdue markers.

**JULIA:**
Right. Today we don’t clear the backlog. We redefine it.

**NINA:**
That sounds like the backlog with extra syllables.

**DAN:**
No, this is reform. By midnight, everything old becomes “forward-scoped.”

A broadcast email lands with a cheerful chime:

> **CIVIL SERVICE BULLETIN:** "Historical delays will be reclassified as legacy horizon items. Congratulations on your progress."

**NINA:**
It congratulates us before we’ve done anything.

**DAN:**
That’s called momentum.

---

## ACT ONE – INT. DELIVERY UNIT WAR ROOM – 8:14 AM

Whiteboards divide tasks into fresh categories:
- Late
- Very Late
- "Awaiting Democratic Weather"
- "Resolved by Narrative"

**ANALYST:**
If we move all items older than six months into “legacy horizon,” completion rates jump to ninety-one percent.

**JULIA:**
Ninety-one? Why not ninety-five?

**ANALYST:**
Ninety-five triggers automatic scrutiny.

**DAN:**
Good catch. Keep excellence safely mediocre.

### SCENE 2 – INT. MINISTERIAL OUTER OFFICE – 8:49 AM

A queue forms outside a scanner labeled **"AMNESTY CODER"**.

Each document gets a new sticker:
- "Deferred for cross-functional alignment"
- "Paused pending citizen co-listening"
- "Actioned in principle"

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Can we reclassify this procurement delay?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
It’s from 2019.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Perfect. Vintage backlog. Put it in archives and call it heritage.

---

## ACT TWO – INT. JOINT DEPARTMENTS VIDEO CALL – 9:37 AM

Six departments present synchronized slides titled: **"FROM DELAY TO DESIGN"**.

**TREASURY REP:**
Our backlog shrank by forty percent in forty minutes.

**HEALTH REP:**
How?

**TREASURY REP:**
We converted overdue actions into “fiscal contemplation threads.”

**TRANSPORT REP:**
We renamed ours “journeys.”

**NINA:**
You can’t rename a missed deadline a journey.

**TRANSPORT REP:**
You can if there’s a map icon.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRESS GRID DESK – 10:22 AM

Draft messaging appears on a shared screen:

- **"Government Ends Era of Administrative Drift"**
- **"Record Delivery Confidence Across Whitehall"**
- **"Backlog Burden Lifted in One Bold Day"**

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Any evidence the work is done?

**JULIA:**
The burden is lifted. We never said by whom.

**PRESS OFFICER:**
And if journalists ask for before-and-after numbers?

**DAN:**
Give after-and-after. The nation prefers optimism.

---

## ACT THREE – INT. LIVE BRIEFING ROOM – 11:16 AM

A spokesperson stands beside a chart with dramatic upward arrows and very small footnotes.

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Today marks a turning point in government agility.

**REPORTER 1:**
How many overdue items were completed?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
A significant number were transitioned.

**REPORTER 2:**
Transitioned where?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Into clearer ownership pathways.

**REPORTER 1:**
So… not completed.

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Completion is a spectrum.

Dan, off to the side, mouths: *don’t say spectrum*. Too late.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRIME MINISTER’S OFFICE – 11:53 AM

The PM scans a one-page summary:

> **BACKLOG AMNESTY OUTCOME:** 84% status improvement, 0% operational disruption.

**PM:**
How can status improve by eighty-four percent in one morning?

**ADVISER:**
We simplified language.

**PM:**
Did we finish anything tangible?

**ADVISER:**
We finished categorizing the unfinished things.

PM stares into the middle distance.

**PM:**
And tomorrow?

**DAN:**
Tomorrow we launch a Delivery Restoration Sprint to rebuild confidence in what we just classified.

---

## CLIMAX – INT. CABINET ROOM – 12:31 PM

Emergency review. Everyone has a printed dashboard. Every dashboard contradicts every other dashboard.

**COMMITTEE CHAIR:**
Why does this version show six hundred completed tasks while this one shows eleven?

**ANALYST:**
Different definitions of “completed.”

**NINA:**
What’s the strict definition?

**ANALYST:**
Task done.

Silence.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
That feels operationally rigid.

**TREASURY REP:**
Can we pilot “done-adjacent”?

**PM:**
No. We are not inventing done-adjacent.

**JULIA:**
Then we need an official rollback statement.

**DAN:**
We can’t call it rollback. Call it Phase Two Realism.

**PM:**
Fine. Draft it. Include one sentence in plain English so people trust it.

Everyone looks personally attacked.

---

## TAG – INT. RECORDS BASEMENT – 6:07 PM

Metal shelving groans under freshly boxed files stamped:

**"LEGACY HORIZON — REVISIT Q4"**

Nina adds one final box labeled: **"THINGS WE PROMISED TO EXPLAIN"**.

Dan walks in carrying two mugs of tea and a label maker.

**DAN:**
Good news: tomorrow’s initiative is simpler.

**NINA:**
What is it?

**DAN:**
National Clarity Week.

**NINA:**
You’re joking.

Dan quietly prints a label: **"CLARITY (PENDING DEFINITION)"**.

**END**
