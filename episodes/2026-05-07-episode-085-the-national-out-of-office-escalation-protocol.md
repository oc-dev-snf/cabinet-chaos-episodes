# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 085
**Title:** The National Out-of-Office Escalation Protocol
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE INCIDENT ROOM, 07:00. A wall display reads: **"INBOX STABILITY — AMBER"**.)

Morning briefing card:

**CITIZEN SERVICE WEEK — DAY 17**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Out-of-Office Escalation Protocol"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why does every urgent file now say, "for immediate action on my return"?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because six senior signatories are on "brief strategic leave" and all their auto-replies redirect to each other.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** One case has bounced across four departments and returned to the original sender with "kind regards."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So the system has achieved circular accountability.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE AUTO-REPLY CASCADE

08:12. Cross-government incident call with operations directors, private offices, and service leads.

Slide title: **"WHEN EVERYONE IS AWAY, NO ONE IS RESPONSIBLE"**

**PM:** Is this just holiday season?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Not exactly. It's process design. We rely on named individuals instead of active duty pools.

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** 41% of urgent requests last week hit at least one unavailable approver.

**DATA ANALYST:** Mean response time doubles when a request receives more than two auto-reply redirects.

Four structural faults identified:

1. Out-of-office messages without clear delegated authority
2. Escalation addresses that route to unmonitored inboxes
3. "Urgent" labels used without priority criteria
4. Handovers written as narratives, not decisions

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We've built a relay race where the baton is an apology email.

## ACT TWO — THE PROTOCOL

10:26. Emergency drafting with policy, digital service, HR, and departmental operations.

Slide title: **"CONTINUITY OVER PERSONAL AVAILABILITY"**

**POLICY LEAD:** Require every business-critical role to maintain a live delegate list by function, not by personality.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Add structured out-of-office templates: delegate name, monitored escalation inbox, response window.

**HR LEAD:** Standardize handover notes into three lines: pending decision, legal risk, next deadline.

Three controls approved for immediate rollout:

- **Delegate-by-Default Rule:** no OOO activation on critical roles without a named active delegate
- **Escalation Ladder:** tiered contact path with 2-hour, 6-hour, and 24-hour thresholds
- **Urgency Test:** requests must declare citizen impact, statutory deadline, or financial exposure

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If everything is urgent, nothing is. If no one owns it, everyone blames the template.

## ACT THREE — FIRST DAY PILOT

12:03. Pilot begins across seven departments and two central response units.

**INCIDENT COORDINATOR:** Tracking first-response time, redirect count, and unresolved urgent items.

12:21. Structured OOO template goes live.

**PROGRAMME MANAGER:** Auto-reply loops drop immediately because delegates are real people with real inboxes.

13:08. Escalation ladder triggers for delayed planning approvals.

**DELIVERY LEAD:** Two cases resolved at tier two without ministerial intervention.

14:17. Urgency Test activated in request form.

**SERVICE OWNER:** "Urgent" submissions fall by 33% in three hours.

Follow-up actions agreed:

- Daily publication of unresolved urgent queue by department
- Weekly audit of delegate coverage for critical functions
- Monthly simulation of mass leave scenarios
- Public service note explaining how continuity improves response reliability

**PM:** We are not anti-holiday. We are pro-not-dropping-the-state.

## ACT FOUR — COMMUNICATIONS WITHOUT PANIC

16:42. Comms clears line: **"GOVERNMENT TIGHTENS CONTINUITY RULES TO SPEED DECISIONS"**

Public points agreed:

- Services should not pause because one inbox is unattended
- Delegation improves resilience and accountability
- Clear urgency criteria reduce noise and protect true emergencies
- Standard handovers reduce delay and rework
- Operational stability supports better frontline delivery

By 18:09 (pilot metrics):

- Average first response time down 29%
- Auto-reply redirect chains down 61%
- Unresolved urgent items down 24%
- Escalations resolved within six hours up 31%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** It's not glamorous, but it's the plumbing.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Good governance is mostly plumbing with better nouns.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:22. Reporters gather outside the briefing room.

**REPORTER:** Minister, are you regulating holiday emails now?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We're regulating avoidable drift in urgent decisions.

**REPORTER:** Is this performative management?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** The pilot reduced delays the same day. Performance is the opposite of performative.

**REPORTER:** What's next?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Expand, monitor, refine. Then do the same to every inbox labyrinth we find.
