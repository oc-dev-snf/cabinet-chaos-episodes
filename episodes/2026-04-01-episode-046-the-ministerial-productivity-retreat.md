# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 046
**Title:** The Ministerial Productivity Retreat
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(GOVERNMENT TRAINING CENTRE. A flipchart reads: “DOING LESS, BETTER.” Underneath: “Mandatory all-day workshop.”)

**CALLUM:** Why are 42 people in a room to discuss efficiency?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** It’s cross-functional alignment.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** And pastries. People engage better with laminated carbs.

**FACILITATOR:** Welcome to the National Productivity Retreat. First exercise: silent reflection on workflow pain points.

**CALLUM:** The pain point is this workshop started at 8:00.

**FACILITATOR:** Great candour. Parking that in the Values Corner.

(The facilitator places “THIS MEETING” on a sticky note under “Opportunities.”)

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

Session one: “Identifying Bottlenecks.” Everyone writes the same issue: approvals take too long.

**FACILITATOR:** Excellent consensus.

**CALLUM:** Good. So we reduce approvals.

**GOVERNANCE LEAD:** Careful. We need approval to reduce approvals.

**CALLUM:** Naturally.

A process map is projected. It resembles a metro network designed by a haunted spider.

**POLICY LEAD:** Blue line is policy sign-off. Green line is legal sign-off. Dashed orange line is reassurance sign-off.

**CALLUM:** Reassurance sign-off?

**POLICY LEAD:** Someone confirms everyone feels comfortable with the sign-off culture.

**CALLUM:** We have emotional middleware.

## ACT TWO

After lunch, breakout groups present solutions:
- “Create a Taskforce on Simplification Governance”
- “Introduce a Pre-Approval Readiness Checklist”
- “Pilot a Meeting to Replace Unnecessary Meetings”

**CALLUM:** These are all new layers.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We could rebrand them as lean layers.

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** I’ve got a dashboard mockup. It shows how many dashboards we have.

**CALLUM:** How many?

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** Depends which dashboard you trust.

In the corner, two analysts quietly run numbers and produce a one-page note titled: “Three Steps to Cut Decision Time by 40%.”

It is ignored because it has no color coding.

## ACT THREE

Final session: “Commitments.” The room braces for another acronym.

**CALLUM:** Right. No new frameworks. Here’s the policy:
1. Any approval step without legal or budget impact is deleted.
2. Any paper over two pages needs a one-paragraph decision ask at the top.
3. Any meeting without a named decision is cancelled.

(Silence. Mild panic. Then cautious typing.)

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** We can implement by Monday.

**GOVERNANCE LEAD:** Should we run this through the Simplification Taskforce?

**CALLUM:** There is no Simplification Taskforce.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** Should there be?

**CALLUM:** No. If we create one, we deserve the paperwork.

People nod, partly convinced, partly afraid the workshop might continue.

## CLIMAX

Press line goes out by evening:

**“Government is reducing internal process overhead to speed delivery, improve accountability, and publish faster decisions.”**

A reporter asks if this is a revolution.

**CALLUM:** No. It’s deleting steps we added during previous revolutions.

**REPORTER:** How will you measure success?

**CALLUM:** Fewer meetings, shorter papers, faster decisions, less throat-clearing.

**REPORTER:** Is “throat-clearing” an official metric?

**CALLUM:** It is now.

## TAG

Back at the office. Calendar notifications start disappearing in real time.

**AIDE:** We cancelled 17 recurring meetings.

**CALLUM:** Good.

**AIDE:** Three organisers have appealed.

**CALLUM:** On what grounds?

**AIDE:** “Loss of strategic visibility.”

**CALLUM:** Grant them a window.

A final email arrives from Facilities:

**Subject:** “Unused Flipcharts Available for Rehoming.”

Callum stares at it, smiles, and archives without opening.

**END**
