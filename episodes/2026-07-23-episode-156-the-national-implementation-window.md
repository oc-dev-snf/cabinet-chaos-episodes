# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 156
**Title:** The National Implementation Window
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the Prime Minister announced the National Implementation Window.

It was described as a dedicated period during which departments would be allowed to implement things without first announcing a further period of dedication.

Communications said the public wanted action.

Policy said action required sequencing.

Treasury said sequencing required costs.

Legal said costs required definitions.

The Cabinet Secretary said the window would bring clarity by placing all delivery activity inside a clearly understood aperture.

The junior adviser asked whether the window was open.

Communications said it was open in principle.

Legal said it was open subject to conditions.

Treasury asked whether heat loss had been modelled.

Digital offered to build a portal called Window View, showing real-time implementation visibility.

Policy said real time might create unrealistic expectations about time.

The Prime Minister said the country deserved a government that could look through a window and see the work beginning to prepare to start.

The Cabinet Secretary wrote "IMPLEMENTATION WINDOW: OPENING SOON" on the whiteboard.

Everyone agreed it showed momentum, provided nobody asked from which side.

## ACT ONE

By 08:30, every department had been asked to submit items suitable for the implementation window.

Transport sent a timetable for deciding when timetables should become operational.

Health sent a diagram of a patient pathway with the patient represented by a question mark.

Education sent a lesson plan for explaining delivery to stakeholders not yet identified.

The Home Office sent a folder marked "window-sensitive".

Treasury sent a note asking whether implementation could be spread over several financial years by calling it observation.

Communications rejected the submissions because too many of them looked like documents.

Policy said implementation often looked like documents at first.

Communications said the camera hated first.

The Cabinet Secretary proposed a standard window pack:

ONE INTENTION NEAR GLASS.

ONE DECISION WITH LIGHT ON IT.

ONE ACTIONABLE SHADOW.

ONE BACKUP PHRASE IN CASE OF DRAUGHT.

Legal asked whether shadows could be actionable.

Policy said only when properly minuted.

The junior adviser asked what would happen if something was implemented outside the window.

The room went quiet.

Treasury said it would be inefficient.

Communications said it would be off-message.

Legal said it might constitute unauthorised delivery.

## ACT TWO

At 10:00, the Prime Minister rehearsed the launch line.

"Today, we are opening the National Implementation Window."

Communications liked opening.

Legal worried opening implied access.

Policy said access implied engagement.

Treasury said engagement implied refreshments.

The line became: "Today, we are confirming the conditions under which implementation may become visible."

Digital displayed the Window View dashboard:

WINDOW STATUS: PRE-OPEN.

DELIVERY LIGHT: ADEQUATE.

DRAUGHT RISK: MANAGED.

PUBLIC VISIBILITY: CALIBRATED.

The junior adviser asked whether the dashboard showed anything actually happening.

Digital said it showed readiness for activity to be recorded once activity had agreed to be recordable.

Communications said that was too honest.

Policy changed it to "activity-enabled".

Meanwhile, a department reported that one small reform had accidentally gone live.

Communications asked whether anyone had photographed it.

Legal asked whether it had consented to being live.

Treasury asked whether it could be treated as a pilot of itself.

The Cabinet Secretary ordered the reform placed just outside the window until the launch was complete.

## ACT THREE

At midday, the cross-government Window Readiness Group convened beside an actual window overlooking a courtyard.

Communications said the symbolism was excellent.

Policy said the courtyard represented the delivery landscape.

Treasury said the courtyard was already owned.

Legal said the window could not be opened because the building manual described it as decorative.

The Prime Minister asked whether that mattered.

The Cabinet Secretary said decorative windows were often more stable than operational ones.

Digital updated the dashboard:

WINDOW FUNCTION: REPRESENTATIVE.

IMPLEMENTATION FIELD: VISIBLE.

OPENING MECHANISM: STRATEGIC.

AIRFLOW: NOT A COMMITMENT.

The junior adviser noticed the courtyard was full of officials carrying boxes labelled "urgent, awaiting window".

Communications said that was useful movement.

Policy said movement was the public face of progress.

Treasury said boxes were cheaper than outcomes.

Legal said labels should avoid verbs.

The Cabinet Secretary instructed all boxes to be relabelled "under consideration for movement".

## CLIMAX

The afternoon press conference began with the Prime Minister standing beside the decorative window.

"This government is opening the way for implementation to be seen, understood, and responsibly approached," the Prime Minister said.

A reporter asked whether the window was actually open.

Communications signalled "confidence".

Policy signalled "framework".

Legal signalled "hinges".

The Prime Minister said: "The window is open in the sense that the country can now see the conditions for opening."

Another reporter asked what had been implemented today.

The Cabinet Secretary stepped forward and explained that implementation was not an event but a managed environment.

Digital refreshed Window View.

It displayed:

VISIBLE ACTION: PENDING.

IMPLEMENTATION CLIMATE: FAVOURABLE.

PUBLIC SIGHTLINE: ACTIVE.

OPENNESS LEVEL: REVIEWABLE.

A reporter pointed out that the small reform had already gone live outside the official window.

Legal said it had not gone live; it had become prematurely available.

Treasury said premature availability could be cheaper if nobody relied on it.

Communications said the government welcomed early evidence of future delivery.

The Prime Minister concluded that the National Implementation Window had proved the government was serious about opening things.

Especially things that did not open.

## TAG

By evening, departments had requested blackout curtains for sensitive implementation.

The Cabinet Office announced a Window Coverings Strategy to maintain transparency during periods when seeing things would be unhelpful.
