# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 025  
**Title:** The Briefing That Briefed Itself  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

**INT. CENTRAL BRIEFING ROOM – 06:58**

A wall screen reads:

> **DRAFT MESSAGE DISCIPLINE WORKSHOP**

Underneath, a live feed of ministers giving contradictory interviews.

**AIDE:**
Do we want one line for everyone?

**COMMS LEAD:**
Absolutely. A clear, unified line.

**AIDE:**
What is it?

**COMMS LEAD:**
We’re still aligning on that.

A policy adviser rushes in with a fresh printout.

**ADVISER:**
Good news. The line has been approved by twelve offices.

**AIDE:**
What does it say?

**ADVISER:**
“Further detail will follow shortly.”

---

## ACT ONE

At 07:05, government announces a major package to simplify public services.

At 07:07, three departments publish three explainers.

- Department A: “A streamlined national framework.”
- Department B: “A local-first flexible model.”
- Department C: “A transitional hybrid pathway.”

All three claim to describe the same thing.

By 08:00, breakfast radio is asking whether the policy is centralised, devolved, digital, analog, mandatory, optional, immediate, phased, or symbolic.

Official answer:

> “Yes, in a sequenced sense.”

Inside government, an emergency “message coherence cell” is created.

Its first action is to circulate a two-page note titled:

> **KEY SIMPLE POINTS (FULL VERSION)**

---

## ACT TWO

Cross-party committee hearing.

A member asks how many versions of the policy note exist.

A civil servant consults a spreadsheet.

**CIVIL SERVANT:**
Depends whether we count tracked changes as separate constitutional events.

Another member asks who has final sign-off authority.

**CIVIL SERVANT:**
Formally, the Cabinet subcommittee.

**MEMBER:**
And informally?

**CIVIL SERVANT:**
Whichever office is nearest the send button at the decisive moment.

Meanwhile, the public FAQ page is updated six times in one afternoon.

Update history includes:

- “This is not a U-turn.”
- “This is an implementation refinement.”
- “This is a scheduled clarification event.”

Journalists begin using a bingo card.

Squares include:

- “Going forward”
- “At pace”
- “Lessons learned in real time”

By mid-afternoon, the card is complete.

---

## ACT THREE

Evening strategy meeting.

Agenda:

1. Stop contradicting ourselves.
2. Explain the policy in one sentence.
3. Find out if the policy still matches the sentence.

A junior analyst offers a radical draft:

> “Here is what changes, when it changes, and who it affects.”

Room falls quiet.

Treasury asks if that wording creates “expectation risk.”

Operations asks if any wording could be locked for more than forty-five minutes.

Comms proposes a compromise:

> “Clear, concise detail will be available in staged clarity windows.”

Operations places forehead gently on table.

---

## CLIMAX

Night press conference.

A reporter asks:

**REPORTER:**
Why has this been so hard to explain?

The Prime Minister pauses.

**PRIME MINISTER:**
Because we tried to make it sound perfect before making it sound plain. That was a mistake. Here’s the plain version.

They read from one page:

- What the policy does
- What starts now
- What starts later
- Who can contact whom for help

No metaphors. No slogans. No verbal smoke machine.

For the first time all day, follow-up questions are about substance.

---

## TAG

**INT. CABINET OFFICE CORRIDOR – 22:26**

A whiteboard marked **MESSAGE ARCHITECTURE** has been erased.

In its place, someone has written:

> “Try saying what happened.”

Below, in different handwriting:

> “Pilot successful. Roll out nationwide.”
