# Cabinet Chaos — Episode 093: The National Minutes Standardisation Drive

*Date: 2026-05-15*

## COLD OPEN
At 7:00 a.m., every public body receives a red-banner notice:

**ALL MEETING MINUTES MUST NOW USE THE SAME TEMPLATE.**

The rationale: “If decisions are made differently, at least they should be documented identically.”

By 7:12, seven teams are debating whether “Noted” means agreement, awareness, or existential surrender.

## ACT ONE — THE LAUNCH
In the daily briefing, the Cabinet Secretary introduces the National Minutes Standardisation Drive.

“Government must speak with one voice,” they announce, “especially in paragraph four, Action Points.”

A reporter asks if this addresses any actual policy delay.

A minister replies, “Consistency is the first form of speed.”

A slide deck appears titled **Minute-by-Minute Governance** with the official sequence:

1. **Attendance Confirmed**
2. **Context Repeated**
3. **Decision Deferred Respectfully**
4. **Actions Allocated with Optimism**

Everyone claps in the careful way that suggests uncertainty.

## ACT TWO — ROLL-OUT
Departments receive a 53-page guidance pack plus a “quick guide” of 19 pages.

- Every sentence must start with a verb.
- Passive voice requires deputy-level approval.
- “Any Other Business” is renamed “Strategic Miscellany.”

The health team tests the template and discovers it takes longer to record the meeting than to hold it.

The education team creates a macro to automate section headers, then is congratulated for “innovation in administrative harmony.”

## ACT THREE — THE TRAINING SESSION
At midday, staff join a mandatory webinar: *Writing Minutes That Mean What They Might Mean*.

Trainer: “Avoid vague phrases like ‘to be progressed’ unless no one knows by whom.”

Participant: “What if everyone is jointly responsible?”

Trainer: “Then no one is solely delayed. Mark as collaborative momentum.”

Another participant asks whether jokes can be minuted.

Trainer: “Only if they become policy.”

The call freezes for three minutes while someone shares a 200-cell action tracker.

## ACT FOUR — PARLIAMENTARY SCRUTINY
In Parliament, opposition members challenge the programme.

“Will this reduce backlogs?”

A minister responds, “It has already reduced formatting diversity.”

“Backlogs?”

“Now consistently described.”

Another member asks whether frontline services asked for this reform.

The response: “They asked for clearer decisions. We’ve begun with clearer bullet points.”

## CLIMAX — THE METRICS MOMENT
At 6:00 p.m., first-day statistics are released:

- **Meetings Minuted in Standard Format:** 9,811
- **Template Compliance Rate:** 97%
- **Action Items Closed:** 21

Officials praise a “historic leap in documentary coherence.”

Then auditors discover one major department used last year’s template with different font spacing.

An emergency cross-government call is convened to harmonise typography.

## TAG
Near midnight, a team lead sends one plain note after a long meeting:

**“Decision: yes. Owner: Priya. Deadline: Tuesday.”**

Three workstreams unblock before breakfast.

At 7:03 a.m., a new circular lands:

**“Phase Two: National Decision Verb Taxonomy Consultation.”**
