# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 024  
**Title:** The National Outage of Clarity  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

**INT. GOVERNMENT MEDIA HUB – 06:59**

Three giant screens display three official headlines for the same announcement:

1. **"SYSTEMS RESILIENT"**
2. **"SERVICES PARTIALLY IMPACTED"**
3. **"TEMPORARY INTERRUPTION TO NORMAL CONTINUITY"**

A junior aide raises a hand.

**AIDE:**
Which one are we using?

**PRESS DIRECTOR:**
All of them. Different audiences, different confidence levels.

A minister enters, checking their phone.

**MINISTER:**
My constituency app says everything is fine.

**AIDE:**
That app hasn’t updated since Tuesday.

**MINISTER:**
Excellent. Finally, a reliable platform.

---

## ACT ONE

At 07:10, several public service portals slow to a crawl.

Not down. Not up. Just... contemplative.

Cabinet calls it a "performance weather event."

The central briefing note proposes approved phrases:

- “Intermittent responsiveness”
- “Adaptive loading experience”
- “User-paced access journey”

A technical lead suggests saying "we’re investigating an outage."

The room reacts as if someone proposed replacing Parliament with a vending machine.

Meanwhile, departments begin issuing updates:

- Health: “Appointments unaffected, except where affected.”
- Transport: “Timetables remain accurate in principle.”
- Tax Office: “Deadlines unchanged; submission pathways currently philosophical.”

By lunchtime, the government launches a single status page.

It immediately shows:

> **STATUS PAGE CURRENTLY EXPERIENCING HIGH DEMAND**

---

## ACT TWO

A cross-party committee convenes to understand what happened.

They are shown a flowchart titled:

> **NATIONAL DIGITAL RESILIENCE: SIMPLIFIED OVERVIEW (V12 FINAL FINAL)**

The chart contains 46 boxes, 71 arrows, and one box labelled "legacy magic."

An opposition member asks who signed off the system architecture.

A civil servant answers carefully:

**CIVIL SERVANT:**
It evolved through iterative governance.

**OPPOSITION MEMBER:**
So nobody.

**CIVIL SERVANT:**
Collectively everybody.

Back in communications, a draft public statement appears:

> “Today’s challenges demonstrate the robustness of our challenge-response framework.”

Another draft:

> “We are not where we wanted to be, but we are exactly where we currently are.”

A senior adviser circles both and writes: "warmer, more human."

---

## ACT THREE

Emergency evening meeting.

Agenda:

1. Restore confidence.
2. Restore systems.
3. Decide which one can be restored first.

Treasury asks whether emergency tech funding will be needed.

Cabinet asks whether existing tech funding can be renamed "emergency" retroactively.

The operations team, running on tea and determination, finally gets stable performance across key services.

They request one thing:

> “Please stop making last-minute announcement pages during incidents.”

Communications nods, then quietly asks for "a tiny microsite explaining resilience."

Operations just stare.

---

## CLIMAX

Late-night press conference.

A reporter asks:

**REPORTER:**
Was this an outage or not?

The Prime Minister exhales.

**PRIME MINISTER:**
People couldn’t access services they needed. So yes, for them, it was an outage. Systems are now stabilised. Tomorrow we’ll publish what failed, how long each service was affected, and what changes we’re making. No jargon, no spin.

A brief silence.

Then the practical questions begin.

For once, answers are in normal English.

---

## TAG

**INT. CABINET OFFICE KITCHEN – 22:31**

Someone has taped a new label over the coffee machine:

> **NATIONAL RESILIENCE UNIT (CAFFEINE DIVISION)**

Below it, another note:

> “Machine unavailable due to intermittent responsiveness. Please queue with confidence.”

Two exhausted staffers laugh anyway.

**END**
