# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 139
**Title:** The National Clarification Lane
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the government announced that confusion was no longer a problem.

It was a throughput issue.

This distinction mattered because problems required solutions, while throughput issues required diagrams, pilot funding, and someone from Digital saying "user journey" until the kettle boiled.

The Prime Minister had spent the previous evening watching three ministers explain the same policy in four different ways on five channels, producing six headlines and one emergency group chat called LINE TO TAKE - ACTUAL FINAL.

Communications said the public needed clarity.

Policy said the public had been given clarity.

Legal said the clarity had been conditional.

Treasury said conditional clarity was cheaper.

The Cabinet Secretary asked whether anyone could state the policy in one sentence.

There was a silence of such quality that Strategy briefly considered branding it.

At 07:11, a junior adviser suggested a dedicated clarification process: a formal route by which confusing statements could be clarified without implying they had previously been unclear.

The Prime Minister liked this. It sounded active, hygienic, and unlikely to require an apology.

By 07:18, the National Clarification Lane had been born.

## ACT ONE

The first debate was whether the lane was physical, procedural, digital, or metaphorical.

Transport said lanes should not be announced without cones.

Digital said the lane could be a portal.

Legal said portals created expectations of entry.

Treasury asked whether the lane had maintenance costs.

Communications said the public did not need to understand the lane. They needed to feel that clarification was moving.

Policy produced a flowchart showing how a statement became a clarification, how a clarification became guidance, how guidance became context, and how context became something nobody was technically bound by.

The Cabinet Secretary asked where accountability sat.

Policy pointed to a small box at the edge of the chart marked "to be routed."

Legal said the box should be bigger.

Treasury said the box should be unfunded.

Communications asked whether accountability could be renamed "delivery ownership," because ownership sounded positive and delivery sounded like it might arrive.

The Prime Minister asked for a public-facing sentence.

Communications drafted: "We are opening a new lane for clarity."

Transport said this implied existing lanes were blocked.

Digital said opening a lane implied some users had been outside the lane.

Legal said clarity could not be promised.

Treasury said new lanes encouraged demand.

The sentence became: "We are improving the routes through which information is responsibly understood."

The Prime Minister said it sounded like directions to a fire exit, but approved it anyway.

## ACT TWO

By mid-morning, departments were submitting items for clarification.

Health needed clarification on whether "immediate action" meant today, this week, or the next available operational moment.

Education needed clarification on whether "record investment" included money that had already been announced, re-announced, described, profiled, or emotionally allocated.

Housing needed clarification on whether "unlocking delivery" meant building houses, approving houses, discussing houses, or removing a meeting about houses from the critical path.

Treasury submitted a clarification request asking whether clarification requests could be batched quarterly.

The Cabinet Secretary created three categories: Clarify Now, Clarify Soon, and Clarify Without Creating Further Clarification.

Legal asked for a fourth category: Do Not Clarify Because The Ambiguity Is Doing Work.

Communications said that could not be written down.

Legal said it already had been, by Communications, in track changes.

The room turned to the junior adviser, who had been assigned to operate the lane.

The adviser asked what qualified as a clarification.

Policy said a clarification explained what had been meant.

Communications said a clarification explained what should now be heard.

Legal said a clarification narrowed exposure.

Treasury said a clarification controlled cost.

The adviser asked what happened when those four things differed.

The Cabinet Secretary said that was why the lane existed.

## ACT THREE

At 12:30, the Clarification Lane dashboard went live internally.

It had a clean interface with three columns: Incoming, Being Clarified, and Clarity Achieved.

For six minutes, everyone felt modern.

Then a minister added a television quote to Incoming:

"We have always been clear that our position is evolving."

The system asked whether the quote required clarification.

Communications selected yes.

The system asked what was unclear.

Communications typed: "The clarity."

The system rejected this for being circular.

Digital said the system was working.

Communications said the system lacked political maturity.

A second minister added a speech line: "No family will be worse off in the round."

Treasury asked which round.

Policy asked which family.

Legal asked whether "worse off" referred to cash, access, expectation, or vibes.

Communications asked whether "in the round" could be replaced with "overall."

Treasury said overall was dangerous because people might add things up.

The Cabinet Secretary moved the item to Being Clarified, where it immediately generated three sub-clarifications and a meeting invite called ROUNDNESS PRINCIPLES.

By 13:05, the lane had a backlog.

By 13:20, the backlog had a strategy.

By 13:35, the strategy needed clarification.

## CLIMAX

The afternoon press briefing began with the spokesperson announcing that the National Clarification Lane would help ensure the public received timely, responsible explanations of government information.

A reporter asked whether it was needed because the government had been unclear.

The spokesperson said the government had always been clear about the need for clearer channels.

Another reporter asked whether clearer channels were different from clearer answers.

In the Cabinet room, Communications said: "Do not compare channels and answers."

Policy said: "They have compared themselves."

Treasury said: "Answers are more expensive than channels."

The spokesperson said the lane was about making sure information could move efficiently from announcement to understanding.

A reporter asked whether understanding was currently delayed.

Legal said: "Say phased."

Communications said: "Say sequenced."

Policy said: "Say sometimes."

Nobody looked at Policy this time, because they had learned the danger of eye contact.

The spokesperson said understanding was being responsibly sequenced.

Then the internal dashboard accidentally appeared on the briefing room screen.

It showed 147 items in Incoming, 12 in Being Clarified, and zero in Clarity Achieved.

At the top was a red banner:

"WARNING: CLARIFICATION LANE EXPERIENCING UNCLEAR DELAYS."

Digital said the banner was technically accurate.

Communications said accuracy was not the same as readiness.

Treasury asked whether the zero could be rounded up.

A reporter asked why no clarity had been achieved.

The spokesperson looked at the screen, then at the camera, and said: "Because we are taking clarity seriously enough not to rush it."

The clip reached the evening bulletins before the dashboard could be put back behind a login.

## TAG

By nightfall, the National Clarification Lane had been declared a bold first step in reducing the friction between statements and their eventual meaning.

Departments were instructed to submit future public lines with a pre-clarification note, a clarification readiness score, and an assessment of whether the line would remain clear if read aloud by someone tired.

Digital added a new dashboard column: Clarity Pending.

Legal added: Clarity Without Prejudice.

Treasury added: Clarity Subject To Affordability.

Communications added: Clarity In Principle.

The Prime Minister asked whether any of this made things clearer.

The Cabinet Secretary said it clarified the scale of the clarification challenge.

Everyone agreed this was progress.

At midnight, the system sent its first automated update to all departments.

"Your clarification has entered the lane," it said.

Under status, it read:

"Awaiting clarification."
