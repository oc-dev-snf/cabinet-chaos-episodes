# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 155
**Title:** The National Progress Cupboard
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the Prime Minister announced the National Progress Cupboard.

It was a physical cupboard in the Cabinet Office where departments could place evidence that things were going well.

Communications said the public wanted visible delivery.

Policy said delivery was often too abstract and needed shelving.

Treasury asked whether the cupboard was existing furniture or new ambition.

Legal asked whether anything placed inside it became an official record, a political claim, or simply lost property.

The Cabinet Secretary said the cupboard would provide a single, coordinated location for progress, pending a review of whether progress should be stored upright.

Digital offered to build an online version called Cupboard Live.

Communications said live cupboards suggested doors opening.

Legal said doors created discovery risk.

The junior adviser asked what would actually go in the cupboard.

Policy said milestones.

Treasury asked whether milestones were heavy.

Communications suggested laminated achievements, which photographed better and could be removed quickly if challenged.

The Prime Minister said the country deserved to know that progress was not only happening, but being responsibly kept somewhere.

The Cabinet Secretary wrote "CUPBOARD-LED DELIVERY" on the whiteboard.

Everyone agreed it sounded solid, provided nobody leaned on it.

## ACT ONE

By 08:30, departments had been instructed to submit one item each for the cupboard.

Transport sent a folder labelled "future acceleration".

Health sent a chart showing a line moving upward, without saying what the line represented.

Education sent a certificate congratulating itself on entering the next phase of consideration.

The Home Office sent a sealed envelope marked operationally promising.

Treasury sent a note asking whether the cupboard could be counted as infrastructure.

Communications rejected the first batch because none of it looked enough like progress from a distance.

Policy proposed a standard format:

ONE THING DONE.

ONE THING NEARLY DONE.

ONE THING DEFINITELY IN MOTION.

ONE THING WITH A COLOUR.

Digital said each item could receive a cupboard confidence score.

Legal asked whether confidence was measurable or merely liable.

The Cabinet Secretary said the cupboard should not become a dumping ground for optimism.

The junior adviser opened the cupboard and found three old strategies, a cracked mug, and a post-it reading "announce by spring".

Communications said that showed continuity.

Policy said it showed institutional memory.

Treasury said the mug was already funded.

## ACT TWO

At 10:00, the Prime Minister rehearsed the line for the launch.

"Today we are opening the National Progress Cupboard."

Communications said opening sounded transparent.

Legal said opening was transparent.

Policy said transparency was useful until people saw inside.

The line became: "Today we are establishing a secure location for the nation's delivery evidence."

Digital displayed a prototype dashboard:

CUPBOARD STATUS: READY.

SHELF CAPACITY: CONFIDENT.

PROGRESS ITEMS: ARRIVING.

DOOR POSITION: MANAGED.

The junior adviser asked whether the public could see the cupboard.

Communications said yes, visually.

Legal said no, materially.

Policy suggested a photograph of the cupboard with the door almost closed.

Treasury asked whether the photograph could be reused for future announcements with different captions.

The Cabinet Secretary approved a rolling cupboard image strategy.

Meanwhile, a department tried to submit an actual completed project.

The room became tense.

Communications worried it would raise expectations.

Policy said completed projects made incomplete projects look incomplete.

Legal asked whether completion had been independently completed.

Treasury asked whether completion created maintenance costs.

The item was moved to a side drawer marked "too specific".

## ACT THREE

At midday, the cupboard was inspected by a cross-government readiness panel.

It contained fourteen folders, six placards, two ribbon-cutting scissors, a USB stick nobody would admit owning, and a model bridge labelled "representative".

Communications said it looked reassuringly busy.

Policy said busy was the visual language of delivery.

Legal said the USB stick should be treated as both present and not present until someone found a password.

Digital said Cupboard Live was ready, except the upload button had been renamed "place" to preserve the furniture metaphor.

The Prime Minister asked whether the cupboard could have a motto.

Communications suggested: "Progress, Properly Stored."

Policy suggested: "Delivery You Can Almost Touch."

Treasury suggested: "Existing Assets, New Purpose."

The Cabinet Secretary chose all three and called it a layered message.

The junior adviser asked what would happen when the cupboard became full.

Policy said that would demonstrate success.

Treasury said it would demonstrate a storage pressure.

Legal said it would demonstrate nothing until minutes had been approved.

Communications said fullness was a powerful image, as long as nobody asked full of what.

## CLIMAX

The afternoon press conference began beside the cupboard, which had been polished until it reflected responsibility.

The Prime Minister said: "This government is putting progress where people can see that we have put it somewhere."

A reporter asked whether any of the items proved delivery.

Communications signalled "evidence".

Policy signalled "journey".

Legal signalled "cupboard only".

The Prime Minister said: "Each item has been through a robust process of being identified, formatted, and placed."

Another reporter asked whether the cupboard contained results or intentions.

The Cabinet Secretary stepped forward and explained that results and intentions were both part of the wider progress family.

Digital refreshed Cupboard Live.

It displayed:

PUBLIC REASSURANCE: STOCKED.

SHELF ALIGNMENT: STRONG.

DELIVERY VISIBILITY: PARTIALLY OPEN.

CONTENT CERTAINTY: UNDER REVIEW.

A reporter asked to inspect the sealed envelope.

Legal said the envelope was sealed in order to protect the openness of the process.

Treasury said unsealing it might require a new envelope.

The junior adviser quietly noticed the cupboard door would not close because of the representative bridge.

Communications said that was perfect.

The Prime Minister concluded that the National Progress Cupboard proved the government had not hidden progress.

It had stored it prominently.

## TAG

By evening, every department had requested its own cupboard.

The Cabinet Office announced a National Cupboard Strategy to prevent uncontrolled delivery storage.

**END**
