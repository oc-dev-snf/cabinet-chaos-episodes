# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 107
**Title:** The Neutral Corridor Briefing Protocol
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

A junior aide wheels a whiteboard into a corridor outside the press room.

The board reads:
- **TODAY'S LINE TO TAKE (DRAFT)**
- **TODAY'S BACKUP LINE TO TAKE (WORKING DRAFT)**
- **TODAY'S LINE ABOUT WHY THE LINE TO TAKE IS LATE**

A second aide arrives with a second whiteboard labeled **AUTHORIZED ERASER HOLDER**.

Cut to opening titles.

## ACT ONE

After several mixed messages on a routine policy update, officials launch the **Neutral Corridor Briefing Protocol**.

Official aim: ensure all public lines are coordinated before anyone reaches a microphone.

Unspoken aim: stop three departments giving four versions of the same sentence by lunchtime.

The protocol defines three roles:
- Drafting Unit: writes the initial line
- Alignment Unit: confirms wording consistency
- Delivery Unit: says the line out loud with confidence

A fourth role appears by accident:
- Clarification Unit: explains what everyone meant once the line has already gone out

Comms calls this “dynamic message resilience.”

## ACT TWO

A live rehearsal is scheduled.

Scenario: reporters ask whether the new initiative starts now, soon, or after consultation.

The Drafting Unit prepares:
“Implementation begins at pace, subject to practical sequencing.”

The Alignment Unit edits it to:
“Implementation pacing begins practically, subject to sequence.”

The Delivery Unit receives both versions and a sticky note saying:
“Use whichever sounds calmer.”

During the rehearsal, each participant delivers a different answer that is technically compatible but emotionally incompatible.

An observer marks the drill “successful with learnings.”

## ACT THREE

By afternoon, concern grows about who can authorize final wording.

A temporary governance map is produced with arrows between teams and a legend that says:
- Solid line: approval authority
- Dashed line: advisory authority
- Dotted line: “constructive visibility”

Most lines are dotted.

A minister asks for one accountable owner.

Officials propose a shared ownership model called **Collective Single Point Accountability**.

The minister asks what that means.

An aide replies, “It means no single surprise, only coordinated surprise.”

## CLIMAX

At the evening briefing, the spokesperson announces:

“We have introduced a robust protocol that guarantees coherence under pressure.”

A reporter asks which team approved that sentence.

The spokesperson checks notes and says, “It was cleared through the corridor.”

Another reporter asks for the written protocol.

A binder is produced titled:
**Neutral Corridor Briefing Protocol v1.0 (Interim Final Draft)**

The first page reads: “All statements require final confirmation.”

The second page reads: “Final confirmation pathway pending.”

## TAG

At 8:11 p.m., staff receive an update email:

**Subject:** Clarification on Final Confirmation Pathway (Temporary Permanent Guidance)

- Drafting Unit may draft pre-final lines
- Alignment Unit may align pre-aligned drafts
- Delivery Unit may deliver conditionally aligned lines
- Any dispute escalates to the Morning Huddle, regardless of time

Postscript:

“Please avoid discussing this protocol in corridors until corridor use guidance is updated.”

**END**
