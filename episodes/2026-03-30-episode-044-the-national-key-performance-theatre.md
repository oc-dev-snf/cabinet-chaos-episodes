# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 044
**Title:** The National Key Performance Theatre
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET PERFORMANCE WAR ROOM. Three giant screens show arrows, pie charts, and one unexplained emoji.)

**CALLUM:** Why is there a smoke machine in my briefing room?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** The Delivery Unit says performance updates need “atmosphere.”

**POLICY LEAD:** Also, every department has submitted its top three Key Performance Indicators.

**CALLUM:** Good.

**POLICY LEAD:** None measure outcomes.

**CALLUM:** Bad.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** We have metrics for “emails acknowledged,” “meetings initiated,” and “documents moved to folder titled ACTIONED.”

**CALLUM:** Actioned by whom?

**OPS DIRECTOR:** The folder.

(A junior adviser wheels in a trophy labeled “Most Improved Dashboard Colour Palette.”)

**CALLUM:** Is that from Finance?

**AIDE:** No, from Transport. They beat Housing by 0.3 gradients.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

Emergency KPI alignment meeting. Eight officials, eleven acronyms, zero eye contact.

**CALLUM:** Right. We need fewer vanity metrics and more reality.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Reality polls poorly.

**POLICY LEAD:** Suggest a simple framework: what changed for people, how fast, and at what cost.

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** Counterproposal: a live leaderboard.

**CALLUM:** Of public outcomes?

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** Of departmental confidence levels.

**CALLUM:** That is mood tracking.

Whiteboard fills rapidly:
- **Input metrics:** money spent, staff assigned, coffee consumed.
- **Output metrics:** memos issued, guidance published, webinars attended by other civil servants.
- **Outcome metrics:** suspiciously blank.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** Departments say outcomes take time.

**CALLUM:** Fine. Then show leading indicators connected to actual delivery.

**POLICY LEAD:** They’ve offered “number of times delivery is mentioned in meetings.”

**CALLUM:** We are governing by word count.

## ACT TWO

A “National KPI Showcase” is launched to encourage transparency.

Every department presents one flagship metric.

Health reports: “Average waiting room playlist satisfaction up 22%.”

Education reports: “Homework policy clarity document downloaded 91,000 times.”

Justice reports: “Case-management portal login success vibes: stable.”

**CALLUM:** Where are the difficult numbers?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** In annexes.

**CALLUM:** Where are the annexes?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** In a portal.

**CALLUM:** Where is the portal?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Under maintenance for improvements to metric visibility.

Meanwhile, press office asks for one sentence on what changed this quarter.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** I can give them “cross-functional momentum.”

**CALLUM:** Can we give them a bus that arrived on time and a service that answered the phone?

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** That’s very specific.

## ACT THREE

Cabinet review. Callum demands a reset.

**CALLUM:** New rule: every metric must answer “so what?”

(Room reacts as if he has banned oxygen.)

**POLICY LEAD:** We can map each KPI to a real-world promise.

**OPS DIRECTOR:** And assign one owner per promise.

**DIGITAL MINISTER:** What about the leaderboard?

**CALLUM:** Keep one screen for morale. The other two are for reality.

They pilot a trimmed dashboard:
- Fewer indicators.
- Clear baselines.
- Deadlines with names next to them.
- Public explanation in plain language.

For the first time, a chart appears that everyone understands in under ten seconds.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** This is dangerously comprehensible.

**CALLUM:** That’s the point.

## CLIMAX

Press briefing. No smoke machine. Noticeably less theatre.

**CALLUM (to cameras):** We’ve simplified national performance reporting so people can see what is improving, what is not, and who is accountable.

**REPORTER:** Are you admitting previous dashboards were mostly decorative?

**CALLUM:** I’m saying they were high in visual confidence.

**REPORTER 2:** Will ministers lose bonuses for missing targets?

**CALLUM:** Ministers don’t get bonuses.

(Pause.)

**REPORTER 2:** Symbolic penalties then?

**CALLUM:** We’ve introduced a strict sanction: they must explain missed targets in plain English on camera.

(Room murmurs. Someone in the back whispers, “harsh.”)

## TAG

Late night. Office lights dim.

Callum opens the new dashboard on his laptop.

One tile reads:

**“Public service issue resolved this week: 1,342”**

He nods, almost impressed.

Then a push notification appears:

**“NEW KPI ADDED: Number of KPIs removed.”**

Current value: 0.

**CALLUM:** We’ve metricated the cure.

He closes the laptop.

**END**
