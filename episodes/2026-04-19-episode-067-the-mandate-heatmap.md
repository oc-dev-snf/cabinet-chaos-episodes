# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 067
**Title:** The Mandate Heatmap
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:01. A wall screen shows a giant color-coded map labelled **PUBLIC PRIORITIES LIVE**. Every region is flashing a different shade of orange.)

Morning briefing card:

**DELIVERY CONFIDENCE RESET — DAY 1**  
**Scenario:** *"The Mandate Heatmap"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is everything orange?

**DATA LEAD:** Orange means "needs attention."

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** They all need attention.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So you've built me a £2 million way of saying "do your job."

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE SCIENCE OF LOOKING BUSY

Strategy room, 08:19. Whiteboard reads: **VISIBLE PROGRESS, REAL PROGRESS**.

**PM:** Voters asked for delivery. We need proof by Friday.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Preferably proof that can survive a hostile interview.

**ANALYTICS DIRECTOR:** We can produce a national heatmap updated hourly.

**OPERATIONS DIRECTOR:** Or we can fix the three service bottlenecks causing 80% of complaints.

Beat.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Can we do the second one and pretend it was always the first one?

At 09:02 they list what the dashboard currently measures:

1. Number of meetings held
2. Number of updates posted
3. Number of stakeholders "engaged"
4. Number of slides produced
5. Number of times the word "delivery" appears in documents

**PM:** None of that is outcomes.

**ANALYTICS DIRECTOR:** It is activity adjacent to outcomes.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** That's not a category. That's a warning label.

## ACT TWO — THE METRIC INTERVENTION

Ops floor, 11:11. Slide title: **FROM SIGNAL TO NOISE RATIO**.

**SERVICE OWNER:** We tested what citizens actually feel.

**PM:** And?

**SERVICE OWNER:** Three things matter: response time, first-time resolution, and whether people get conflicting advice.

**COMMS LEAD:** We can explain those in one sentence each.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. If the public needs a glossary, we've already lost.

They redesign the heatmap logic:

**GREEN:** on target for response + resolution + consistency  
**AMBER:** one indicator slipping  
**RED:** two or more indicators failing  
**GREY:** no data because a team forgot to submit

**PM:** What happens if someone paints amber as success?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** We publish definitions on the same page.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Radical transparency. Hide the silverware.

By 12:27, first refresh goes live with fewer colors and plain labels.

Call from regional director:

**REGIONAL DIRECTOR:** We can finally tell what needs fixing today, not next quarter.

## ACT THREE — THE ACCOUNTABILITY CARNIVAL

Cross-department review, 14:36. Agenda: **WHO OWNS RED?**

**FACILITATOR:** Ground rule: no one may say "shared ownership" unless they can name a person.

General discomfort.

Findings:

- Teams respond faster when a named owner appears beside each metric
- Weekly averages hide daily service failures
- Some "improvements" came from changing definitions mid-month
- Public trust drops when targets move without explanation

**PM:** So we stop moving targets in the dark.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Publish change logs with reasons.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Good. If we alter the ruler, we announce it before measuring success with it.

Breaking ticker appears:

**"Government simplifies performance dashboard to focus on citizen outcomes."**

**COMMS LEAD:** That is true and suspiciously readable.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We'll open an inquiry into how that happened.

## ACT FOUR — THE 17:00 HONESTY TEST

Decision room, 16:58. Board heading: **NO SPIN, JUST STATUS**.

Protocol approved:

- Each red area gets one accountable owner and a 72-hour recovery plan
- Definitions locked for 30 days unless emergency change is justified
- Daily update published at a fixed time with a short public summary
- Metrics page includes "what this does not measure"
- Departments must log corrective action, not just narrative updates

By 18:09:

- Two long-standing bottlenecks moved from red to amber
- Call waiting times dropped by 18% in pilot regions
- Conflicting guidance incidents halved in one week
- Internal meeting volume down, field decisions up

**PM:** Is this politically comfortable?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** No.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Great. Comfort was never a key performance indicator.

## CLIMAX — THE BRIEFING

Press room, 19:04.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**  
Today we changed our dashboard to report outcomes people experience, not process people admire. If a service is slow, confusing, or contradictory, it will show as such.

**REPORTER:** Are you admitting the old system misled people?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I'm saying it reassured the building more than it reassured the country.

**REPORTER:** Will you miss your favorable metrics?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I prefer unfavorable facts to favorable fiction.

## TAG

21:07. Nearly empty office. A junior analyst edits the dashboard footer.

New line added:

**"If a number cannot trigger action, it is decoration."**

In the corridor, a monitor quietly turns three regions from amber to green.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (passing):** Look at that — evidence with consequences.

**END**
