# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 068
**Title:** The Briefing Soundboard
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:00. A prototype lectern has giant illuminated buttons labelled **STRONG PLAN**, **DELIVERING**, **MOVING FORWARD**, and **NEXT QUESTION**.)

Morning briefing card:

**MESSAGE DISCIPLINE SPRINT — DAY 1**  
**Scenario:** *"The Briefing Soundboard"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is the podium glowing like a nightclub fruit machine?

**DIGITAL ADVISER:** It's a support tool. One tap gives you approved phrasing.

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** For policy explanation?

**DIGITAL ADVISER:** Primarily for survival.

(PETER-LIKE MINISTER presses **MOVING FORWARD** by accident. A loud voice booms: "WE REMAIN FOCUSED ON DELIVERY.")

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Splendid. We've automated evasiveness.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE LANGUAGE EMERGENCY

Comms room, 08:12. Whiteboard reads: **CONSISTENCY BUILDS TRUST**.

**PM:** Last week three ministers described the same policy as "pilot," "rollout," and "historic reset."

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Markets dislike surprises. Voters dislike dictionaries.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** So we standardize wording across departments.

**OPERATIONS DIRECTOR:** Or we standardize the policy itself.

Silence.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Let's do both, in that order, before the order changes again.

At 08:47 they review common briefing failures:

1. Answering a different question from the one asked
2. Using three metaphors in one sentence
3. Announcing timelines no delivery team has seen
4. Saying "world-leading" without a comparative source
5. Promising updates that arrive as podcasts

**PM:** We need fewer slogans and more nouns.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Preferably nouns attached to functioning services.

## ACT TWO — THE PROTOTYPE

Press prep suite, 11:03. Slide title: **CLEAR ANSWER PROTOCOL (v0.9)**.

**DIGITAL ADVISER:** The soundboard is temporary. It nudges speakers toward plain language.

**POLICY LEAD:** Does it include actual facts?

**DIGITAL ADVISER:** Yes. Buttons now trigger short factual blocks, not just confidence noises.

Updated buttons:

- **WHAT CHANGED?**
- **WHO BENEFITS?**
- **WHEN STARTS?**
- **WHAT IF DELAYED?**
- **WHERE TO TRACK?**

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Now we're talking.

Pilot simulation:

**MOCK REPORTER:** Is this cut or a rebrand?

Minister hits **WHAT CHANGED?**

Prompt appears: *"Budget moved from consultancy to frontline staffing, region by region, starting May 1."*

**MOCK REPORTER:** Better. Slightly terrifyingly better.

By 12:21, they add a lockout: no timeline can be spoken unless it matches the published implementation plan.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** If it's not in the plan, it doesn't leave the lips.

## ACT THREE — THE CROSS-DEPARTMENT PANIC

Interdepartmental call, 14:14. Agenda: **ONE MESSAGE, MANY REALITIES**.

**TRANSPORT REP:** Your phrase "rapid deployment" means six months for us.

**HEALTH REP:** For us it means six weeks.

**TREASURY REP:** For us it means "after review." 

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** This is why the public thinks time is fictional.

They agree a shared translation table:

- **Immediate** = within 7 days
- **Short term** = within 90 days
- **This year** = before fiscal year end
- **Under review** = no decision yet, with decision date published

**PM:** If we can't define the words, we don't use the words.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Put the table on every release note.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Beautiful. Bureaucracy with subtitles.

Ticker appears:

**"Government introduces plain-language standard for policy briefings."**

**HEAD OF COMMS:** That's either reassuring or deeply incriminating.

## ACT FOUR — THE LIVE TEST

Press room, 16:59. Small sticker on podium reads: **ANSWER THE QUESTION ASKED**.

Protocol in force:

- First sentence must directly answer the question
- If uncertain, say what is known, unknown, and next update time
- No comparative claim without source
- Any changed timeline must include reason and mitigation
- Full transcript with citations published within one hour

By 18:02:

- Briefing length down 22%
- Clarification requests down 41%
- Contradictory follow-up statements: zero in pilot
- Social clips focus on policy details, not verbal detours

**PM:** Are we finally coherent?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Intermittently, but in a promising direction.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I'll take intermittent reality over consistent spin.

## CLIMAX — THE AFTERNOON LOBBY

18:37. Cameras up.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this new system just message control?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It's error control. Message control is what we had when everyone said different things with the same confidence.

**REPORTER:** So you'll admit uncertainty now?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Publicly and on schedule.

**REPORTER:** That's unusual.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So is being understood.

## TAG

20:46. Empty briefing room. Cleaner wipes fingerprints off the old soundboard.

Most buttons are gone. One remains.

Label: **SHOW THE WORK**

It doesn't play a slogan. It opens the implementation tracker.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (from doorway):** Keep that one.

**END**
