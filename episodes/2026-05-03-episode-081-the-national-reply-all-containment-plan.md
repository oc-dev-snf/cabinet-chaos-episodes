# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 081
**Title:** The National Reply-All Containment Plan
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE INCIDENT ROOM, 07:01. A wall of screens shows inboxes refreshing in real time.)

Morning briefing card:

**PUBLIC SERVICE DELIVERY WEEK — DAY 13**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Reply-All Containment Plan"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why do I have 214 emails titled "Noted" before breakfast?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** A distribution list test escaped containment.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** We are now in phase two: people replying-all to ask people to stop replying-all.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. A self-replicating bureaucracy.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — CASCADE GOVERNANCE

08:23. Emergency coordination call. Slide title: **"INBOX SATURATION EVENT"**.

**PM:** How many recipients were on the original message?

**DIGITAL LEAD:** 18,400, including legacy aliases no one remembers creating.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Several teams missed operational alerts because "Thanks" arrived in battalions.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Journalists are asking whether this is a cyber incident.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It is. A cyber incident caused by thumbs.

Rapid assessment identifies four structural faults:

1. Over-broad mailing lists with weak ownership
2. No guardrail on mass internal sends
3. Unclear norms for acknowledgement messages
4. Critical alerts sharing channels with general broadcast traffic

**PM:** We cannot run public services through an avalanche of courtesy.

## ACT TWO — CONTAINMENT ARCHITECTURE

10:41. Joint task group assembled: digital operations, private offices, HR, security, and departmental IT leads.

Slide title: **"FEWER RECIPIENTS, BETTER SIGNAL"**

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Apply send-threshold controls: above 250 recipients requires confirmation plus policy prompt.

**HR LEAD:** Standardise acknowledgement etiquette: use reactions or manager roll-ups, not mass replies.

**SECURITY LEAD:** Split urgent operational alerts onto protected channels with elevated priority markers.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Assign list owners with monthly audits and auto-expiry on dormant groups.

Three immediate controls approved:

- **Mass-send confirmation gate** for large distribution lists
- **Reply-all warning banner** with plain-language consequences
- **Critical-channel separation** for incidents and service alerts

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We are now regulating the national impulse to be polite in public.

## ACT THREE — LIVE TEST

12:16. Controlled simulation across 22 departments.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Inject one broad informational email and one urgent alert in parallel.

12:19. Informational email lands.

**SYSTEM PROMPT (simulated):** "Reply-all reaches 12,620 people. Continue?"

Reply-all attempts drop sharply.

12:24. Urgent alert issued through protected channel.

**INCIDENT COORDINATOR:** Alert acknowledged by all duty teams in under three minutes. No inbox spillover.

12:31. Simulation closes with full decision log.

Follow-up package agreed:

- Weekly list-owner hygiene checks
- Mandatory onboarding module: "Internal Messaging Discipline"
- Monthly dashboard: mass sends, reply-all events, alert latency
- 60-day review of legacy aliases and dormant groups

**PM:** If we can reduce email volume and increase useful information, that's practically constitutional reform.

## ACT FOUR — COMMUNICATING THE UNEXCITING WIN

16:48. Press line approved: **"GOVERNMENT INTRODUCES INTERNAL MESSAGE SAFETY CONTROLS"**.

Public-facing points emphasize:

- Better separation of critical alerts from general traffic
- Reduced accidental disruption from large-list replies
- Clear accountability for list ownership
- Faster routing of operational information
- Practical standards that support staff focus

By 18:03 (pilot metrics):

- Reply-all incidents down 72%
- Critical alert acknowledgment speed improved 39%
- Non-essential broadcast traffic down 44%
- Time spent triaging inbox noise down 33%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** It's hard to make "fewer emails" sound heroic.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Quiet competence rarely trends, but it does keep the lights on.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:27. Reporters gather by the main entrance.

**REPORTER:** Minister, are inbox rules really a national priority?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Communication systems are infrastructure. When they clog, decisions slow.

**REPORTER:** Critics say this is managerial overreach.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Standards are not overreach; they are how large systems remain usable.

**REPORTER:** Is this temporary?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It's routine maintenance with measurable outcomes.

## TAG

20:09. New footer appears on government-wide emails: **"SEND WITH INTENT. SHARE BY NEED."**

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (off-screen):** Tomorrow: calendar invites with no owner, no agenda, and seventeen optional observers.
