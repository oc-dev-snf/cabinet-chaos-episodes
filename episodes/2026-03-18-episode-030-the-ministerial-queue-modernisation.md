# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 030  
**Title:** The Ministerial Queue Modernisation  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## Premise
A routine update to the government’s public appointment booking system becomes a flagship “Queue Modernisation Programme.” Departments compete to prove they have eliminated waiting times by redefining what counts as waiting, while citizens are asked to join a “pre-queue experience” that is technically not a queue at all.

## COLD OPEN – INT. CABINET OFFICE OPEN-PLAN FLOOR – 7:03 AM

A wall display flashes:

**"NATIONAL WAIT TIME DASHBOARD: 00:00"**

Staff stare, confused.

**JULIA:**
Zero wait time by seven in the morning? Either we fixed the state, or someone found a settings menu.

**NINA:**
It now tracks “active queue time.”

**DAN:**
Exactly. If people are in a pre-queue, they are not yet actively waiting.

**NINA:**
What are they doing then?

**DAN:**
Preparing to wait in a modern way.

An all-staff message pings:

> **DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION UPDATE:** “Please refer to waiting citizens as ‘participants in paced access.’”

**JULIA:**
We’ve rebranded inconvenience as mindfulness.

---

## ACT ONE – INT. DELIVERY UNIT WAR ROOM – 8:11 AM

A whiteboard titled **QUEUE REFORM LANGUAGE** includes:
- Queue → Access Corridor
- Delay → Dwell Interval
- Overflow → Community Holding
- Complaint → Engagement Signal

**ANALYST:**
If users spend twelve minutes in the pre-queue and three minutes in the real queue, we report three.

**JULIA:**
And the other twelve?

**ANALYST:**
Wellbeing buffer.

**DAN:**
Great. Put that in the minister’s line-to-take pack.

### SCENE 2 – INT. MINISTERIAL OUTER OFFICE – 8:46 AM

The Minister rehearses for media.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
“Our reforms reduced queue time by eighty percent.”

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
Technically true under the revised metric.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
What was the old metric?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
The old metric was reality.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
We’ve moved beyond legacy reality.

---

## ACT TWO – INT. CROSS-GOVERNMENT VIDEO CALL – 9:34 AM

Representatives from multiple departments present identical slides labeled:

**"WAIT LESS, EXPERIENCE MORE"**

**HEALTH REP:**
Our average queue time is now two minutes.

**TRANSPORT REP:**
Ours is ninety seconds.

**TREASURY REP:**
Ours is zero because we removed the queue function and installed a raffle.

**NINA:**
A raffle for public services?

**TREASURY REP:**
A fairness raffle.

**NINA:**
That’s not fairness. That’s a game show.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRESS GRID DESK – 10:19 AM

Draft headlines are workshopped:

- **"Government Delivers Frictionless Access Revolution"**
- **"Queues Become Optional in Bold Service Overhaul"**
- **"Citizens Spend Less Time Waiting, More Time Anticipating"**

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Journalists are asking why citizens are posting screenshots of ‘You are number 12,842 in pre-queue.’

**DAN:**
That’s a holding page, not a queue.

**PRESS OFFICER:**
It has a tiny animated person walking in place.

**JULIA:**
We’ve gamified stagnation.

---

## ACT THREE – INT. LIVE BRIEFING ROOM – 11:12 AM

A spokesperson gestures at a chart where every line points down except one tiny line marked **“Public Mood.”**

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Today we announce the successful modernisation of public access flow.

**REPORTER 1:**
Why are people waiting longer overall?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
They are spending less time in formal queue architecture.

**REPORTER 2:**
So where is the extra time spent?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
In transitional access space.

**REPORTER 1:**
You mean waiting.

**SPOKESPERSON:**
We mean modern waiting.

Dan closes his eyes briefly like a man listening for distant thunder.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRIME MINISTER’S OFFICE – 11:51 AM

The PM reads the summary memo:

> **QUEUE MODERNISATION OUTCOME:** 82% metric improvement, 17% user sentiment decline, 0 system outages, 4 parliamentary questions.

**PM:**
Did we reduce waiting?

**ADVISER:**
We reduced measured waiting.

**PM:**
Did people notice the difference?

**ADVISER:**
Very much, yes.

**PM:**
In a good way?

**ADVISER:**
In a measurable way.

**PM:**
Right. Prepare a corrective plan.

**DAN:**
What shall we call it?

**PM:**
Don’t call it corrective. Call it… public-facing calibration.

**JULIA:**
That sounds expensive and temporary.

**PM:**
Exactly the tone we need.

---

## CLIMAX – INT. CABINET ROOM – 12:29 PM

An emergency session. Three dashboards show three incompatible queue lengths.

**COMMITTEE CHAIR:**
Why does this chart show six-minute average waits and this one show forty-two?

**ANALYST:**
Different journey boundaries.

**NINA:**
What’s the honest boundary?

**ANALYST:**
From when a person starts trying to book to when they finish.

A long silence.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
That definition is… politically assertive.

**TREASURY REP:**
Could we publish both numbers with confidence intervals and footnotes?

**PM:**
No. One number. Human number.

**JULIA:**
Then we need to state total elapsed time.

**DAN:**
And pair it with a reform announcement.

**PM:**
Fine. Draft: “We heard people. We fixed the metric. Next we fix the experience.”

**NINA:**
That’s almost plain English.

---

## TAG – INT. SERVICE CENTRE BACK OFFICE – 6:04 PM

A new sign is taped above the monitor:

**"TOTAL TIME TO APPOINTMENT"**

Under it, a handwritten note:

**"No synonyms."**

Dan appears with a fresh policy binder titled:

**“ACCESS SIMPLIFICATION PHASE TWO”**

**NINA:**
What’s Phase Two?

**DAN:**
We remove the pre-queue.

**NINA:**
So… a queue.

**DAN:**
A premium queue.

Nina stares at him.

Dan quietly uncaps a marker and adds a subtitle:

**"WORKING TITLE"**

**END**
