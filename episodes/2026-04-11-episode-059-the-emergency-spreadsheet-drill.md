# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 059
**Title:** The Emergency Spreadsheet Drill
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:02. A wall screen flashes red. Every desk printer starts producing color-coded pie charts at high speed.)

Emergency banner:

**NATIONAL READINESS EXERCISE — LIVE**
**Scenario:** *"The Emergency Spreadsheet Drill"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why are there seventeen dashboards and none of them agree on how many dashboards exist?

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Different teams track readiness differently.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** One says "stable," one says "caution," one says "vibes improving."

**AIDE:** The Treasury sheet has replaced all numbers with confidence emojis.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Brilliant. We have converted crisis planning into decorative accounting.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — METRICS WITHOUT MERCY

War room. Whiteboard heading: **IF EVERYTHING IS PRIORITY, NOTHING IS**

**PM:** Explain what happened in one sentence.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** We merged departmental readiness trackers into a single real-time workbook.

**CHIEF WHIP:** And?

**DIGITAL LEAD:** It now refreshes every twenty seconds and overwrites itself.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So the nation is defended by a haunted spreadsheet.

By 08:30, consequences arrive:

1. Regional teams receive three conflicting action lists
2. Helplines are told to quote "provisional confidence intervals" to callers
3. A flood response table auto-sorts by font size
4. Broadcast media calls it "Excelpocalypse"
5. Trending tag: **#StopSharingTheSheet**

**PM:** Freeze it.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** We can't. Twelve people are in it live and someone enabled collaborative macros.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Of course. Nothing says resilience like anonymous script execution.

## ACT TWO — THE LANGUAGE PROBLEM

Comms room. Draft statements on screen.

**DRAFT V1:** "A synchronized preparedness data harmonization event is underway."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** That's not English, it's camouflage.

**DRAFT V2:** "Our emergency spreadsheet broke and confused everyone."

**CHIEF WHIP:** Brutal. Accurate. Slightly too honest for breakfast.

**DRAFT V3:** "A planning data tool generated inconsistent guidance during an exercise. We have paused updates and restored manual command channels."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** V3. Print it before legal finds adjectives.

Then a slide leak appears online:

**"KPI STACK: CONFIDENCE / ALIGNMENT / NARRATIVE READINESS"**

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** People think we rehearsed press lines more than logistics.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** To be fair, we did both badly.

## ACT THREE — THE HEARING REHEARSAL

Committee prep, 13:10.

**CHAIR (speakerphone):** Minister, did your department rely on unvalidated spreadsheet automation for emergency coordination?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We relied on a tool intended to support decisions, not replace them. In practice, that boundary blurred. We are correcting it.

**OPPOSITION MP:** Was public safety ever at risk?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** This was an exercise, which is exactly why failure here is useful if we actually learn from it.

**OPPOSITION MP:** Why was manual fallback not immediate?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because process drift is real, and we let convenience outrun control.

Legal note arrives:

Replace "failed" with "performed below expected coherence threshold."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. It failed. The sentence is shorter and truer.

## ACT FOUR — BORING FIXES, REAL IMPROVEMENT

Recovery plan signed off:

- Lock national exercises to one read-only command view
- Separate analysis sheets from live operational instructions
- Require named owner + timestamp for each status change
- Ban macros in incident-mode workbooks
- Run quarterly no-digital fallback drills with published outcomes

By 16:45:

- Regional commands report aligned instruction sets
- Exercise completion recovers to schedule
- Public criticism shifts from "dangerous" to "embarrassing but fixable"

**PM:** Can we describe this as modernization in progress?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We can describe it as a reminder: systems help, people decide.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Evening press wants one line.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Give them: reliability first, dashboards second.

## CLIMAX — THE BRIEFING

Press briefing, 18:22.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**
Today, during a national preparedness exercise, a shared planning tool produced inconsistent operational guidance.

No live incident was affected, but the exercise exposed weak controls. We paused the tool, restored manual command channels, and completed the drill using verified instructions.

We are now introducing a simple rule: in emergency operations, clarity beats cleverness. Every critical instruction must have a named owner, a timestamp, and a fallback path.

**REPORTER:** Who approved the original tool?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Approval records will be published with the after-action review.

**REPORTER:** Is this another case of style over substance?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Substance is the only thing that counts when decisions are time-critical.

## TAG

22:41. Mostly dark office. One desk lamp on.

An intern delivers a folder marked:

**"EXERCISE UPGRADE PACK — AI AUTO-BRIEF GENERATOR"**

Inside: a prototype that turns every risk into "opportunity adjacent." 

Peter-like minister sighs, writes across the cover:

**"TRY LESS MAGIC. ADD MORE CHECKLISTS."**

Places it on the shelf labeled **LATER, MAYBE, NEVER**.

Phone buzzes with headline:

**"Government Retires ‘Chaotic Sheet,’ Promises Plain-Text Commands."**

He nods once.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Progress: now measurable by fewer tabs.

**END**
