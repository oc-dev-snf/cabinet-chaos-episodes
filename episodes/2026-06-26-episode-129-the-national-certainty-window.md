# Cabinet Chaos Episode 129: The National Certainty Window

## Cold Open
At 07:00, the Cabinet Office announced the National Certainty Window, a carefully timed period during which the government would provide clarity, subject to clarity being available during the window.

The Prime Minister arrived with the Agenda Certainty folder, the readiness receipt, the National Reassurance Dashboard, and yesterday's grey Progress Label, which had been placed in a transparent sleeve to make it look more decisive. Communications said the public wanted certainty. Policy said certainty was best offered in controlled amounts. Treasury asked whether certainty created expectations, because expectations had a habit of becoming invoices.

The Permanent Secretary explained that the window would not guarantee a decision. It would guarantee a recognised opportunity for decision-shaped language.

Everyone liked "recognised opportunity." It sounded open without implying anyone had to climb through it.

## The Briefing
The opening slide read "Certainty, Responsibly Timed." Beneath it were three proposed window sizes: narrow, balanced, and extended.

Communications preferred a narrow window because it sounded focused. Delivery preferred an extended window because it left room for something to happen. Legal asked whether a window could close before certainty arrived. Strategy said that would be managed through expectation ventilation. Treasury liked balanced because it implied nothing had been over-committed.

The Minister for Coordination asked whether departments would be expected to put actual dates inside the window. The room became alert in the way rooms become alert when a harmless noun has wandered near a deadline.

Policy proposed date ranges. Legal proposed "indicative date ranges." Communications proposed "moments." Digital offered a portal where citizens could see whether a certainty window was open, closing, under review, or subject to a glazing assessment. Procurement said glazing assessment might require specialist support.

The emergency affordability bell rang once when someone said "window architecture."

## The Complication
The trouble began when Transport asked for a certainty window around a timetable announcement, even though the timetable announcement was itself about improving certainty around timetables.

Health requested a flexible window that could expand if the underlying position evolved. Education wanted a window that opened after stakeholder engagement but before stakeholders noticed they had been engaged. Digital asked whether the window could be embedded in the National Reassurance Dashboard. Communications said yes, provided the window looked calm and did not resemble a countdown.

By mid-morning, the window had developed fittings. A latch meant ministerial clearance. A curtain meant sensitive detail. Frosted glass meant information existed but could not yet be recognised from outside. A small plant on the sill meant confidence.

Delivery objected that the public might prefer certainty to window dressing. Communications said window dressing was a hostile phrase and should be replaced with "contextual framing." Legal agreed, noting that windows without framing could be structurally risky. Treasury asked whether the plant was real.

Nobody knew.

## The Spin Room
At lunchtime, the spokesperson stood before a lectern labelled "Certainty Window: Open In Principle."

"The government is creating a clearer route for people to understand when they can expect more detail," they said.

A reporter asked when the public would receive the detail. The spokesperson said the window would indicate the responsible period for further indication. Asked whether that meant today, they said today was within the wider family of possible days. Asked whether the government had made a decision, they said the decision process was progressing through the certainty environment.

Another reporter asked if the window was just a delay with better lighting. Communications had prepared for this.

"It is not a delay," the spokesperson said. "It is a transparency mechanism with timing discipline."

Within minutes, the phrase became an online weather forecast: "Transparency Mechanism With Timing Discipline, Chance Of Detail Later." Strategy said forecasts were useful because nobody blamed the sky for moving.

## Corridor Conversations
In the corridor, the Chancellor asked whether certainty could be announced without funding. Treasury said certainty could be announced freely if it was about the existence of a process, less freely if it was about the existence of money.

The Minister for Delivery carried a draft list of completed items and asked whether those could go straight to the public. Communications said completed items were powerful but disruptive, because they created a precedent for completion. Policy suggested placing them inside a "confidence alcove." The minister asked whether an alcove was smaller than a window. Legal said it depended on the building regulations of the metaphor.

The paper diary minister proposed writing the window in the diary and drawing a box around it. Digital said the official window should be interactive. The minister replied that paper had excellent battery life and did not ask users to accept cookies. Procurement asked whether cookie acceptance would need its own window.

Meanwhile, an official discovered that the National Reassurance Dashboard had automatically coloured the certainty window teal. Legal asked whether teal now implied impending certainty. Communications said teal implied a mature relationship with imminence. Delivery quietly opened a real window.

## Closing Beat
By evening, the National Certainty Window entered controlled use. Departments were allowed to announce that a window was open, nearly open, preparing to open, or being assessed for future openness.

The Prime Minister recorded a statement promising that the public would receive clarity at the right time. Communications liked "right." Treasury liked "time" because it was cheaper than dates. Legal approved the statement after adding that a certainty window did not constitute a guarantee of certainty, timing, detail, decision, movement, daylight, or access.

In the final scene, the National Progress Label team asked whether the certainty window itself qualified as "Progress In View."

The dashboard refreshed.

The window showed grey.

Treasury called it balanced.

**Episode Notes**
- Tone: Satirical but even-handed; the joke stays on managed timing, process language, and the bureaucracy of promising clarity.
- Running theme: Certainty becomes a scheduled access period before anyone agrees what can safely be certain.
- Continuity hook: The Agenda Certainty folder, readiness receipt, National Reassurance Dashboard, Progress Label, and emergency affordability bell now feed into the National Certainty Window.
