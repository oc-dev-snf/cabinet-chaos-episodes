# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 033  
**Title:** The Office of Mutual Accountability  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## Premise
To prove government is finally joining up, the centre launches a new "Office of Mutual Accountability," where every department gets veto power over every other department’s plan. Coordination improves for nine minutes, then slows to a constitutional traffic jam.

## COLD OPEN – INT. CABINET OFFICE LOBBY – 7:03 AM

A vinyl banner hangs crooked over reception:

**"WELCOME TO THE OFFICE OF MUTUAL ACCOUNTABILITY"**

Below it, a smaller sign reads:

**"NO DECISION WITHOUT SHARED OWNERSHIP"**

A courier wheels in eight identical crates stamped:

**INTERDEPARTMENTAL COMMENT FORMS (REV 1.0)**

**SENIOR ADVISER:**
Are those all ours?

**RECEPTIONIST:**
No, those are just for this morning.

**POLICY LEAD:**
How many forms are there?

**RECEPTIONIST:**
One for each policy, multiplied by each department, plus a reflection annex.

**SENIOR ADVISER:**
We’ve weaponised stationery.

---

## ACT ONE – INT. JOINT DELIVERY HUB – 8:14 AM

A giant screen shows a workflow map titled:

**"DECISION LIFECYCLE (SIMPLIFIED)"**

It loops back on itself three times.

**OPERATIONS ANALYST:**
Before today, sign-off required two departments.

**DIRECTOR:**
And now?

**OPERATIONS ANALYST:**
All of them. For resilience.

**DIRECTOR:**
Resilience against what?

**OPERATIONS ANALYST:**
Being blamed alone.

### SCENE 2 – INT. MINISTERIAL OUTER OFFICE – 8:52 AM

A private secretary flips through a briefing pack thicker than a paving slab.

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
You’re speaking at ten. Line to take is, "This creates clear accountability."

**MINISTER:**
Does it?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
Not in practice. But in diagram form, yes.

**MINISTER:**
How many people can block this policy?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:**
Technically everyone. Which means no one is excluded from ownership.

**MINISTER:**
And no one is excluded from delay.

---

## ACT TWO – INT. CROSS-GOVERNMENT CLEARANCE CALL – 9:36 AM

Thirty-one participants. Twenty-seven muted. Four saying "just quickly" before beginning long speeches.

**CHAIR:**
Agenda item one: approve the pilot guidance note.

**TRANSPORT REP:**
Content is fine, but we can’t approve without legal seeing paragraph seven.

**LEGAL REP:**
Legal is content with seven but needs procurement input on paragraph three.

**PROCUREMENT REP:**
We’re broadly comfortable, pending strategic comms on tone.

**COMMS REP:**
Tone is strong, pending transport assurance that this isn’t transport policy.

**TRANSPORT REP:**
We can’t provide that assurance until legal signs seven.

A pause of pure institutional recursion.

**CHAIR:**
Right. So, conditionally approved, subject to final pre-clearance for preliminary agreement.

### SCENE 2 – INT. STRATEGY POD – 10:21 AM

A whiteboard split into three columns:

1. **Decision made**
2. **Decision deferred**
3. **Decision deferred pending decision**

Column three is full.

**SENIOR ADVISER:**
Can we simplify to one accountable lead per policy?

**DIRECTOR:**
That sounds dangerously comprehensible.

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Draft line: "Government has embedded collective challenge at every level."

**SENIOR ADVISER:**
Can we add, "and mostly at the same level"?

---

## ACT THREE – INT. PRESS BRIEFING ROOM – 11:13 AM

A spokesperson stands beside a slide headed:

**"CLEAR LINES OF ACCOUNTABILITY"**

Underneath is a network diagram resembling dropped spaghetti.

**REPORTER 1:**
Who is in charge of this programme?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
All participating departments are jointly empowered.

**REPORTER 2:**
Who takes responsibility if it fails?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
We reject the language of failure.

**REPORTER 2:**
Fine. Who takes responsibility if it succeeds?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
We celebrate success collectively.

Backstage, an aide quietly closes a laptop that has frozen on "Loading stakeholder comments (1,842)."

### SCENE 2 – INT. PM’S OFFICE – 11:49 AM

A one-page update lands on the desk:

> **MUTUAL ACCOUNTABILITY OFFICE – DAY ONE**  
> Participation: complete  
> Ownership: universal  
> Throughput: limited  
> Number of unresolved comments: rising confidently

**PM:**
Why does every decision now need everyone?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:**
Because every department asked for a safeguard.

**PM:**
Against what?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:**
Future headlines and past colleagues.

**PM:**
So the system is safe for careers, not for clocks.

---

## CLIMAX – INT. CABINET ROOM – 12:31 PM

Emergency review. Coffee cups, red folders, silent dread.

**CABINET SECRETARY:**
Option A: keep full mutual sign-off. Maximum consent, minimal speed.

**TREASURY REP:**
Unaffordable in working hours.

**SERVICE REP:**
Unusable in real time.

**CABINET SECRETARY:**
Option B: primary owner model. One lead, two challengers, fixed response windows.

**SENIOR ADVISER:**
If challengers don’t respond in forty-eight hours, silence counts as agreement.

Half the room visibly processes the concept of deadlines.

**PM:**
Do it. Shared input, named owner, clock starts immediately.

**CABINET SECRETARY:**
And if departments object?

**PM:**
They can object on time.

---

## TAG – INT. CABINET OFFICE CORRIDOR – 6:06 PM

A new poster appears:

**"ACCOUNTABILITY REQUIRES A NAME AND A DATE."**

Someone has added a sticky note beneath:

**"Can we workshop this wording cross-government?"**

A second sticky appears:

**"Only if one person owns the workshop."**

A third, in thick black marker:

**"Deadline: tomorrow, 10:00."**

For the first time all day, nobody objects.

**END**
