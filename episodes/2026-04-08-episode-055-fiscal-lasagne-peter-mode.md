# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 055
**Title:** Fiscal Lasagne (Peter Mode)
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE. 08:03. Everyone already looks like they’ve lost a by-election.)

**AIDE:** Minister, small issue. Draft budget pack got uploaded publicly.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** “Small issue” is what you call a tea shortage, not fiscal self-immolation.

**AIDE:** It was live for fourteen minutes.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Fourteen minutes online is geological time. That’s enough for ten think pieces, three memes, and someone selling novelty aprons.

A screen flashes trending hashtag: **#FiscalLasagne**

**COMMS:** They’re saying our plan is “layered nonsense, cold in the middle.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Splendid. We’ve been filleted by people with Wi-Fi and zero punctuation.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

Emergency huddle with PM, Chief Whip, and Comms Director.

**PM:** Containable?

**CHIEF WHIP:** If by containable you mean “will this ruin lunch,” yes. If you mean “will this ruin Thursday,” no.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We need one honest line, not six cowardly ones.

**COMMS:** “Iterative transparency process”?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** That sounds like we dropped a filing cabinet down a staircase and called it citizen engagement.

Leaked horrors on the table:

- Kettle levy
- Rural transport “demand optimisation”
- School lunch “nutritional autonomy”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Whoever coined “nutritional autonomy” should be banned from nouns for six months.

## ACT TWO

Select Committee hearing. Hostile room. Dry mouths.

**CHAIR:** Is this document genuine?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Genuine draft. Not genuine policy.

**CHAIR:** Why does it say “clouds are a fiscal externality until 2028”?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because at 2 a.m. economists become poets and spreadsheets become crime scenes.

**CHAIR:** Did you model cuts to disability transport?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Draft options were tested. Those options were rejected.

**CHAIR:** Who approved publication?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Nobody. That is the point of the scandal.

In the back row, a journalist shouts:

**JOURNALIST:** So why draft bad ideas at all?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because government is choosing among imperfect options, not writing fan fiction for idealists.

## ACT THREE

Back in the bunker, damage plan:

1. Publish final principles today, in plain English.
2. Pull forward two popular policies.
3. Name accountable owners for every correction.
4. Open assumptions to external review.

**PM:** Are we apologising?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Yes. Briefly. Clearly. No interpretive dance.

**CHIEF WHIP:** And blame?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Mine, operationally. Ours, institutionally. The internet’s, spiritually.

Prime-time interview.

**INTERVIEWER:** Is government making policy by accident?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. Government is making policy under pressure, with scrutiny, while half the country live-tweets the draft margins.

**INTERVIEWER:** Harmless embarrassment?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Embarrassment is a bruise. Trust is a bone.

## CLIMAX

Evening debrief. Polling flatlines instead of collapsing.

**COMMS:** Focus groups say you sounded “sober and competent.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** That is the least erotic compliment in the English language.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Fire mostly out.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Good. Let’s now govern like adults and panic like professionals.

## TAG

(10:49 PM. Minister alone. Cold tea. Red box untouched.)

Notification: **“#FiscalLasagne aprons now available.”**

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (text):** If I see one in Westminster, I’m moving to a lighthouse and communicating only by foghorn.

He shuts the laptop.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Right. Same chaos tomorrow, but with fewer adjectives.

**END**
