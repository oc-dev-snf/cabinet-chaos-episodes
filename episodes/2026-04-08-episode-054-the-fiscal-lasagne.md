# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 054
**Title:** The Fiscal Lasagne
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET COMMS BUNKER. A giant monitor shows a trending tag: **#FiscalLasagne**.)

**PRESS OFFICER:** Tiny issue. Draft budget notes were uploaded to the public portal.

**CALLUM:** The one called “public” in giant letters?

**PRESS OFFICER:** Yes.

**CALLUM:** For how long?

**PRESS OFFICER:** Fourteen minutes.

**CALLUM:** Long enough for the internet to name it, meme it, and print it on tea towels.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We’re being described as “layered nonsense, cold in the middle.”

**CALLUM:** Brutal. Also annoyingly concise.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

Emergency meeting with PM, Whip, and comms.

**PM:** Tell me this is containable.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Depends. In this building, yes. In public, no.

**CALLUM:** We need one line that isn’t evasive and isn’t suicidal.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** “Iterative transparency exercise?”

**CALLUM:** That sounds like we dropped a filing cabinet down the stairs and called it consultation.

The leak includes three radioactive bullets:

- A proposal to tax domestic electric kettles.
- Rural transport reductions framed as “demand optimisation.”
- School lunch changes described as “nutritional autonomy.”

**CALLUM:** Whoever coined “nutritional autonomy” should be banned from verbs.

## ACT TWO

Select Committee hearing. Cameras, hostile MPs, and one mug of tea going cold on cue.

**CHAIR:** Is the leaked document authentic?

**CALLUM:** It was a working draft, not settled policy.

**CHAIR:** Why does it include this line: “Cloud capacity is a fiscal externality until 2028”?

**CALLUM:** That line was not ministerially endorsed.

**CHAIR:** Did your department draft cuts to disability transport?

**CALLUM:** No final decision was taken, and no such measure is in the final package.

A journalist shouts from the back:

**JOURNALIST:** Then why draft any of this?

**CALLUM:** Because serious governments test bad options in private so they can reject them in public. The leak reversed that order.

In the bunker, comms watches clips go live.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** You’re trending as “the minister who said the quiet part with grammar.”

**CALLUM:** I’ll take grammar as a win.

## ACT THREE

Counter-plan approved before evening bulletins:

1. Publish final budget principles early, with plain-language notes.
2. Announce two high-support measures to change the cycle.
3. Put named owners on every correction and timeline.
4. Open the modelling assumptions for external review.

**PM:** Are we apologising?

**CALLUM:** Yes. Cleanly. No legal origami.

**CHIEF WHIP:** And accountability?

**CALLUM:** Mine. My office. Our fix.

Prime-time interview.

**INTERVIEWER:** Is this government making policy by accident?

**CALLUM:** No. We’re making policy under pressure, in daylight, with scrutiny. That process is messy. The final decisions still follow evidence, cabinet agreement, and law.

**INTERVIEWER:** Was the leak harmless?

**CALLUM:** No. Embarrassment fades. Trust doesn’t auto-repair.

## CLIMAX

Late briefing with revised lines and updated polling.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Focus groups say you sounded “responsible under fire.”

**CALLUM:** Please never workshop me into a motivational poster.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Damage is stabilising. Not fixed, stabilising.

**CALLUM:** Good. We’re not here to win the evening. We’re here to be credible on Friday.

## TAG

(10:47 PM. OFFICE LIGHTS LOW. A notification pings.)

**AIDE (text):** “Crisis mostly contained. Also #FiscalLasagne merch now exists.”

**CALLUM (text):** “If I see my face on an apron, I’m resigning to become a lighthouse keeper.”

He closes the laptop, looks at tomorrow’s red box, and sighs.

**CALLUM:** Right. Another day of functional chaos, then.

**END**
