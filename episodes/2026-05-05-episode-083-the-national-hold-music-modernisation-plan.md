# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 083
**Title:** The National Hold Music Modernisation Plan
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE INCIDENT ROOM, 07:00. A status board flashes: **"PUBLIC CONTACT PERFORMANCE — AMBER"**.)

Morning briefing card:

**CITIZEN SERVICE WEEK — DAY 15**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Hold Music Modernisation Plan"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is there a cross-government alert about pan flutes?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because average call waiting time hit 19 minutes and the same 12-second music loop appears to be worsening public mood.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Complaints describe it as "audio déjà vu with administrative consequences."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So national confidence is being eroded by a recorder solo.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE SOUND OF SYSTEM STRAIN

08:11. Emergency call with service directors, digital leads, and contact centre managers.

Slide title: **"WAIT EXPERIENCE AS OPERATIONAL RISK"**

**PM:** Are we now governing by playlist?

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** We are governing call abandonment, escalation spikes, and repeated contact. The playlist is the symptom.

**HEAD OF CONTACT SERVICES:** People hang up, retry, then join a new queue. That multiplies load.

**DATA ANALYST:** Channels with shorter queue messaging and clearer updates show lower repeat call rates.

Four drivers identified:

1. Static hold loops with no useful queue information
2. Inconsistent callback options across departments
3. Peak-hour staffing misaligned with demand patterns
4. Script-heavy agent handovers causing avoidable delay

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We've built a maze and then supplied it with elevator jazz.

## ACT TWO — THE PLAN

10:27. Cross-functional task group convened: operations, digital, comms, service design, and workforce planning.

Slide title: **"KEEP PEOPLE INFORMED, NOT IMPRISONED"**

**SERVICE DESIGN LEAD:** Replace passive looping audio with short rotating updates: expected wait, callback availability, and online alternatives.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Standardise callback option by minute three across all major helplines.

**WORKFORCE PLANNER:** Shift break schedules and lunch coverage to protect the 11:30–14:00 surge.

Three immediate controls approved:

- **Dynamic queue messaging** with 90-second update intervals
- **Unified callback policy** with transparent position retention
- **Peak-hour roster rebalance** for high-volume lines

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Finally, policy that sounds better because it behaves better.

## ACT THREE — PILOT LAUNCH

12:03. Pilot starts across 14 high-volume public lines.

**INCIDENT COORDINATOR:** Monitoring abandonment rate, repeat calls, and first-contact resolution.

12:18. Dynamic messaging goes live.

**CONTACT CENTRE SUPERVISOR:** Immediate drop in "operator please" interruptions.

13:02. Callback option reaches full deployment in pilot group.

**SERVICE METRICS LEAD:** Repeat call attempts down in first hour; queue churn stabilising.

14:26. Staffing rebalance settles into rhythm.

**WORKFORCE PLANNER:** Average answer time improving without overtime spike.

Follow-up actions agreed:

- Daily queue-health dashboard for departmental leads
- Weekly script simplification review with frontline staff
- Monthly accessibility check for audio clarity and language
- Publishing a plain-English "what happens when you call" guide

**PM:** Turns out reassurance is a throughput tool.

## ACT FOUR — ANNOUNCING SOMETHING SENSIBLE

16:41. Communications drafts line: **"GOVERNMENT IMPROVES CONTACT EXPERIENCE WITH PRACTICAL SERVICE UPDATES"**

Public points cleared:

- Clear waiting information reduces uncertainty
- Callback options reduce repeat dialing and queue pressure
- Better peak staffing improves answer times
- Simpler handovers support faster resolution
- Service quality is measured in outcomes, not slogans

By 18:05 (pilot metrics):

- Call abandonment down 33%
- Repeat call attempts down 29%
- Average answer time down 24%
- First-contact resolution up 17%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** This is competent, measurable, and oddly difficult to sensationalise.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Perfect. Let's do more of that.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:22. Press gather outside briefing room.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this a rebrand of being on hold?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. It's a redesign of how we respect people's time.

**REPORTER:** Critics say hold music isn't a policy priority.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Waiting happens at scale. Improving it is operational policy.

**REPORTER:** Will this expand?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If the data keeps improving, yes.

## TAG

20:09. New standard line appears on pilot services: **"YOU CAN STAY ON THE LINE OR KEEP YOUR PLACE WITH A CALLBACK."**

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (off-screen):** Tomorrow: an interdepartmental summit on forms that ask the same question three different ways.
