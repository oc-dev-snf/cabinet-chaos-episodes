# Cabinet Chaos Episode 125: The National Clarity Rehearsal

## Cold Open
At 07:00, the Cabinet Office discovered that every department was ready to explain the new plan, provided nobody asked for the explanation yet.

The Prime Minister arrived with the Agenda Certainty folder, now reinforced with a second elastic band after a junior official described the first one as "symbolically stretched." Inside was a rehearsal schedule for the government's public-facing clarity moment. It had six columns, all headed "Message."

Communications said the public wanted a clear story. Policy said the story was clear in principle. Delivery said the principle was clear in theory. Treasury asked whether the theory had implications for spending. Legal asked whether "clear" implied an enforceable standard.

The Permanent Secretary announced the National Clarity Rehearsal: a controlled cross-government exercise to ensure that every minister could describe the announcement simply, consistently, and without accidentally creating a new announcement.

## The Briefing
The opening slide read "Confidence Through Pre-Clarity." Beneath it was a flowchart showing how an idea became a line, how a line became a position, and how a position became "the agreed direction of travel, pending exact wording."

The Minister for Coordination asked whether the rehearsal would include questions from the public. Communications said simulated questions would be safer. Policy said real questions could create fresh policy. Legal agreed, noting that unmanaged curiosity had a history of producing minutes.

Each department had been asked to prepare a single sentence. Health submitted one with three semicolons. Education submitted a sentence that began "At the heart of our approach" and was immediately referred to the Positioning Compass. Transport submitted "We are moving forward," which Delivery flagged as promising but hard to evidence while seated.

Treasury produced a sentence so concise that everyone became suspicious. It read: "Subject to affordability." The room admired its discipline and feared its accuracy.

## The Complication
The rehearsal began smoothly until the first minister was asked to explain the plan in plain English.

There was a pause. A special adviser coughed. Someone from Strategy whispered, "accessible ambition." The minister said the plan was about "unlocking delivery through a renewed focus on practical outcomes." Communications stopped the clock and asked for a version that could survive a kitchen radio.

The second attempt was shorter but accidentally promised a start date. The third avoided dates but introduced a new review. The fourth removed the review and implied funding. Treasury pressed the emergency affordability bell, which had been installed after the National Minutes Reconciliation Exercise and was meant to be used sparingly.

By mid-morning, the Clarity Rehearsal had produced twelve approved ways of saying the same thing. Legal said twelve was manageable if nobody claimed they were the same. Delivery proposed a Master Line. Communications preferred a Core Line. Policy suggested a Central Line, which was rejected after Transport asked if it required timetable approval.

## The Spin Room
By lunchtime, reporters had learned that the government was rehearsing clarity and wanted to know whether clarity was ready.

The spokesperson took the podium with a folder marked "Use If Asked Whether We Practised." It contained a single sheet and two paperclips arranged in a way that suggested confidence.

"Good government prepares carefully," they said. "This exercise is about making sure the public receives clear, consistent information at the right time."

Asked what the information was, the spokesperson said it would be shared at the right time. Asked whether the right time had been selected, they said timing was part of the clarity. Asked whether clarity could be clear before it was shared, they said the government would not provide a running commentary on clarity preparation.

The press team drafted three lines. For television: "We are focused on what matters." For radio: "The message is clear." For online: "No announcement has been rehearsed beyond normal standards." Legal added: "Rehearsal does not constitute publication."

## Corridor Conversations
In the corridor, the Chancellor asked whether clear communication made a proposal more expensive. Treasury said clarity could increase demand, demand could increase expectations, and expectations could arrive before the envelope had been located.

The Minister for Delivery argued that people might appreciate hearing one honest sentence. A senior official said honesty was important, but one sentence could be clipped, quoted, compared with previous sentences, and eventually understood. The sentence was therefore expanded into a "controlled clarity paragraph."

The paper diary minister suggested simply writing the plan on a sheet of paper and reading it aloud. Communications liked the authenticity. Legal asked who owned the paper. Digital asked whether the paper had a screen-reader equivalent. Procurement asked whether existing paper frameworks covered spontaneous sincerity.

Meanwhile, an official found an archived guide called "Plain Speaking For Complex Moments." It had been replaced by "Accessible Strategic Narrative Guidance," which had been replaced by a dashboard, which had been replaced by a holding line saying the dashboard remained under active consideration.

## Closing Beat
By evening, the National Clarity Rehearsal issued interim findings. Departments were cleared to say the plan was clear, provided they did not specify whether it was clear to ministers, officials, stakeholders, the public, or the person currently saying it.

The Prime Minister recorded a clip saying the government would speak plainly. Communications called it strong. Policy asked whether plainly meant simply or firmly. Treasury asked whether plainly committed them to anything. Legal approved the wording after adding that plain speech may contain necessary qualifications.

In the final scene, the Agenda Certainty folder was placed beside the compass, the readiness receipt, and the emergency affordability bell.

The bell rang once by itself.

Everyone agreed it had probably misunderstood the message.

**Episode Notes**
- Tone: Satirical but even-handed; the joke stays on institutional message rehearsal and the machinery of making simplicity complicated.
- Running theme: The system becomes more confident in the rehearsal of clarity than in the thing being clarified.
- Continuity hook: The Agenda Certainty folder now sits beside the Positioning Compass, readiness receipts, approved action vocabulary, and an emergency affordability bell.
