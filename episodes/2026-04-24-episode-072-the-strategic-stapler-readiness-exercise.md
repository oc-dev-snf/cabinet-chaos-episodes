**Episode:** 072
**Title:** The Strategic Stapler Readiness Exercise
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:00. A wall screen shows: **"NATIONAL DELIVERY WEEK — READINESS STATUS"**. Under it: **"CRITICAL DEPENDENCY: STAPLERS (ASSORTED)"**.)

Morning briefing card:

**GOVERNMENT OPERATING DISCIPLINE WEEK — DAY 4**  
**Scenario:** *"The Strategic Stapler Readiness Exercise"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is stationery on the national risk dashboard?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because three departments ran out yesterday and had to borrow from each other via courier.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** The courier was delayed by a meeting about reducing unnecessary courier traffic.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Good. We are failing efficiently.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE SUPPLY CHAIN OF SMALL THINGS

08:12. Cross-government operations room. Whiteboard reads: **MINOR FAILURES, MAJOR HEADACHES.**

**PM:** We keep tripping over tiny dependencies. Not policy disagreements. Clips, toner, card readers, meeting rooms that can actually display slides.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Departments all buy basic supplies differently. We have fourteen ordering portals and nineteen approval paths.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Press already has the phrase "paperwork gridlock." It is spreading.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I refuse to lose a news cycle to hole punches.

Initial review finds five repeating faults:

1. No shared minimum stock standard for common office essentials
2. Approval chains longer for low-value items than for high-value travel
3. Incompatible catalog labels across procurement systems
4. No cross-department visibility of available spare stock
5. Escalations based on personality, not service thresholds

**PM:** We are launching a practical protocol. Less drama, more drawers that open.

## ACT TWO — THE READINESS MODEL

10:41. Joint workshop with procurement, digital, facilities, and policy teams.

Slide title: **"BORING THINGS THAT KEEP GOVERNMENT MOVING"**.

**PROCUREMENT LEAD:** Proposal: one shared baseline list of critical everyday items. Each department keeps a seven-day minimum.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** We can add a lightweight stock board: green, amber, red by item category.

**FACILITIES REP:** Include simple substitution rules. If binder clips are red, approved alternatives are pre-cleared.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Keep it proportionate. We need accountability, not a parliamentary inquiry for pens.

Protocol agreed with four mandatory controls:

- **Baseline:** common item list with minimum coverage target
- **Visibility:** daily dashboard updated by 10:00
- **Fallbacks:** approved substitutions for top 20 essentials
- **Escalation:** time-based service thresholds, not title-based requests

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Sensible. If we can align on staples, perhaps we can align on strategy.

## ACT THREE — THE LIVE DRILL

13:05. Simulated disruption exercise across four departments.

**FACILITATOR:** Assume delayed deliveries for 48 hours. Maintain operations without emergency spending.

**DEPARTMENT A:** Reached amber on toner; switched to print-priority protocol.

**DEPARTMENT B:** Avoided outage by using substitution matrix for mailing supplies.

**DEPARTMENT C:** Triggered escalation too late due to stale dashboard entry.

**DEPARTMENT D:** Passed cleanly; had accurate stock counts and fallback plans.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Useful result. The system works when updated, fails when "someone meant to." 

Mid-drill correction package approved:

- Auto-reminder at 09:30 for dashboard owners
- Mandatory backup owner per department
- Weekly random spot-check by central operations
- Five-line playbook for emergency sharing requests

**PM:** This is not glamorous. It is governance with sleeves rolled up.

## ACT FOUR — THE ANNOUNCEMENT

17:11. Press office publishes: **"EVERYDAY READINESS STANDARD FOR GOVERNMENT OPERATIONS"**.

Release includes:

- Common baseline supply list
- Daily visibility requirement and ownership model
- Substitution and continuity playbook
- Department-by-department adoption timetable
- First monthly review date and metrics

By 18:04 (pilot figures):

- Small-item outage incidents down 58%
- Average request approval time cut from 26 hours to 6
- Cross-department borrowing requests down 41%
- Operational interruptions in pilot teams reduced by 37%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We can now say we fixed a real problem without pretending it was heroic.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Heroism is overrated. Competence scales better.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:36. Media pen outside the briefing room.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this really what government should be focusing on?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Government should focus on outcomes. Outcomes depend on systems. Systems depend on details.

**REPORTER:** So this is a stationery strategy?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** This is a reliability strategy that happens to include stationery.

**REPORTER:** And if departments ignore it?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Then they explain delays in writing. Assuming they can find a pen.
