# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 057
**Title:** The Corridor Pilot
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:06. A corridor full of aides speed-walking with folders they clearly haven’t read.)

A red banner flashes on every internal screen:

**PILOT SCHEME LAUNCHED NATIONWIDE BY MISTAKE**
**Programme:** *"Queue Freedom Corridor"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is a pilot national?

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Technically, it’s now a fleet.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** The memo said “launch in one corridor.”

**AIDE:** Someone interpreted that as every administrative corridor.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Define “administrative corridor.”

**AIDE:** Every service desk in England, Wales, Scotland, and bits of Northern Ireland that were awake.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Marvelous. We’ve delivered universal policy by grammar accident.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — WHAT EVEN IS IT?

War room. Whiteboard reads: **QUEUE FREEDOM CORRIDOR: BENEFITS?**

Nobody knows.

**PM:** What does this policy actually do?

**POLICY ADVISER:** It allows citizens to switch service lines if wait times exceed ten minutes.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Isn’t that just… joining another queue?

**POLICY ADVISER:** Yes, but with choice architecture.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We have rebranded walking.

Incoming problems by 08:14:

1. Citizens are queue-hopping continuously
2. Ticket systems are looping numbers
3. One council office has formed a “fast lane committee”
4. Breakfast TV calls it “The Musical Chairs State”
5. Trending tag: **#CorridorOfDoom**

**PM:** Can we pause it?

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Not cleanly. The rollback button triggers the onboarding video.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Of course. Policy now held hostage by animation.

## ACT TWO — THE EXPLAINER CRISIS

Comms drafts a public explainer.

**DRAFT V1:** “The pilot demonstrates adaptive transactional mobility.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** That sounds like a mobility scooter grant.

**DRAFT V2:** “People may move lines for efficiency.”

**CHIEF WHIP:** Also known as “moving lines.”

**DRAFT V3:** “A test feature was activated too widely. We are simplifying service queues today.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Keep V3. English remains undefeated.

Meanwhile, a training webinar accidentally goes public.

Presenter says: “If users get confused, reassure them confusion is transitional.”

Clip reaches social media in six minutes.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** They’ve remixed it with circus music.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Frankly, that’s editorial restraint.

## ACT THREE — SELECT COMMITTEE WARM-UP

Emergency committee pre-brief.

**CHAIR (on call):** Minister, did you authorise a nationwide launch without impact modelling?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. I authorised a local pilot with guardrails and a map.

**CHAIR:** Then why is it everywhere?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because software scales faster than caution.

**OPPOSITION MP:** Is this ideology or incompetence?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It is process failure with excellent branding.

**OPPOSITION MP:** So incompetence.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** With typography, yes.

Back in the office, legal requests wording changes.

Replace “mistake” with “unintended expansion event.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. If we broke the vase, we don’t call it ceramic decentralisation.

## ACT FOUR — CONTAINMENT BY LUNCH

Containment protocol launched:

- Freeze queue-switching at high-volume sites
- Revert all offices to single-line mode
- Publish wait-time dashboards per location
- Put senior staff on in-person floor duty
- Replace jargon FAQ with six plain answers

By 14:30:

- Ticket loops drop 72%
- Complaints plateau
- One local office reports “calm, with mild sarcasm”

**PM:** Good. Can we claim this was a stress test?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We can claim gravity optional too, but stairs will disagree.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Evening news wants a live interview.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Fine. One message: we overreached, we corrected, here’s proof.

## CLIMAX — THE PODIUM

Press briefing, 18:02.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**
This morning, a limited queue pilot was activated far beyond its intended scope. That was wrong, and we corrected it.

People deserve services that are clear, stable, and boring in the best possible way.

Here is what we’ve done today: returned affected sites to standard queueing, added live wait-time transparency, and locked pilot activation behind independent sign-off.

We will publish the full sequence of decisions and technical changes within 48 hours.

**REPORTER:** Was anyone disciplined?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Accountability follows facts, and facts are being published.

**REPORTER:** Is this digital reform failing?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Reform fails when clarity fails. So we restore clarity first.

## TAG

22:47. Corridor finally quiet.

A laminated sign leans against a wall:

**“QUEUE FREEDOM CORRIDOR — PHASE 2 (DRAFT)”**

Peter-like minister picks it up, flips it over, writes in marker:

**“NO.”**

Hands it to an aide.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Archive that under lessons learned.

Phone buzzes with new headline:

**“Government Abolishes Waiting, Accidentally Invents More Waiting.”**

He nods once.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Fair.

**END**
