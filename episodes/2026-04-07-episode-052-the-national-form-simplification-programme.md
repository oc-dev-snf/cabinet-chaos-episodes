# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 052
**Title:** The National Form Simplification Programme
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET DELIVERY SUITE. A giant wall screen reads: “FORM TO REQUEST ACCESS TO THE FORM REDUCTION FORM.”)

**CALLUM:** Why are citizens filling a nine-page form to ask us to shorten forms?

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** It includes mandatory fields on preferred stapling orientation.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Also a declaration confirming they have read the Plain Language Pledge, annexes A to H.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Morning radio is calling it “admin by obstacle course.”

**CALLUM:** Brilliant. We’ve bureaucratized simplicity.

**POLICY DIRECTOR:** We can launch a National Form Simplification Programme.

**CALLUM:** If this creates more forms, I’m defecting to paperless silence.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE

Audit team reports after a 72-hour sweep:

- 612 active public-facing forms across central services.
- 38% ask for information government already holds.
- Average completion time for high-volume forms: 27 minutes.

**CALLUM:** We’re collecting data like it’s a hobby.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Proposal: one rule set for all forms — Ask once, explain why, save progress, no duplicate identity checks unless legally required.

**CALLUM:** Human language. Human length. Human patience.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Set a target: reduce median completion time by 40% in 90 days.

**CALLUM:** Good. Measurable and mildly terrifying.

Treasury joins with procedural caution.

**BUDGET OFFICIAL:** Simplification projects often become expensive platforms.

**CALLUM:** Then no grand platform. Fix top 20 forms first. Ship weekly.

## ACT TWO

Pilot begins in housing support, licensing renewals, and small business grants.

Day four:

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** Completion rate up from 61% to 79% on two pilot forms.

**CALLUM:** Evidence. Keep it coming.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Problem: legal teams are re-adding caution paragraphs in six-point text.

**CALLUM:** Move legal essentials to expandable sections. Mandatory, readable, plain English.

**GOVERNANCE ADVISER:** We need consistency for appeals and audit trails.

**CALLUM:** Keep audit trails. Drop the ritual wording no one understands.

**DELIVERY LEAD:** Some teams insist every field is “critical.”

**CALLUM:** Add field ownership. Every field needs a named owner and justification.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** What if no one wants ownership?

**CALLUM:** Then the field leaves.

## ACT THREE

Comms drafts launch messages:

- “Shorter forms, faster answers.”
- “Ask only what is needed.”
- “Clarity is a public service.”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Should we brand this as a once-in-a-generation reform?

**CALLUM:** It’s fewer boxes and better wording. Let competence do the branding.

Implementation package agreed:

1. Top 20 high-volume forms redesigned first, published in weekly waves.
2. “Ask once” rule enforced across participating departments.
3. Every field tagged with legal basis, owner, and necessity score.
4. Reading level cap applied to all guidance text.
5. Weekly dashboard: completion rate, drop-off step, median time, repeat submissions.

**POLICY DIRECTOR:** We may finally reduce repeat calls.

**CALLUM:** That’s the dream: fewer calls, fewer apologies.

## CLIMAX

Evening press huddle.

**REPORTER:** Is this just cosmetic editing?

**CALLUM:** No. We removed unnecessary questions, reduced duplication, and made decisions traceable to evidence.

**REPORTER:** How do you stop forms growing back?

**CALLUM:** Field ownership and public metrics. If a form expands, we can see who added what and why.

**REPORTER:** Are staff being blamed for legacy complexity?

**CALLUM:** No. The system evolved this way over years. This is about fixing it fairly and visibly.

## TAG

(LATE NIGHT. DELIVERY SUITE. A dashboard shows: “Pilot median completion time: 27m → 14m.”)

**AIDE:** Complaints about confusing questions down 46% this week.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Someone sent thanks for “a form that ends before my tea gets cold.”

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We could print posters: “If it needs a glossary, it needs a rewrite.”

**CALLUM:** Print fewer posters. Rewrite more forms.

A notification appears.

**Subject:** “Request to add optional commemorative footer quote to all applications.”

Callum clicks REJECT, assigns a review note, and quietly deletes three adjectives from the next draft.

**END**
