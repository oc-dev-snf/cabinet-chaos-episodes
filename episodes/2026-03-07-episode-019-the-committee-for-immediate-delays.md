# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 019  
**Title:** The Committee for Immediate Delays  
**Genre:** Dark political satire

---

## COLD OPEN – INT. MINISTERIAL CORRIDOR – 07:58

A fresh brass plaque is being screwed into the wall while journalists watch.

It reads:

> **RAPID DELIVERY TASKFORCE (INTERIM)**

A civil servant places a second sign directly beneath it:

> **DELAY MANAGEMENT SUBGROUP (PERMANENT)**

Both signs point to the same room.

**PRESS OFFICER ELI SHAW** appears holding two binders labelled **URGENT** and **NOT YET**.

**SHAW:**
Morning. Government is moving fast by introducing safeguards against the risks of moving fast.

A reporter glances at the doors.

**REPORTER:**
So... are decisions being sped up or slowed down?

**SHAW:**
Yes.

---

## Premise
Facing criticism for stalled reforms, ministers launch a “rapid delivery” committee to prove momentum. The committee immediately creates a delay subgroup to protect against bad headlines. Within hours, both groups begin approving and blocking the same proposals, producing a flawless bureaucratic loop in which everyone is accountable for speed and nobody is responsible for movement.

---

## ACT 1 – THE LAUNCH

Cabinet briefing suite, 09:10.

A slide deck opens with triumphant music and the slogan:

> **DELIVER SOONER, REVIEW LONGER**

Chair Rowan Pike addresses a room full of advisers.

- Week 1 target: unblock 40 pending decisions
- Week 1 safeguard: pause any decision lacking “narrative resilience”
- Week 1 metric: publish a dashboard proving “controlled acceleration”

**PIKE:**
We are not slow. We are selectively immediate.

Deputy Chair Lena Cross asks for operational clarity.

**CROSS:**
Who has final sign-off?

**PIKE:**
The Delivery Committee, pending review by Delay Management.

**CROSS:**
And Delay Management reports to...?

**PIKE:**
Delivery, for timeliness.

Nobody laughs because everyone is taking notes.

---

## ACT 2 – THE PROCESS MAP

Operations room, 11:22.

A giant flowchart fills an entire wall.

Start node:

> **TAKE DECISIVE ACTION**

Six arrows later, every path leads to:

> **SEEK FURTHER DECISIVE CLARIFICATION**

A policy analyst presents the new approval states:

- Green: “Ready now”
- Amber: “Ready after confidence check”
- Blue: “Ready in principle, pending emotional weather”
- Grey: “Ready to revisit readiness”

At lunch, someone accidentally shares the internal tracker to the public press folder.

Top rows include:

- **Average Delay per Urgency (days)**
- **Blame Distribution Heatmap**
- **Visibility of Action (without action)**

One note cell reads:

> *“If pressure rises, announce a sprint.”*

Another reads:

> *“If sprint fails, announce a lessons-learned sprint.”*

---

## ACT 3 – THE BRIEFING SPIRAL

Press room, 15:47.

Shaw stands at the podium with a confidence of someone who has not slept.

**REPORTER:**
How many stalled decisions were actually approved today?

**SHAW:**
We approved the framework for deciding which stalled decisions qualify for approval.

**REPORTER 2:**
So none?

**SHAW:**
None is an outdated way of measuring early progress.

On a live panel, opposition MP Tessa Vale responds from a windy street outside Parliament.

**VALE:**
They’ve invented a machine that turns urgency into paperwork and calls it leadership.

Government backbencher Simon Kerr tries to calm things on drive-time radio.

**KERR:**
Look, the public wants careful action. We’re carefully acting on being action-ready.

He hears himself in his earpiece and goes very quiet.

---

## ACT 4 – THE VICTORY DECLARATION

Evening statement, 19:03.

Official release headline:

> **RAPID DELIVERY TASKFORCE HITS 100% OF DAY-ONE TARGETS**

Footnote:

> *Target was to establish baseline target methodology.*

At 19:19, a correction lands:

> **“Methodology remains provisional pending delay assurance.”**

At 19:42, a second correction:

> **“Delay assurance paused pending accelerated review.”**

At 20:10, the dashboard goes live with one animated gauge labelled:

> **MOMENTUM**

It spins continuously but never points to a number.

---

## TAG – 22:58

The office is nearly empty.

Cross opens tomorrow’s agenda draft.

Item 1:

> **Emergency Meeting: Why Is Delivery Perceived As Delayed?**

Item 2:

> **Proposal: Rename Delays as ‘Strategic Buffering’**

In the corner, a junior staffer edits the flowchart title from:

> **DECISION PIPELINE**

to:

> **EXPECTATION MANAGEMENT JOURNEY**
