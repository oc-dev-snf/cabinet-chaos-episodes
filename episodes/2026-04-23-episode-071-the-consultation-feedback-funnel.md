**Genre:** Original political satire

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:00. A projector displays a slide reading: **"PUBLIC FEEDBACK: 48,219 RESPONSES"**. A second line beneath it reads: **"UNREAD: 48,203"**.)

Morning briefing card:

**GOVERNMENT OPERATING DISCIPLINE WEEK — DAY 3**  
**Scenario:** *"The Consultation Feedback Funnel"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why does that number look like a mild emergency?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because every department launched consultations in the same month, Minister.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** We currently have six inboxes, four spreadsheets, and one intern's "very intuitive" colour-coding system.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** "Intuitive" to whom?

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** The intern. Briefly.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE FEEDBACK FLOOD

08:18. Situation room. Whiteboard reads: **LISTENING IS POLICY, NOT DECORATION.**

**PM:** We asked for input. Input arrived. Now we must demonstrate we read it before we announce conclusions.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** At present, three teams are summarising the same responses with different templates.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Journalists have noticed. They're asking if consultation means "reply-all with vibes."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It does not. It means "administrative cardio."

Rapid assessment identifies five operational faults:

1. No shared taxonomy for response themes
2. Duplicate submissions inflating headline totals
3. Accessibility submissions routed late for specialist review
4. Regional impacts noted, then lost in generic summaries
5. No standard link between feedback and final policy changes

**PM:** New standard: every major decision must show "what we heard" and "what changed."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. Traceability with fewer footnotes than a legal thriller.

## ACT TWO — THE FUNNEL DESIGN

10:37. Cross-department workshop. Slide title: **ONE FUNNEL, MANY VOICES**.

**DATA ADVISER:** We propose a single triage pipeline: Intake, Clean, Cluster, Escalate, Publish.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Add a compliance checkpoint before synthesis, especially for statutory consultations.

**CIVIL SOCIETY LIAISON:** And an equity check so high-volume respondents don't drown out low-volume, high-impact groups.

Pipeline agreed with four mandatory outputs per consultation:

- **Count:** verified submissions after deduplication
- **Themes:** top recurring points with confidence notes
- **Impact:** who is affected and where
- **Response:** what policy changed, and why if not

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It's almost beautiful. Bureaucracy with a memory.

By 11:21 they add a required question to every ministerial brief: **"Show me one decision this feedback altered."**

**PM:** If the answer is "none," we publish the reason in plain English.

## ACT THREE — THE LIVE TRIAL

13:08. Pilot run using a transport consultation and a health workforce consultation.

**FACILITATOR:** Ninety minutes. Produce publishable summaries with auditable links.

**TRANSPORT TEAM:** Deduplicated 12,404 responses to 9,871 verified.

**HEALTH TEAM:** Flagged 312 specialist submissions for expedited review.

**DIGITAL REP:** Built a reference map linking each policy clause to feedback themes.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Look at that. Democracy, but sortable.

Mid-pilot snag: one department uploaded narrative summaries without source tags.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** We cannot have "trust me" as a metadata standard.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Proposed fix: no source tags, no sign-off.

Fix package approved:

- Source tagging mandatory for summary claims
- Weekly cross-check sample audit (10%)
- Accessibility lane with 24-hour triage target
- Public methodology note attached to each outcome report

**PM:** We are not just collecting voices. We are proving we handled them responsibly.

## ACT FOUR — THE PUBLIC RELEASE

17:02. Press office rollout: **CONSULTATION HANDLING IMPROVEMENT UPDATE**.

Release set includes:

- Government-wide feedback funnel standard
- Plain-language explainer of triage and theme clustering
- Publication template showing "heard / changed / not changed"
- Audit cadence and data-quality thresholds
- Department adoption timeline

By 18:09:

- Duplicate-response inflation reduced by 63% in pilots
- Summary production time reduced from 9 days to 4
- Accessibility escalations actioned within SLA in both pilots
- Public-facing "what changed" sections published for first two consultations

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We can now answer "did feedback matter" with evidence instead of adjectives.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Frame that sentence. Then make it mandatory reading.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:41. Media pen outside briefing room.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this genuine listening or just better paperwork?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Better paperwork is how genuine listening survives contact with reality.

**REPORTER:** Are you promising every comment changes policy?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. We're promising every material theme gets a clear answer.

**REPORTER:** Why now?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because asking the public for input is easy. Showing your workings is governance.
