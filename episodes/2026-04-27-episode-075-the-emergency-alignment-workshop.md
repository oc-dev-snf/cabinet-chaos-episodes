# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 075
**Title:** The Emergency Alignment Workshop
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE TRAINING ROOM, 07:00. Flipchart reads: **"ALIGNMENT DAY"**. Beneath it, three arrows point in different directions.)

Morning briefing card:

**CROSS-GOVERNMENT DELIVERY DISCIPLINE WEEK — DAY 7**  
**Scenario:** *"The Emergency Alignment Workshop"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is this called an emergency workshop when it has branded tote bags?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because normal meetings failed, Minister. We are now escalating to facilitation.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Twelve directorates reported "full strategic alignment" using twelve different definitions.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Perfect. We have consensus in theory and chaos in practice.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — ALIGNMENT WITHOUT A COMPASS

08:18. Situation room. Screen title: **"ONE PLAN, MANY VERSIONS."**

**PM:** I keep receiving status updates that all say green while the deadlines are visibly on fire.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Teams are aligned vertically, horizontally, diagonally, and emotionally. Just not operationally.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Reporters are asking whether "alignment" is now a synonym for "to be confirmed."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If we say "joined-up government" one more time, someone will invoice us for punctuation.

Rapid diagnosis identifies five recurring faults:

1. Objectives written at three different levels of abstraction
2. Milestones tracked by calendar, quarter, or vague optimism
3. Ownership split between policy leads and delivery leads with no tie-break rule
4. Risk logs updated after risks become headlines
5. Escalation routes mapped but rarely used

**PM:** Build a method that survives contact with busy people.

## ACT TWO — THE WORKSHOP OFFENSIVE

10:42. Emergency workshop convened: policy, delivery, finance, digital, legal, and comms.

Slide title: **"ALIGNMENT = SHARED DECISIONS + SHARED DATES"**

**FACILITATOR:** We are replacing inspirational language with verifiable commitments.

**FINANCE LEAD:** Budget gates must match delivery gates. No more "approved in principle" at launch minus two days.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Dependencies go on one map, not hidden in team-specific decks.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Keep accountability role-based, not personality-based.

Four mandatory controls are adopted:

- **Single objective format:** goal, measure, owner, deadline in one line
- **Milestone discipline:** weekly checkpoints with clear pass/fail status
- **Decision log:** every major call recorded with rationale and review date
- **Escalation window:** unresolved blockers raised within 24 hours

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Tremendous. A governance framework that fits on two pages and a human brain.

## ACT THREE — LIVE FIRE EXERCISE

13:14. Trial run on a high-visibility policy rollout scheduled for next week.

**FACILITATOR:** Simulate change request at 13:30 and supplier delay at 14:00.

13:31. Policy team requests scope expansion.

**DELIVERY LEAD:** Logged. Impact assessed. Decision required by 15:00.

14:02. Supplier delay confirmed.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Escalated within window. Contingency plan activated from reserve list.

15:01. Decision log updated, scope adjusted, milestone moved with explanation.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Astonishing. The system works when people use the system.

Mid-trial improvements approved:

- 07:45 daily dependency check before first ministerial brief
- Shared risk board visible to all participating teams
- Friday "red issues only" review to prevent decorative reporting
- One-page escalation guide attached to every project kickoff pack

**PM:** We are not reducing ambition. We are reducing avoidable confusion.

## ACT FOUR — THE ANNOUNCEMENT

17:06. Press office release: **"GOVERNMENT ADOPTS COMMON DELIVERY ALIGNMENT STANDARD"**.

Statement includes:

- Unified objective and milestone format
- Written decision logging with review cadence
- Clear escalation timing for live blockers
- Shared dependency and risk visibility
- 30-day implementation review timetable

By 18:10 (trial metrics):

- Conflicting status reports reduced 64%
- Escalation response time improved from 3d 4h to 6h
- Last-minute scope changes down 41%
- On-time checkpoint completion up 38%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We have announced administrative coherence. Nobody knows how to argue with that yet.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Competence is unpopular right up until it is required.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:37. Media pen outside briefing room.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this just process theatre?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Theatre has costumes. This has deadlines.

**REPORTER:** Critics say workshops don't deliver outcomes.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Correct. Workshops don't deliver outcomes. Teams with clear decisions do.

**REPORTER:** What if departments slip back into old habits?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Then the dashboard records it, and we fix it before it becomes folklore.

## TAG

20:22. Training room door now labeled: **"ALIGNMENT ROOM (USE SPARINGLY)"**.
