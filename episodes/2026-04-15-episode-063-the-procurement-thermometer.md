# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 063
**Title:** The Procurement Thermometer
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:01. A giant dashboard on the wall flashes **"VALUE FOR MONEY: AMBER"**.)

Emergency banner:

**EFFICIENCY SPRINT — DAY ONE**  
**Scenario:** *"The Procurement Thermometer"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is the savings programme measured like a fever chart?

**COMMERCIAL DIRECTOR:** It makes urgency visible.

**FINANCE LEAD:** It makes panic visible.

**OPERATIONS AIDE:** It currently says we are "tepidly compliant."

Silence.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Brilliant. We have invented a medical condition for stationery.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — CHEAPER, FASTER, SAFER, PICK THREE

War room, 08:16. Whiteboard heading: **BUY LESS, GET MORE**

**PM:** We need tangible savings this quarter.

**TREASURY REP:** And no drop in service.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** And no shortcuts on process.

**CHIEF WHIP:** And no headlines about chaos.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Perfect. We'll perform administrative magic under procurement law.

By 09:40, the plan has acquired conflicting instructions:

1. Freeze all non-essential contracts, but accelerate "priority essentials"
2. Consolidate suppliers into one framework while promoting competition
3. Cut approval steps from six to three, then add two new assurance panels
4. Publish monthly savings figures before agreement on how savings are counted
5. Launch a hotline for departments to report barriers, routed to the same team creating them

**PM:** Are we at least aligned on definitions?

**FINANCE LEAD:** Mostly.

**PM:** What does "mostly" mean?

**FINANCE LEAD:** Three definitions and a footnote.

## ACT TWO — THE SPREADSHEET INQUEST

Finance room, 11:12. Projector title: **BASELINE RECONCILIATION v19_FINAL_FINAL**

**TREASURY REP:** Department A counts avoided renewals as savings.

**COMMERCIAL DIRECTOR:** Department B counts renegotiations.

**OPERATIONS AIDE:** Department C counts "intent to negotiate." 

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Intent is not money. Intent is a mood.

They draft common rules:

**RULE 1:** Savings only count when signed and auditable.

**RULE 2:** Avoided spend can count with documented baseline.

**RULE 3:** No double-counting across departments.

**PM:** Keep those. Put them in plain English.

At 12:31, a new complication appears: a key supplier flags that accelerated timelines reduce bidder quality.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** If we rush, we shrink competition.

**CHIEF WHIP:** If we don't rush, we miss the quarter.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Then we separate urgent from important before both fail.

## ACT THREE — THE REALITY HEARING

Cross-department call, 14:22. Forty people. Twelve muted apologies.

**FACILITATOR:** What's actually slowing you down?

Findings from delivery teams:

- The bottleneck is not tendering; it's unclear approval ownership
- Smaller suppliers struggle with duplicated forms across departments
- Most delays happen between "approved in principle" and "signed in reality"
- Teams need a standard playbook, not another slogan

**PM (quietly):** That's the first useful sentence we've heard all day.

Media alert pings:

**"Government efficiency drive bogged down in process maze."**

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Not ideal.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It's also not inaccurate.

## ACT FOUR — RESET TO BORING COMPETENCE

Reset meeting, 16:11. New heading: **ONE METHOD, MANY DEPARTMENTS**

Agreed operating model:

- Single savings definition across government, with audit trail requirements
- Standard procurement pack for common purchases, usable by all departments
- Named decision owner per contract stage, published internally
- Weekly unblock session focused only on contracts over risk threshold
- Monthly public dashboard with methodology note and confidence band

By 18:36:

- Three duplicate approval checkpoints removed
- Supplier forms reduced from nine templates to three
- Savings headline revised to "verified savings to date" with baseline note
- Hotline replaced with ticket queue and response-time commitment

**PM:** Is this less bold?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It's less theatrical. Which is how savings usually happen.

## CLIMAX — THE BRIEFING

Press room, 19:19.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**  
Today we tightened how we buy, and clarified how we measure savings.

From now on, savings figures will be based on signed decisions with auditable baselines. We are also simplifying supplier paperwork and assigning clear decision owners at each contract stage.

This is not a one-day fix. It is a repeatable method.

**REPORTER:** Did you overstate progress this morning?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We stated ambition this morning. We are publishing evidence tonight.

**REPORTER:** Are targets still realistic?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Targets are useful if definitions are honest. We've fixed the definitions first.

## TAG

21:47. Back office. The old dashboard still glows **"AMBER"**.

A junior official updates the label manually:

**"AMBER (WITH METHODOLOGY)"**

**AIDE:** Doesn't exactly sing, does it?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. But it might survive contact with auditors.

Printer hums. First standardized procurement pack lands in the tray.

Unexciting. Legible. Usable.

**END**
