# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 056
**Title:** Full Chaos: The Apology Pyramid
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:41. Alarm tones, printer jams, and the spiritual collapse of middle management.)

A giant screen flashes:

**BREAKING: GOVERNMENT APP SENDS “TEST ALERT” TO 31 MILLION PHONES**
**Message reads:** *“Remain calm. Further instructions follow.”*

No further instructions follow.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Wonderful. We’ve just recreated nationwide existential dread before breakfast.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** People think it’s cyberwar.

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Or a coup.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Or both, with push notifications.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Right. Who authorised the alert?

**AIDE:** It was supposed to go to internal testers only.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** “Internal testers” now appears to include every grandmother in Kent.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE TRIAGE

Emergency room, whiteboards everywhere, nobody breathing properly.

Crisis stack by 08:10:

1. Alert panic
2. Hospital switchboards jammed
3. Schools calling local MPs
4. Opposition demanding COBRA and resignations
5. Trending tag: **#InstructionGate**

**PM:** We need a statement in ten minutes.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Draft line: “An isolated systems anomaly.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. That sounds like we found a haunted USB stick.

**CHIEF WHIP:** What’s true?

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** A deployment script pointed to production instead of staging.

Silence.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** So the country got stress-tested by typo.

**PM:** Can we reverse it?

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** The alert can’t be unsent.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Splendid. We’re governed by physics and regret.

## ACT TWO — THE APOLOGY PYRAMID

Comms strategy appears on screen:

- Tier 1: Immediate reassurance
- Tier 2: Accountability
- Tier 3: Fix plan
- Tier 4: Independent review

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is this a pyramid?

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** Visual clarity.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We are in a national panic, not an MBA webinar.

Draft statement workshop collapses in real time.

**DRAFT V1:** “We regret inconvenience.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Rejected. This is not a delayed train.

**DRAFT V2:** “We apologise for concern caused.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Better. Still sounds like we accidentally sneezed near policy.

**DRAFT V3:** “We sent an alert in error. It should not have been sent. We are sorry.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Keep that. Plain language: the final luxury.

Meanwhile, IT briefing turns feral.

**TECH LEAD:** The release guardrail was disabled for performance testing.

**CHIEF WHIP:** By whom?

**TECH LEAD:** A contractor account named “temp-admin-2.”

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Of course. The constitutional role of temp-admin-2.

## ACT THREE — COMMITTEE AMBUSH

By noon, Peter-like minister is hauled before an emergency committee session.

**CHAIR:** Did your department knowingly run a live public alert test?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No.

**CHAIR:** Who made the final click?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We are tracing the precise chain now and will publish it.

**CHAIR:** Are heads rolling?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We’re doing causality before theatre.

**OPPOSITION MP:** Isn’t this proof digital government is fundamentally unsafe?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. It’s proof that safety controls were bypassed and must be engineered so they cannot be bypassed by anyone, including enthusiastic idiots.

Room erupts.

**CHAIR:** Did you just say “enthusiastic idiots” in reference to your own department?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I said what the public already suspects.

## ACT FOUR — FULL CHAOS PEAK

15:23. Secondary crisis.

A regional authority copies the original alert wording for a flood warning by mistake.

Now half the country thinks “further instructions” are finally coming.

**COMMS DIRECTOR:** We need one authoritative channel.

**PM:** Use all channels.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** No. One source of truth mirrored everywhere. If five people improvise, we get jazz panic.

War room protocol launched:

- Single command desk
- Numbered public updates every 20 minutes
- Myth-busting section on every post
- Direct briefing to broadcasters and local councils
- Helpline script simplified to 6 lines

**OPERATIONS LEAD:** Switchboards stabilising. NHS call volume dropping.

**CHIEF WHIP:** Opposition still calling for resignations.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** They can have a review, a timeline, and my face on camera for as long as required. They can’t have fiction.

## CLIMAX — THE 6PM STATEMENT

Press room packed. Atmosphere: blood sport.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (at podium):**
This morning, a government emergency alert was sent in error. It should not have happened. I am sorry.

Here is what happened: a protected deployment path was bypassed, and a test message reached the live system.

Here is what happens now: we have frozen all nonessential alert releases, implemented dual-key approval with independent sign-off, and commissioned a rapid external audit whose findings will be published in full.

You are entitled to competence. Today we fell short. We fix this in public.

Questions begin.

**REPORTER:** Why should anyone trust your system again?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Because trust isn’t requested. It is rebuilt, line by line, with evidence.

**REPORTER:** Will anyone be fired?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Accountability will follow facts, not hashtags.

**REPORTER:** Are you resigning?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I’m staying to repair what broke.

## TAG

23:12. Office empty. One desk lamp. One untouched sandwich.

Dashboard summary:

- Call volumes down 68%
- Misinformation trend decelerating
- External review panel confirmed

Notification pops up:

**“New meme: minister labelled ‘Lord Further Instructions.’”**

Peter-like minister stares at it for three full seconds.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If democracy survives on memes, at least let them spell my title correctly.

He opens tomorrow’s brief: **“Resilience Strategy Refresh.”**

He exhales like a punctured tyre.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Right. Back into the blender.
