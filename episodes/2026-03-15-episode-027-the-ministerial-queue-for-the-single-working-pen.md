# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 027  
**Title:** The Ministerial Queue for the Single Working Pen  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## Premise
A minor stationery supply glitch metastasizes into a full governmental emergency when every official pen in Whitehall simultaneously fails before a crucial signing ceremony. As departments scramble to locate one functioning biro, policy stalls, alliances fracture, and a routine administrative day collapses into performative brinkmanship over ink allocation, signature sovereignty, and whether verbal initials are constitutionally binding.

## COLD OPEN – INT. CABINET OFFICE CORRIDOR – 7:01 AM

A framed sign reads: **"TODAY: ROUTINE ADMINISTRATION ONLY."**

A junior aide clicks a pen. Nothing. Clicks another. Nothing.

**AIDE:**
Why are these all dead?

**DAN:**
Could be humidity. Could be procurement. Could be fate.

Inside the conference room, eight ministers sit before identical folders marked **"SIGN HERE"** and identical useless pens.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Who issued decorative writing instruments to the entire executive?

**JULIA:**
They were classed as "premium resilient." Apparently resilience was aspirational.

An alert flashes across everyone's phone:

> **PRIORITY NOTICE: PEN SHORTAGE ESCALATION PROTOCOL ACTIVATED**

**NINA:**
There’s a *protocol* for this?

**DAN:**
There is now.

---

## ACT ONE – INT. CENTRAL SUPPLIES ROOM – 8:12 AM

Metal shelving stands half-empty. Three officials guard a locked drawer like it contains state secrets.

**SUPPLIES MANAGER:**
Inventory check confirms one functioning black ballpoint.

Silence.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
One?

**SUPPLIES MANAGER:**
Two if you count a marker that leaks through legal paper.

**DAN:**
No. We are not ratifying fiscal guidance in permanent marker.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRESS OFFICE – 8:39 AM

A whiteboard lists draft messaging:

- **"Government fully in control of pen environment"**
- **"Temporary ink optimization period"**
- **"Signatures remain available in spirit"**

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Which line are we leading with?

**JULIA:**
None. "In spirit" sounds like we’ve abolished paperwork and replaced it with vibes.

**DAN:**
Keep it simple: "The government has located a pen and is using it responsibly." 

**PRESS OFFICER:**
That sounds worse.

---

## ACT TWO – INT. TEMPORARY PEN ALLOCATION COMMITTEE – 9:24 AM

A laminated agenda reads **"INK FAIRNESS FRAMEWORK (DRAFT 9)"**.

**COMMITTEE CHAIR:**
Proposal A: first come, first signed.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Absolutely not. Major portfolios require priority access.

**NINA:**
Proposal B: time-slots by urgency category.

**DAN:**
Define urgency.

**NINA:**
Anything likely to become a headline before lunch.

Everyone nods.

A backbencher raises a hand.

**BACKBENCHER:**
Could we not borrow pens from another department?

**JULIA:**
We tried. They requested an interdepartmental memorandum, signed in ink.

### SCENE 2 – INT. CABINET ANTEROOM – 10:06 AM

A velvet rope has been installed around a single pen resting on a silver tray.

**SECURITY OFFICER:**
No one handles the instrument without witness signatures.

**DAN:**
Witness signatures with what?

**SECURITY OFFICER:**
...Good point.

A minister approaches with a stack of five urgent papers.

**SECURITY OFFICER:**
One signature per person per rotation.

**MINISTER:**
I have national decisions.

**SECURITY OFFICER:**
So does everyone. Take a number.

The dispenser prints: **"YOUR POSITION IN QUEUE: 47"**.

---

## ACT THREE – INT. BROADCAST BRIEFING ROOM – 11:21 AM

A spokesperson stands at the lectern while a producer holds up cue cards hand-written in pencil.

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Government operations continue uninterrupted.

Behind them, two aides quietly argue over whose initials are currently drying.

**REPORTER:**
Is it true Cabinet has implemented "signature rationing"?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
We prefer "targeted writing access."

**REPORTER:**
And the photograph of ministers queueing behind velvet rope?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
A demonstration of orderly administration.

**DAN (off-camera):**
Stop saying orderly. No one believes orderly when a queue ticket system is involved.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRIME MINISTER’S STUDY – 11:52 AM

A constitutional adviser places three unsigned documents on the desk.

**ADVISER:**
Without signatures, these cannot take effect.

**PM:**
Can we do digital approval?

**ADVISER:**
System requires handwritten confirmation that digital approval is intended.

The PM stares at the ceiling.

**PM:**
Who designed this?

**DAN:**
A taskforce on modernization. They met weekly for two years and trusted stationery forever.

---

## CLIMAX – INT. CABINET ROOM – 12:31 PM

The final pre-lunch document sits in the center of the table.

The single working pen is passed from hand to hand with ceremonial dread.

**COMMITTEE CHAIR:**
Decision point: do we use the last visible ink on emergency planning or transport funding?

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Emergency planning.

**TRANSPORT MINISTER:**
Transport funding *is* emergency planning if you’ve seen commuter rail.

A drop of ink appears. Then stops.

**NINA:**
That’s it. It’s dry.

A collective gasp.

Dan quietly takes out a cheap promotional pen from his jacket.

**JULIA:**
Where did that come from?

**DAN:**
Hotel conference in 2019. I kept it out of professional pessimism.

He clicks. It writes perfectly.

Long silence.

**PM:**
You just ended a national stationery crisis with a free pen.

**DAN:**
I’d like this reflected in my pension.

---

## TAG – INT. NO.10 KITCHENETTE – 6:08 PM

A new sign on the wall reads: **"PEN CONTINGENCY DRILL – FIRST MONDAY OF EVERY MONTH."**

Nina opens a drawer. Inside: thirty identical promotional pens.

**NINA:**
We had these the whole time.

**DAN:**
Why weren’t they logged?

**NINA:**
Drawer labeled "misc cables." No one looked.

Julia enters, holding a briefing note.

**JULIA:**
Tomorrow’s emergency: nobody can find a compatible charger.

Dan sighs into his tea.

**DAN:**
Fine. Activate the cable taskforce. And lock the drawer before someone forms a committee.

**END**
