# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 016  
**Title:** The National Resilience App (Beta)  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN – INT. NO.10 BRIEFING ROOM – 7:01 AM

A launch banner reads: **"NATIONAL RESILIENCE APP — CALM IN YOUR POCKET."**

A giant countdown screen hits zero. Every phone in the room vibrates.

**DAN:**
That was dramatic.

**JULIA:**
It’s meant to reassure people.

All phones display:

> **ALERT LEVEL: MILDLY CONCERNED**  
> **RECOMMENDED ACTION: WAIT BY A KETTLE**

**MINISTER FELTON:**
Excellent. Practical. Domestic. British.

A second alert appears.

> **CORRECTION: DO NOT WAIT BY A KETTLE**  
> **KETTLE STATUS UNKNOWN**

**DAN:**
We’ve issued contradictory hydration guidance in thirty seconds.

---

## ACT ONE – INT. PRESS OFFICE – 8:20 AM

Screens show social posts from across the country:

- "App says my street is amber but my neighbour is turquoise."  
- "It congratulated me for surviving Tuesday."  
- "My dog now has a preparedness score of 94."

**JULIA:**
Why are there colours that don’t exist in the official framework?

**APP LEAD:**
We added "emotion-adjacent shades" to improve engagement.

**DAN:**
You gamified civil contingency.

### SCENE 2 – INT. MINISTERIAL OFFICE – 8:46 AM

Felton rehearses a statement.

**FELTON:**
"The app empowers communities through confidence architecture." Good?

**DAN:**
Say: "It’s a pilot and we’re fixing issues." Humans trust honesty.

**FELTON:**
That sounds alarmingly plain.

A notification pings on Felton’s phone:

> **YOU ARE IN A LOW-TRUST ZONE.**  
> **TRY SPEAKING IN SHORT SENTENCES.**

**DAN:**
For once, I support the app.

---

## ACT TWO – INT. COBRA SIDE ROOM – 9:35 AM

Officials from multiple departments crowd around a map where districts blink between "steady," "wobbly," and "vibes pending."

**COBRA CHAIR:**
Can we lock one national status for at least ten minutes?

**APP LEAD:**
Not while the sentiment engine is live.

**NINA:**
What sentiment engine?

**APP LEAD:**
It scans headlines, weather, commuter sigh volume, and biscuit purchases.

**DAN:**
Biscuit purchases are now a constitutional signal.

### SCENE 2 – INT. CALL CENTRE WAR DESK – 10:12 AM

Operators read scripts while alarms chime.

**OPERATOR:**
Hello, yes, if your app says "build local ark," please ignore that update.

**SECOND OPERATOR:**
No, madam, "fortify brunch" is not legally binding.

Nina holds up a printed escalation memo.

**NINA:**
We’ve had three thousand calls asking whether "be emotionally weatherproof" is tax-deductible.

**DAN:**
I hate that it’s a fair question.

---

## ACT THREE – INT. LIVE BROADCAST STUDIO – 11:28 AM

Felton goes on air.

**FELTON:**
The public should remain calm. The app is functioning exactly as intended, with minor interpretive flexibility.

A producer signals panic from behind camera.

On-screen chyron suddenly auto-updates:

> **GOVT CONFIRMS INTERPRETIVE FLEXIBILITY**

**JULIA (whispering):**
That phrase means nothing.

**DAN:**
It means everything to committee inquiries.

### SCENE 2 – INT. DIGITAL RESPONSE HUB – 11:52 AM

Engineers type furiously.

**ENGINEER:**
We can roll back to Version 1.2.

**APP LEAD:**
That’s the one that sent "prepare your hallway" at 3 AM.

**ENGINEER:**
Version 1.3 then.

**NINA:**
What does that do?

**ENGINEER:**
Mostly asks reflective questions.

All phones buzz:

> **HOW PREPARED ARE YOU, SPIRITUALLY, FOR LIGHT DISRUPTION?**

**DAN:**
Pull the plug.

---

## CLIMAX – INT. CABINET ROOM – 12:40 PM

A final decision meeting.

**COBRA CHAIR:**
Option A: suspend the app. Option B: keep it live and issue hourly clarifications.

**FELTON:**
Hourly clarifications show responsiveness.

**JULIA:**
Hourly clarifications show collapse.

Dan slides one page to the Chair.

**DAN:**
Option C: keep emergency alerts, remove everything else, and apologise in normal English.

Silence.

Then, unexpectedly, nods around the table.

**COBRA CHAIR:**
Approved. Minimal mode. Human language only.

Phones ping one last time:

> **UPDATE: WE OVERCOMPLICATED THIS.**  
> **IF THERE IS A REAL RISK, WE WILL TELL YOU CLEARLY.**

The room exhales.

---

## TAG – INT. NO.10 KITCHENETTE – 6:18 PM

Dan stirs instant coffee. Nina checks the app dashboard.

**NINA:**
Engagement is down seventy percent.

**DAN:**
Good. Calm people don’t doom-scroll preparedness.

Felton enters, quietly proud.

**FELTON:**
I gave a straight answer on the six o’clock news.

**DAN:**
How did it feel?

**FELTON:**
Like skydiving without branding.

A soft ping.

The app sends one final notification:

> **STATUS: NORMAL.**  
> **SUGGESTION: HAVE DINNER.**

They all look at one another.

**NINA:**
That’s the first useful thing it’s done all day.

**END**
