# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 076
**Title:** The National Printer Resilience Summit
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE BASEMENT, 06:58. Three multifunction printers glow like suspicious totems. A sign reads: **"PRINT CONFIDENTLY"**. Nobody does.)

Morning briefing card:

**CIVIC OPERATIONS CONTINUITY WEEK — DAY 8**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Printer Resilience Summit"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why are we holding a summit for printers?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because yesterday's briefing pack emerged as 84 pages of blank confidence.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Six departments use six print systems, none of which agree on paper orientation.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We have digital government, provided nobody needs paper.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — WHEN THE PAPER TRAIL GOES MISSING

08:11. Situation room. Screen title: **"DOCUMENT OUTPUT FRAGILITY"**.

**PM:** I signed a policy note upside down because page numbers restarted three times.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Teams are emailing PDFs to themselves, printing at home, and couriering them back as "official copies."

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Journalists are asking whether the phrase "hard copy" now means "optional fiction."

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We cannot run a state on screenshots and optimism.

Rapid review identifies four root failures:

1. No common print settings for formal documents
2. Procurement by unit cost, not reliability under load
3. No fallback process when secure print queues fail
4. Ownership split across IT, estates, and "whoever is nearest"

**PM:** I want one standard that works at 7 a.m. under pressure.

## ACT TWO — THE SUMMIT OF LOW EXPECTATIONS

10:37. Cross-government summit convened: digital, security, estates, procurement, and departmental operations leads.

Slide title: **"IF IT MATTERS, IT MUST PRINT"**

**DIGITAL LEAD:** We define one default profile for ministerial packs: duplex, numbered, stamped, verified.

**SECURITY LEAD:** Secure release remains mandatory, but queue timeout doubles during critical windows.

**PROCUREMENT LEAD:** Future contracts score resilience and support response time, not just per-page price.

**ESTATES LEAD:** Every main building gets one designated continuity printer with tested backup power.

Three immediate controls are agreed:

- **Standard output profile** for all formal submissions
- **Two-step fallback**: alternate device, then controlled external print partner
- **Named ownership rota** for out-of-hours failures

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Finally, a national strategy for not shouting at office machinery.

## ACT THREE — STRESS TEST AT NOON

12:03. Live drill: simulate a 300-page urgent submission across four buildings.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Primary queue in Building B intentionally paused.

12:08. Automatic reroute triggers to continuity printer.

**IT DUTY MANAGER:** Output verified. Pagination intact. No mystery landscape pages.

12:17. Security release delay appears at one site.

**SECURITY LEAD:** Timeout exception applied under protocol. Logged and reviewed.

12:24. Full submission delivered, indexed, and signed.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** That is the first complete noon pack this month that did not involve stationery bargaining.

Post-drill improvements approved:

- Weekly five-minute print continuity check before Monday briefings
- Single incident form with mandatory root-cause field
- Shared stock threshold alerts for paper and toner
- Quarterly cross-building resilience drill

**PM:** Modern government can be paper-light. It cannot be process-light.

## ACT FOUR — THE ANNOUNCEMENT

16:52. Press office release: **"GOVERNMENT ADOPTS COMMON DOCUMENT OUTPUT RESILIENCE STANDARD"**.

Statement includes:

- Unified print profile for formal documents
- Tested fallback path during system interruptions
- Clear operational ownership for incidents
- Procurement criteria updated for resilience outcomes
- 21-day review of implementation readiness

By 18:05 (pilot metrics):

- Failed urgent print jobs down 58%
- Average recovery time down from 2h 10m to 24m
- Submission pagination errors down 73%
- Out-of-hours incident resolution up 44%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We have successfully announced administrative competence in a way that sounds almost dramatic.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Nothing is more dramatic than a deadline and no paper.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:31. Media pen outside briefing room.

**REPORTER:** Minister, isn't this all a bit minor for national attention?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Minor is a word used by people whose paperwork arrived on time.

**REPORTER:** Critics say this is bureaucracy about bureaucracy.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Correct. We are fixing the bureaucracy that blocks actual decisions.

**REPORTER:** What happens if the system fails again tomorrow?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Then we use the fallback we tested today, and nobody pretends surprise.

## TAG

20:19. Basement noticeboard now displays: **"PRINT RESILIENCE: TESTED TODAY"**.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER (off-screen):** Tomorrow we discuss national stapler interoperability. Bring legal.

**END**
