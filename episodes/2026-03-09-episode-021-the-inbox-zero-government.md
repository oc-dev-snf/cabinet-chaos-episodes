**MINISTER ADA REED** walks in, takes one look, and smiles like she’s just discovered gravity can be optional.

There. Proof of delivery culture.

**CHIEF OF STAFF NOAH QUILL:**
That was the sandbox.

---

## Premise
After weeks of criticism that government decisions are stuck in endless correspondence, the Cabinet launches an “Inbox Zero by Noon” initiative. Civil servants are told every email must be resolved immediately: answered, delegated, or archived. By lunchtime, inboxes are pristine, accountability trails are vaporized, and the only messages anyone can find are motivational GIFs and calendar invites for the “Post-Inbox Reflection Forum.”

---

## ACT 1 – THE ANNOUNCEMENT

Press office, 09:05.

A banner reads:

> **CLEAR MAIL. CLEAR MIND. CLEAR MANDATE.**

Underneath, in smaller text:

> *Operational interpretation may vary by department.*

Reed rehearses lines for the podium:

- “We are ending bureaucratic drift.”
- “Every message gets a same-day decision.”
- “If it sits unread, it is not governance.”

Quill circulates emergency guidance:

- All ministerial inboxes must reach zero by 12:00
- “Reply-all” requires director sign-off
- Messages older than 7 days must be “concluded by action code”

Treasury liaison **PRIYA DHAN** scans the appendix.

**DHAN:**
What is action code 7C?

**QUILL:**
“Resolved by strategic silence.”

**DHAN:**
That sounds like ignoring it.

**QUILL:**
No, it’s procedural closure.

## ACT 2 – THE GREAT TRIAGE

Cross-Whitehall call, 11:11.

Every department reports “on track,” each in a slightly panicked tone.

Health has created a folder called **“FOR HISTORICAL OPTIMISM.”**

Transport labels unresolved complaints as **“awaiting modal destiny.”**

Education applies auto-replies reading:

> “Thank you for your email. Your concern is now in active conceptual processing.”

Cabinet Office analyst **FINN LOWE** presents a live tracker:

> **INBOX ZERO DELIVERY GRID v4_FINAL_real_final**

Columns include:

- Unread count
- Archived with confidence
- Archived with hope
- Potentially important (deferred)

A red alert flashes across screens:

> **SYSTEM NOTICE: AUTO-ARCHIVE RULE APPLIED TO ‘URGENT’ TAG**

Thirty microphones unmute at once.

## ACT 3 – THE MEDIA ROUND

News studio, 14:42.

Reed faces journalist **JAYA MENON**, composed and surgical.

**MENON:**

**REED:**
All were reviewed through our new accelerated governance pathway.

**MENON:**
Is that a number?

People want decisions, not email mountains.

**HOST:**
Did this improve decisions?

Pause.

**BROOME:**
It definitely improved folder structure.

## ACT 4 – THE AFTERMATH

Cabinet debrief, 18:37.

Quill presents the day’s success metrics:

- 68% reduction in visible message backlog

Dhan points to the footer:

How are we back above nine thousand?

Everyone replied to the “Inbox Zero achieved” announcement.
