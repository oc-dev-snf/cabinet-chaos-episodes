# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 105
**Title:** The National Pilot Overview Taskforce
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

A conference room screen displays:

**LIVE PILOT TRACKER**
- Pilot to review pilot governance
- Pilot to test pilot tracker usability
- Pilot to decide whether pilots are overused

A deputy director stares at it and says, “Are these active?”

An analyst replies, “Technically yes. Conceptually, still in pilot.”

Cut to opening titles.

## ACT ONE

The government faces criticism that too many policy pilots launch, overlap, and vanish without conclusion.

To restore order, the minister announces the **National Pilot Overview Taskforce** — a central team to catalogue every pilot currently underway.

By noon, the taskforce discovers four different definitions of “pilot” in active use:
- A limited trial with clear end date
- A soft launch with expandable scope
- A temporary workaround expected to become permanent
- Any policy not yet brave enough to call itself policy

Comms brands this “healthy methodological diversity.”

Delivery teams call it “a filing cabinet with weather.”

## ACT TWO

The taskforce releases a mandatory intake form:
- Pilot name
- Intended outcome
- Start date
- End date
- What success looks like
- Whether anyone will decide if it succeeded

Submissions come back quickly, but not cleanly.

Common entries include:
- “End date: when confidence improves”
- “Success metric: fewer escalations, qualitatively”
- “Decision owner: currently being socialized”

One department submits a spreadsheet titled **FINAL_PILOT_LIST_v3_FINAL2_useTHIS.xlsx** and follows it with an email saying, “Please ignore previous list unless preferred.”

## ACT THREE

Under pressure, the taskforce hosts a cross-department **Pilot Triage Sprint**.

Pilots are sorted into three categories:
1. Continue
2. Conclude
3. Merge into something comprehensible

Arguments begin immediately.

Team A insists their pilot cannot conclude until baseline data is complete.

Team B says baseline data cannot complete until Team A concludes the pilot.

A senior official proposes a neutral path: “Let’s declare both evidence-dependent and revisit next quarter.”

Everyone agrees because it sounds decisive.

## CLIMAX

At the evening briefing, the minister declares major progress:

“We now have one dashboard, one naming convention, and one accountable owner for each pilot.”

A journalist asks how many pilots were closed today.

An official answers, “Closed as in administratively, or closed as in no longer happening?”

Another asks whether any pilots have become permanent without decision.

The minister pauses, then says, “We prefer the term organically stabilized.”

## TAG

At 7:11 p.m., staff receive a new directive:

**PILOT COMPLETION STANDARD (INTERIM)**

- Every pilot must have an end condition
- Every end condition must have an owner
- Every owner must confirm they are the owner
- If ownership is unclear, default to shared accountability pending clarification

Footer note:

“Version 0.9 (Operational) — supersedes Version 1.0 (Conceptual).”

**END**
