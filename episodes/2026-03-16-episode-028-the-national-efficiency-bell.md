# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 028  
**Title:** The National Efficiency Bell  
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## Premise
A well-meaning internal initiative to improve meeting discipline spirals into a cross-government obsession when a ceremonial "efficiency bell" is introduced to end rambling discussions. Within hours, every department weaponizes bell-ringing to shut down awkward questions, fast-track dubious decisions, and claim productivity gains based entirely on how often someone is interrupted.

## COLD OPEN – INT. CABINET OFFICE CONFERENCE SUITE – 7:03 AM

A brass bell sits on a velvet cushion beneath a sign: **"SPEAK BRIEFLY. GOVERN DECISIVELY."**

A policy lead is mid-sentence.

**POLICY LEAD:**
...and if we compare the regional impact assessments—

**DAN:**
(eyes on stopwatch)  
Sixty seconds. Ring it.

Julia rings the bell once. The policy lead freezes.

**JULIA:**
Lovely. Concise. Next.

**NINA:**
We didn’t hear the impact assessments.

**DAN:**
Exactly. We saved three minutes.

A notification pings on everyone’s phone:

> **CABINET CIRCULAR: EFFICIENCY BELL PILOT NOW MANDATORY ACROSS DEPARTMENTS**

**NINA:**
Mandatory *pilot*?

**DAN:**
It’s agile governance. Contradictions are a feature.

---

## ACT ONE – INT. DEPARTMENTAL WAR ROOM – 8:18 AM

A whiteboard reads: **"BELL METRICS DASHBOARD"**.

Columns:
- Rings per hour
- Average sentence length
- Unresolved issues (hidden)

**ANALYST:**
If we ring every forty-five seconds, our productivity score doubles by lunch.

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Do it. We need visible momentum.

**ANALYST:**
Visible to whom?

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Everyone except auditors.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRESS GRID DESK – 8:46 AM

Draft headlines circulate on a screen:

- **"Government Cuts Meeting Waste by 63%"**
- **"New Bell Delivers Faster Decisions"**
- **"Citizens Benefit from Streamlined Speaking"**

**PRESS OFFICER:**
Are these numbers real?

**JULIA:**
Real enough to survive one news cycle.

**PRESS OFFICER:**
And the unresolved issues figure?

**DAN:**
Classify it as "ongoing strategic silence."

---

## ACT TWO – INT. INTERDEPARTMENTAL COORDINATION CALL – 9:31 AM

Twelve squares on a video wall. Every participant has a bell.

**CHAIR:**
Welcome. Let’s align priorities—

Three bells ring at once.

**TREASURY REP:**
Point of order: opening remarks exceeded brevity threshold.

**HEALTH REP:**
I was ringing to flag the treasury ringing.

**TRANSPORT REP:**
I rang because no one explained threshold governance.

A fourth bell rings continuously in the background.

**NINA:**
Who is leaning on the bell?

**VOICE (OFF):**
Sorry, cat sat on it.

### SCENE 2 – INT. CABINET OFFICE CORRIDOR – 10:09 AM

A junior aide wheels a trolley loaded with bells labeled by size:

- "MINOR INTERRUPTION"
- "MAJOR INTERRUPTION"
- "CONSTITUTIONAL INTERRUPTION"

**AIDE:**
Legal requested the constitutional set with soft mallets.

**DAN:**
Why soft?

**AIDE:**
Noise complaints from Constitutional Affairs.

**JULIA:**
The irony is making my eye twitch.

---

## ACT THREE – INT. LIVE BRIEFING ROOM – 11:17 AM

A spokesperson stands at the podium, bell placed prominently beside water jug and briefing folder.

**SPOKESPERSON:**
Today the government launched a practical tool to improve public-service focus—

A reporter raises a hand.

**REPORTER:**
Is it true an environmental review meeting ended after thirty-two seconds?

**SPOKESPERSON:**
A concise exchange occurred.

**REPORTER:**
No exchange occurred. Someone rang before introductions.

**SPOKESPERSON:**
That demonstrates readiness.

From the wings, Dan whispers urgently.

**DAN (off-camera):**
Don’t ring at journalists. We discussed this.

### SCENE 2 – INT. PRIME MINISTER’S OFFICE – 11:54 AM

The PM reviews a stack of one-page decisions, each stamped **"BELL-CLEARED"**.

**PM:**
Why are there no risk sections?

**ADVISER:**
Risk sections exceeded time allowance.

**PM:**
And stakeholder feedback?

**ADVISER:**
Most stakeholders were rung at.

PM pinches the bridge of their nose.

**PM:**
Can we suspend this?

**DAN:**
Technically yes, but suspending the bell requires a Bell Oversight Subcommittee recommendation.

**PM:**
When do they meet?

**DAN:**
Tomorrow. They’ve booked four hours.

---

## CLIMAX – INT. CABINET ROOM – 12:29 PM

A full table. Tension high. Bells everywhere.

**COMMITTEE CHAIR:**
Final agenda item: should the Efficiency Bell continue beyond pilot phase?

**MINISTER HARROW:**
Yes. We are visibly decisive.

**NINA:**
We are audibly decisive. Different thing.

**TRANSPORT MINISTER:**
Could we keep it only for meetings longer than ninety minutes?

**HEALTH MINISTER:**
Could we keep it away from clinicians entirely?

**TREASURY REP:**
Could we monetize ringing rights?

A sudden chorus of rings erupts as everyone interrupts everyone else.

Dan stands, grabs the biggest bell, and places it upside down on the table.

Silence.

**DAN:**
There. Bell neutralized.

Long pause.

**PM:**
What’s the recommendation now?

**NINA:**
Radical idea: we let people finish a sentence.

The room contemplates this as if hearing a forbidden doctrine.

---

## TAG – INT. BASEMENT STORAGE ROOM – 6:11 PM

Shelves are now stacked with retired bells and a fresh label: **"ARCHIVED INNOVATIONS"**.

Nina tapes up a cardboard sign: **"NEXT PILOT: THE TALKING STICK OF ACCOUNTABILITY"**.

**JULIA:**
Please tell me that’s a joke.

**NINA:**
I found a procurement request for seventy-two ceremonial sticks.

Dan appears in doorway with tea and thousand-yard stare.

**DAN:**
Fine. Tomorrow we govern with wood.

**JULIA:**
Any bright side?

**DAN:**
Yes. At least sticks don’t ring.

A distant *clang* echoes from upstairs.

**DAN:**
I stand corrected.

**END**
