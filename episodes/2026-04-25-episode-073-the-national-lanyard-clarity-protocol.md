# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 073
**Title:** The National Lanyard Clarity Protocol
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE, 07:00. Security gate beeps continuously. Screen reads: **"ACCESS EVENT BACKLOG: 1,284"**.)

Morning briefing card:

**GOVERNMENT OPERATING DISCIPLINE WEEK — DAY 5**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Lanyard Clarity Protocol"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why is there a queue to enter our own building?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Too many pass designs. Staff keep presenting visitor badges, contractor badges, retired badges, and one museum membership card.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Security rejected a deputy director because their photo predates their current haircut by three administrations.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We have achieved maximum identity with minimum identification.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — ACCESS DENIED, GOVERNANCE DELAYED

08:09. Incident room. Whiteboard reads: **NO ENTRY, NO DELIVERY.**

**PM:** We are not being defeated by policy. We are being defeated by doors.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Every department issued badges under different rules. Different colors, expiries, naming, escort requirements.

**HEAD OF COMMS:** Press has "government locked out of government" ready to publish.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We cannot spend another morning discussing shades of official blue.

Rapid assessment identifies five structural faults:

1. Badge categories inconsistent across departments
2. Expiry dates displayed in incompatible formats
3. No unified rule for temporary access upgrades
4. Manual exception handling at peak hours
5. Accountability spread across security, HR, and estates with no single owner

**PM:** Launch a practical fix. One system people can follow at speed.

## ACT TWO — THE PROTOCOL DESIGN

10:32. Joint session with security, digital identity, HR operations, and estates.

Slide title: **"IF YOU CANNOT ENTER, YOU CANNOT GOVERN"**.

**SECURITY LEAD:** Proposal: one shared badge taxonomy: permanent, fixed-term, visitor, emergency contractor.

**DIGITAL ID LEAD:** Add machine-readable validation at all central sites, same expiry format everywhere.

**HR DIRECTOR:** Tie badge status to staff records nightly so role changes flow automatically.

**LEGAL ADVISER:** Keep safeguards proportionate. Quick entry, auditable exceptions.

Protocol agreed with four mandatory controls:

- **Classification:** single badge type model across departments
- **Validation:** common scan standard and unified expiry display
- **Ownership:** named access controller per department, plus deputy
- **Exceptions:** 15-minute decision SLA with logged rationale

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Splendid. A policy that can fit on one page and still open a door.

## ACT THREE — THE MORNING STRESS TEST

13:14. Live pilot at three buildings during shift change.

**FACILITATOR:** Simulate high-volume entry with 20% mixed-status badge population.

**SITE A:** Queue times dropped after scanners switched to unified validation profile.

**SITE B:** Temporary contractor flow improved using pre-approved escort matrix.

**SITE C:** Initial delay when deputy access controller not assigned; resolved in 11 minutes.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** Predictable pattern: where ownership is clear, throughput is stable.

Mid-pilot corrective pack approved:

- Daily 06:30 sync of HR-to-badge records
- Backup controller mandatory in every rota
- Weekly red-team test using edge-case badges
- One-page staff explainer distributed before Monday

**PM:** Competence is usually just choreography with fewer surprises.

## ACT FOUR — THE ANNOUNCEMENT

17:06. Press office posts: **"COMMON ACCESS STANDARD ADOPTED ACROSS CENTRAL GOVERNMENT SITES"**.

Statement includes:

- Shared badge categories and definitions
- Unified scanner and expiry standard
- Named departmental ownership model
- Exception handling SLA and audit requirements
- Rollout timeline and first monthly performance review

By 18:01 (pilot metrics):

- Peak-time entry queues down 62%
- Manual overrides reduced by 49%
- Incorrect badge presentation incidents down 54%
- Average building access time cut from 7m 10s to 2m 35s

**HEAD OF COMMS:** We fixed a boring problem, so nothing dramatic happened. Perfect.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Stability rarely trends, but it always compounds.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:33. Media pen by the main turnstiles.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this genuinely a national priority?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If people cannot enter workplaces, priorities remain theoretical.

**REPORTER:** Critics say this is administrative theater.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Theater has scripts. This has service levels.

**REPORTER:** And if departments fail compliance?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Then they explain it at the next review meeting, from the back of the queue.
