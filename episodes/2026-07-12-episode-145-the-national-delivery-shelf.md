# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 145
**Title:** The National Delivery Shelf
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

At 07:00, the government discovered it had delivered several things but could not find where it had put them.

The Prime Minister wanted a list.

Policy had a list of intentions.

Communications had a list of announcements.

Delivery had a list of lists.

Treasury had a note asking whether any of the lists had cost money yet.

The Cabinet Secretary said this was precisely why the country needed a clearer distinction between delivering, preparing to deliver, and being photographed near delivery.

The junior adviser asked whether something counted as delivered if nobody could use it.

The room treated this as a philosophical intervention and moved on.

By 07:18, Delivery proposed the National Delivery Shelf: a secure cross-government place where completed commitments could be placed until somebody was ready to notice them.

The Prime Minister liked it.

It sounded practical, tidy, and reassuringly horizontal.

## ACT ONE

The first question was what qualified for the shelf.

Policy said only completed work.

Communications said only work that looked completed.

Legal said only work that could be described as completed without creating an expectation of functionality.

Treasury said nothing physically large.

Delivery displayed the first draft criteria:

ITEM MUST BE REAL, NAMED, AND NOT CURRENTLY ON FIRE.

The Cabinet Secretary said the tone was insufficiently aspirational.

Communications suggested "ready for public confidence."

Legal changed it to "capable of supporting confidence in appropriate circumstances."

Policy asked whether pilots could go on the shelf.

Delivery said pilots could go on the pilot shelf.

The Prime Minister asked whether there was a shelf for shelves.

Delivery said that was phase two.

Everyone agreed phase two showed momentum.

## ACT TWO

By mid-morning, departments were asked to submit evidence of delivery.

Transport sent a ribbon.

Health sent a dashboard with three green arrows and one arrow marked "narrative pending."

Education sent a photograph of a minister pointing at a wall where a poster would soon be.

The Home Office sent a secure envelope marked "delivered in principle."

Legal said "in principle" was not a location.

The Home Office said it was in their building.

Treasury asked whether the shelf required actual shelving.

Facilities said the building had cupboards.

Communications said cupboards sounded domestic and undynamic.

The Cabinet Secretary said the shelf should be metaphorical until procurement cleared physical reality.

Digital offered to create a virtual shelf.

Treasury approved because virtual shelves depreciated emotionally rather than financially.

The junior adviser asked who would take things off the shelf.

Delivery said that would require a removal protocol.

The Cabinet Secretary said removal sounded negative.

Communications suggested "public-facing release."

Legal suggested "controlled availability."

Policy suggested "activation."

Treasury asked whether activation had staffing implications.

The item was returned to the shelf.

## ACT THREE

At 12:15, the Prime Minister requested a demonstration.

Digital opened the National Delivery Shelf dashboard.

It showed twelve items.

Three were awaiting confirmation.

Four were awaiting alignment.

Two were awaiting a title that would not frighten stakeholders.

One was marked "delivered, pending delivery."

The Prime Minister asked if that was a contradiction.

Policy said it was a lifecycle.

Communications said the public did not want complexity.

The junior adviser said the public might want the thing.

Nobody wrote that down.

Delivery clicked on an item called Community Access Improvement Package.

The dashboard displayed a photo of a locked community centre with a new sign on the door reading ACCESS IMPROVEMENT PACKAGE COMING SOON.

The Prime Minister asked if the package had improved access.

Transport said the sign was more visible.

Legal said visibility was a form of access if nobody promised entry.

Communications said the photo was excellent.

Treasury asked if the sign could be reused elsewhere.

The Cabinet Secretary marked the item as transferable delivery.

## CLIMAX

The afternoon launch was arranged around the phrase "delivery you can see."

Communications placed the Prime Minister beside a freestanding shelf borrowed from a policy library.

On it sat a binder, a branded mug, a miniature road cone, and a folder labelled EVIDENCE.

The Cabinet Secretary said the shelf looked insufficiently national.

Facilities added a small flag.

Legal asked whether the flag implied ownership.

Treasury asked whether it was reusable.

The Prime Minister began: "Today, we are bringing delivery out of the shadows and onto the shelf."

A reporter asked what had actually been delivered.

The Prime Minister gestured to the shelf.

The reporter asked whether any of the items on it were services people could use.

Communications signalled for the Prime Minister to say "families."

Policy signalled for the Prime Minister to say "progress."

Legal signalled for the Prime Minister to say nothing capable of surviving transcription.

The Prime Minister said: "What people want is the confidence that delivery has somewhere to go."

At that moment, the borrowed shelf sagged.

The binder slid into the mug.

The miniature road cone fell into the evidence folder.

Digital's dashboard refreshed behind them and created a new status:

DELIVERED TO FLOOR.

For a moment, everyone looked relieved.

At least the system knew where something was.

Then the junior adviser picked up the folder and found it was empty.

Communications called it a powerful symbol of capacity.

Treasury asked to keep the folder.

## TAG

By evening, departments were instructed to place all completed commitments on the National Delivery Shelf by close of play.

Several asked whether close of play had a shelf.

Delivery opened a consultation on responsible shelf removal.

The Prime Minister announced that the government was clearing the backlog of invisible progress.

The Cabinet Secretary approved the line and asked Facilities to bolt the shelf to the floor.

Facilities said that would require a delivery plan.

The plan was placed carefully on the shelf.

**END**
