# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 086
**Title:** The National Policy Pilot Feedback Loop
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

(CABINET OFFICE INCIDENT ROOM, 07:00. A dashboard reads: **"PUBLIC TRUST INDEX — FLICKERING"**.)

Morning briefing card:

**CITIZEN SERVICE WEEK — DAY 18**  
**Scenario:** *"The National Policy Pilot Feedback Loop"*

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Why does this note say the pilot is both "a success" and "pending basic evidence"?

**PRIVATE SECRETARY:** Because the launch statement was drafted before the pilot started, and the evaluation plan was drafted after it ended.

**OPERATIONS ADVISER:** Also, the public feedback inbox has 14,000 messages titled "Did anyone read this?"

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Excellent. We have achieved post-evidence policymaking.

CUT TO TITLE.

## ACT ONE — THE CELEBRATORY VOID

08:11. Cross-government call with policy leads, delivery heads, analysts, and communications.

Slide title: **"WHEN EVERY PILOT IS A WIN, NONE OF THEM LEARN"**

**PM:** Did the programme improve anything measurable?

**DATA ANALYST:** Depends which measure. Satisfaction was up in one area, down in two, and unmeasured in four.

**CHIEF OF STAFF:** The press line says "strong early momentum."

**SERVICE DIRECTOR:** We ran three pilots with different rules, then combined the outcomes into one chart labelled "encouraging."

Four systemic problems identified:

1. Pilot objectives written as slogans, not testable outcomes
2. Data collection started after implementation
3. Public feedback captured but not triaged by theme or severity
4. Exit criteria missing, so pilots drift into permanent policy by default

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** We are grading our own homework with a highlighter.

## ACT TWO — THE REPAIR KIT

10:24. Emergency design session with policy unit, digital service, treasury liaison, and independent evaluation advisers.

Slide title: **"TEST SMALL, LEARN FAST, DECIDE OPENLY"**

**POLICY LEAD:** Every pilot must declare a baseline, target metric, and stop/go threshold before day one.

**DIGITAL LEAD:** Create a unified feedback pipeline: duplicates collapsed, urgent harms escalated, common themes tagged daily.

**TREASURY LIAISON:** No scale-up funding unless evidence pack includes cost, equity impact, and operational burden.

Three controls approved for immediate rollout:

- **Pilot Charter Rule:** one-page template with objective, metric, sample, timeline, and decision owner
- **Feedback Triage Grid:** severity levels with response SLAs (24h critical, 72h high, 7d standard)
- **Decision Ledger:** public-facing log of what changed, what did not, and why

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If we can't explain the pilot to a tired parent on a bus, it isn't ready for national rollout.

## ACT THREE — FIRST WEEK RESET

12:02. Reset launched across six departments and two local authority partnerships.

**INCIDENT COORDINATOR:** Monitoring evidence completeness, feedback response time, and policy change velocity.

12:37. Pilot Charter Rule enforced.

**PROGRAMME MANAGER:** Two pilots paused within an hour because their success criteria were decorative adjectives.

13:19. Feedback Triage Grid switched on.

**SERVICE OWNER:** Critical complaints now route to live teams, not monthly summary decks.

14:08. Decision Ledger published internally.

**DELIVERY LEAD:** Teams are arguing less about narrative and more about thresholds.

Follow-up actions agreed:

- Weekly public summary of top feedback themes and actions taken
- Independent sampling audit for pilot data quality
- Monthly "sunset review" for pilots older than 90 days
- Department scorecard on evidence readiness before announcements

**PM:** We are not anti-ambition. We are anti-announcing before counting.

## ACT FOUR — COMMS, BUT VERIFIABLE

16:45. Communications clears line: **"GOVERNMENT TIES PILOT EXPANSION TO PUBLISHED EVIDENCE"**

Public points agreed:

- Pilots exist to learn, not to pre-approve outcomes
- Feedback is only useful if categorized, acknowledged, and acted on
- Transparent criteria reduce politicized interpretation
- Clear stop/go rules protect budgets and service quality
- Publishing decisions builds trust even when results are mixed

By 18:10 (reset metrics):

- Pilots with complete baseline metrics up from 22% to 81%
- Critical feedback response within 24h up from 18% to 76%
- Ambiguous "success" claims in briefing drafts down 64%
- Policy changes linked to explicit evidence up 49%

**HEAD OF COMMS:** It's less shiny, but harder to accuse of theatre.

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** If reality is untidy, publish the tidy explanation of the untidy reality.

## CLIMAX — THE LOBBY SCRUM

18:23. Reporters crowd the briefing room.

**REPORTER:** Minister, is this a retreat from bold policy?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** It's a retreat from confident guesswork.

**REPORTER:** Are you admitting previous pilots were weak?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** I'm admitting government improves when it measures what it claims.

**REPORTER:** What's the headline for citizens?

**PETER-LIKE MINISTER:** Fewer promises in advance, more proof before expansion.
