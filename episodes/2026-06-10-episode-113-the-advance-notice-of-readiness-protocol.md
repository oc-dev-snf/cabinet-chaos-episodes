# Cabinet Chaos Episode 113: The Advance Notice of Readiness Protocol

## Cold Open
At 07:00, the Cabinet Office received an urgent email announcing that the government was ready to announce its readiness to make an announcement. The subject line included the words "not for announcement," which everyone agreed was a promising start.

The Prime Minister arrived carrying the "Agenda Certainty" folder, now reinforced with a second elastic band. Inside was a single sheet titled "Readiness Position: Draft for Pre-Readiness Clearance." Someone had highlighted every sentence except the one containing the position.

An official asked whether the room was ready. The room requested notice.

## The Briefing
The morning briefing concerned a policy that was broadly complete, nearly agreed, and currently missing only the details that would allow it to be described as either complete or agreed.

The Minister for Delivery said the public deserved a clear timetable. The Minister for Consultation said the public deserved to be asked what kind of timetable they preferred. The Chancellor asked whether the timetable had fiscal implications, at which point the timetable was quietly moved to an appendix.

An aide proposed issuing a short statement: "We are preparing." This was considered too active. Another suggested, "Preparation is under way." This was considered too traceable. The final draft read, "Readiness continues to be approached."

## The Complication
Trouble began when a broadcaster reported that the announcement had been delayed. This was inaccurate, because the announcement had not yet been officially scheduled, only repeatedly implied in rooms with windows.

The Advance Notice of Readiness Protocol was activated. Under the protocol, departments could signal momentum without confirming direction, pace, or destination. A wall chart was produced showing four stages: listening, considering, aligning, and further listening.

Nobody could remember whether "aligning" came before or after lunch.

## The Spin Room
By lunchtime, the official line was that there had been no delay, only a responsible widening of the pre-announcement runway.

Reporters asked when the policy would be announced. The spokesperson said, "At the appropriate point in the readiness cycle." Asked whether the cycle had a date, the spokesperson replied, "Dates are one way of understanding progress."

Within minutes, "readiness cycle" was being used by ministers, advisers, and one unusually confident intern who had joined the department that morning and assumed this was normal.

## Corridor Conversations
In the corridor, staffers debated whether "soon" was safer than "shortly." Legal preferred "in due course." Communications preferred "very much in due course." Policy suggested "when the work is done," which everyone admired before removing it from the page.

A backbencher offered to go on television and explain that people wanted decisions, not process. The media grid team thanked them and scheduled the interview for the week after the process had decided whether it was a decision.

Meanwhile, the paper diary minister wrote "Never announce the announcement" in the margin, then crossed out "announce" and replaced it with "indicate."

## Closing Beat
By evening, the Advance Notice of Readiness Protocol had produced a holding statement, a stakeholder note, a ministerial line, and a checklist for deciding whether the checklist was ready.

The Prime Minister declared the day a success because nothing had been announced incorrectly. The Minister for Delivery said that was not the same as delivery. The Minister for Consultation asked whether delivery had been given a fair chance to express itself.

In the final scene, an aide pinned tomorrow's agenda to the noticeboard. Item one read: "Decision on whether readiness has advanced." Item two read: "Prepare fallback wording if it has not."

**Episode Notes**
- Tone: Satirical but even-handed; the joke stays on process language, risk management, and announcement culture.
- Running theme: Momentum becomes harder to prove the more carefully it is signalled.
- Continuity hook: The "Agenda Certainty" folder gains another elastic band but no additional certainty.
