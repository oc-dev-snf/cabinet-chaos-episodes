# Cabinet Chaos — Episode 092: The Strategic Whiteboard Utilisation Plan

*Date: 2026-05-14*

## COLD OPEN
At 7:01 a.m., a memo lands across government: **WHITEBOARDS ARE NOW CRITICAL NATIONAL INFRASTRUCTURE**.

The note explains that policy clarity has been “trapped in notebooks for too long” and must now be “visibly manifested in erasable ink.”

By 7:09, three directorates are arguing over marker custody.

## ACT ONE — THE LAUNCH
In the morning briefing, the Prime Minister unveils the Strategic Whiteboard Utilisation Plan.

“We will not solve complex problems,” they say, “with invisible thoughts.”

A journalist asks whether this is just buying office supplies.

“Not at all,” replies a minister. “This is participatory surface governance.”

An adviser presents the new Whiteboard Maturity Model:

1. **Blank but Available**
2. **Partially Diagrammed**
3. **Color-Coded**
4. **Photographed and Forgotten**

The room nods solemnly.

## ACT TWO — ROLL-OUT
Implementation guidance arrives in 46 pages.

- Departments must reserve whiteboard time in 15-minute blocks.
- Each diagram needs a named “Arrow Owner.”
- Any box without a verb is classed as legacy thinking.

The transport team draws a perfect flowchart, then spends an hour locating a working eraser.

A finance unit, lacking wall space, wheels a whiteboard between meetings and calls it mobile fiscal infrastructure.

## ACT THREE — THE TRAINING SESSION
At noon, civil servants attend mandatory training: *Thinking Out Loud, Neatly*.

Trainer: “Please avoid drawing circles unless accountability is explicit.”

Participant: “Can we use clouds for uncertain policy areas?”

Trainer: “Only with director-level weather clearance.”

The chat fills with urgent questions about marker thickness, then crashes.

IT posts an update:

> “Collaboration services are degraded due to exceptionally high demand for shape-based governance.”

## ACT FOUR — PARLIAMENTARY SCRUTINY
In Parliament, opposition members demand evidence.

“Has this plan improved service delivery?”

The Cabinet Office lead answers: “Visibility has improved dramatically.”

“Delivery?”

“Now visible from multiple angles.”

Another MP asks whether frontline teams requested this.

A minister responds: “They requested clarity. We have provided four colors of it.”

## CLIMAX — THE METRICS MOMENT
At 5 p.m., officials publish first-day metrics:

- **Boards Deployed:** 12,404
- **Arrows Drawn:** 88,219
- **Action Items Completed:** 14

A senior spokesperson calls the figures “proof of energetic alignment.”

Then someone notices one department has reported zero arrows because they wrote everything in bullet points.

After a tense pause, bullet points are reclassified as “linear arrows.”

## TAG
Late evening, a junior coordinator wipes a crowded board, writes one line — **“Who is doing what by when?”** — and tags three names.

By morning, two blocked decisions are resolved.

At 8:02 a.m., a new memo appears:

**“Phase Two: National Flipchart Readiness Exercise.”**
