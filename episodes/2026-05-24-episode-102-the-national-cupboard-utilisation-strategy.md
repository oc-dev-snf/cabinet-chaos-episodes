# THE THICK OF ITCH

**Episode:** 102
**Title:** The National Cupboard Utilisation Strategy
**Genre:** Original political satire

---

## COLD OPEN

A civil servant opens a storage cupboard and is buried under 14 ring binders labelled **URGENT: Storage Optimisation**.

From somewhere inside the avalanche, a muffled voice says, “Good news — we’ve identified the problem area.”

Cut to opening titles.

## ACT ONE

The Cabinet Office crisis room is full.

The Prime Minister wants to launch a “practical, common-sense plan” to prove government can still deliver basics. A junior analyst reveals that twelve departments are paying for external archive units while their own cupboards are mostly empty, except for ceremonial lanyards and obsolete printer toner.

A special ministerial unit is formed immediately: **CUSTARD** — the Cupboard Utilisation Strategy Taskforce for Administrative Rational Delivery.

A press grid is drafted before anyone knows what cupboards are for.

## ACT TWO

CUSTARD conducts a rapid review using a national self-reporting form with 119 questions, including:
- “How emotionally prepared is your team for shelf reallocation?”
- “Estimate average cupboard morale (1–10).”
- “Is your mop bucket strategically aligned?”

Within hours, dashboards appear.

Green means “cupboard acknowledged.”
Amber means “cupboard contested.”
Red means “cupboard currently in use by another initiative.”

A senior adviser insists this is a transformational moment. Another quietly points out that half the departments submitted photos of kitchens.

## ACT THREE

To show momentum, ministers announce pilot sites.

Pilot One fails when three departments claim the same cupboard by historic precedent.
Pilot Two fails when Facilities locks the cupboard “pending fire regulation clarification.”
Pilot Three succeeds briefly until someone discovers it contains only a deflated policy banner and twenty-seven unused visitor badges.

A late-night meeting turns tense. Communications wants a hopeful narrative. Policy wants definitions. Operations wants keys.

They agree on a compromise statement: “Progress is being made at pace, with lessons learned in real time.”

## CLIMAX

At the launch podium, the minister unveils the **National Cupboard Utilisation Strategy** with four pillars:
1. Visibility
2. Accountability
3. Practical Shelf Readiness
4. Whole-of-Government Hinges

A reporter asks how much money it saves.

The minister replies, “It’s not just about savings. It’s about confidence.”

Another asks how many cupboards have actually been reorganised.

After a long pause, the analyst whispers, “One. But very thoroughly.”

## TAG

Back in the office, the team celebrates “delivery” with supermarket cake.

Facilities sends an email:

**Subject:** Cupboard 3B Reclassified as “Temporary Corridor Art Storage”

CUSTARD is quietly merged into a new programme: **DRAWER** — the Departmental Review of Administrative Workspace Efficiency and Rationalisation.

No one laughs. Then everyone does.

**END**
